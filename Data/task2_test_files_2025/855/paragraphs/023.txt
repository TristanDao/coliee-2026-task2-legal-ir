I would further observe that the Minister's authority under paragraph 225.2(1) is exercisable when "... it may reasonably be considered that collection of an amount assessed ... would be jeopardized by a delay ...". The expression has sufficiently liberal qualifications to it that its ambit appears to me of far greater scope than that found in Mareva injunctions. The word "may" and the expression "reasonably considered", when read together, provide considerable latitude to the Minister, a latitude which I believe is not found whenever one deals with a seizure before judgment.
