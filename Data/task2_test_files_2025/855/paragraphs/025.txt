I should also observe that paragraph 225.2(1) refers to the "collection of an amount assessed [which] would be jeopardized
by a delay in the collection thereof
..." (my underlining), a wording which establishes the presumption that the amount is a "collectible" amount, collectible forthwith, were it not for the delays which s. 225.1 contemplates. I conclude that, although indicative of the tests which might be applied by a court in dealing with a Minister's direction, the tests found in Mareva proceedings are far from being determinative or conclusive.
