It is by this broader approach to determining the legislative intent, that the authorities relied upon by counsel for the Commission suggest that retrospective application of the legislation may be implied. Thus, the Supreme Court of Canada in Healey v. Attorney General of Quebec, [1987] 1 S.C.R. 158; 73 N.R. 288, at pp. 177-178 S.C.R., and earlier in Acme Village School District v. Steel-Smith, [1933] S.C.R. 47, found an implied intention that legislation be applied with retrospective effect, drawing upon the circumstances in which the legislation was passed, its general purposes and the procedures adopted by the statutes in question.
