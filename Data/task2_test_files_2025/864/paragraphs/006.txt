The Commission becomes involved with a complaint in a number of ways, including:
(a) by a member of the public making a complaint directly to the Commission, pursuant to s. 45.35(1),
(b) by the Commission Chairman initiating a complaint pursuant to s. 45.37(1); or
(c) pursuant to s. 45.41(1), upon written referral of a complaint to the Commission by a complainant who is not satisfied
(i) with the action taken on the complaint by the R.C.M.P., or
(ii) with a direction made by the Commissioner of the R.C.M.P. pursuant to subs. 45.36(5) declining to investigate the complaint.
