The Commission submits that the phrase "a complainant under s. 45.35(1) of the Act", as it appears in s. 45.41(1) of the Act, should include any person who has made a complaint to any of the persons referred to in subss. 45.35(1)(a), (b), or (c) of the Act, i.e., who has taken any of the steps there described to initiate a complaint, whether or not the complaint was made before the coming into force of Part VII of the Act. The Commission argues, referring to s. 12 of the Interpretation Act, supra, that:
"This interpretation is warranted ... if it is the opinion of this Honourable Court that Part VII of the Act operates retrospectively so as to apply to complaints arising from conduct alleged to have occurred prior to the coming into force of Part VII, because the language of s. 45.41(1) contemplates its immediate effect upon proclamation. Further, such an interpretation would be most likely to facilitate the achievement of the purposes of Part VI and VII."
(Memorandum of Fact and Law on behalf of the Commission, p. 17)
