In this case, it is clear that the provisions introduced in Part VII affect procedural rights. Beyond those, however, other rights of the parties are also affected. The former R.C.M.P. complaints procedure contained no provision for an independent public review, such as that now set out in Part VII to be carried out by the Commission, a body external to the R.C.M.P. itself. As counsel for the Attorney General has pointed out, the review process by the Commission, as set out in Part VII, is an external process involving a newly created body which had no role or function in connection with the former R.C.M.P. complaints procedure. This is a change which has an effect on the content or existence of a right. It creates a new right to external, public review of R.C.M.P. conduct, and for most complaints it will extend the time taken for consideration of complaints, it may involve hearings, generally in public, and until matters are finally disposed of the uncertainties attendant on the process will be extended.
