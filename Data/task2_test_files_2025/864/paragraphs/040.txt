Madame Justice L'Heureux-Dubé concluded, in Brosseau, that since the provisions there in question were designed to disqualify from trading in securities those persons whom the Commission found to have committed acts which called into question their business integrity, a measure designed to protect the public, rather than one designed for punishment, the presumption against the retrospective effect of statutes was rebutted. Counsel for the Commission would have me conclude, similarly, that since the provisions in Part VII are designed to protect the public, the presumption does not apply.
