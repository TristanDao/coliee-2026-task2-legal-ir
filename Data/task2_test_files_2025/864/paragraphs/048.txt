I am prepared to conclude that Parts VI and VII in the amendments to the Act were enacted with the primary objective of protecting the public and the R.C.M.P. itself from the risk of an apprehension of or actual bias in dealing with complaints about police conduct. Prior to these amendments, the R.C.M.P. itself was functioning as the sole arbiter of complaints made against it. The opportunity for independent and open review by the Commission of R.C.M.P. disposal of complaints can only enhance confidence of the general public in the force and in its activities.
