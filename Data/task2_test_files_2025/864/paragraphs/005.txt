A member of the public may initiate a complaint by presenting it to the Commission, to any person appointed or employed under the R.C.M.P. Act, or to a provincial official responsible for receipt of complaints by the public against police (s. 45.35(1)). In addition, the Commission Chairman may initiate a complaint (s. 45.37(1)). However, initiated, all complaints are to be reported to the Commissioner of the R.C.M.P. who is responsible for dealing with each complaint, by way of informal disposition, by declining to investigate or by investigating it, and for informing the complainant and the person complained about of the disposition of the matter (ss. 45.35(3)(4); 45.36; 45.37(2)-(4); 45.38 to 45.45).
