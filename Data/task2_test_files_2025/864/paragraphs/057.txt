Finally, the Commission argues, in relation to the effect of postponement arising from the time between enactment of Part VII and its coming into force upon proclamation, that even if Part VII were not to apply to conduct antedating enactment, delay in Part VII coming into force ought not to preclude the Commission dealing with complaints about conduct occurring after the date of Royal Assent. For the Attorney General it is agreed that if Part VII is to have retrospective effect that should be to March 26, 1986, the date of Royal Assent, but no earlier, for conduct giving rise to a complaint.
