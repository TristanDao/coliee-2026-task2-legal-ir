Resolution of these issues depends upon interpretation of the legislation in question, in particular Part VII of the Act. There is no dispute that interpretation depends upon the purposes or objectives of the legislation, in accord with s. 12 of the Interpretation Act, R.S.C. 1985, c. I-21, which provides:
"Every enactment is deemed remedial, and shall be given such fair, large and liberal construction and interpretation as best ensures the attainment of its objects."
It is in the application of this general principle to the situations brought to the attention of the Commission, by referral or initiation of complaints from members of the public, that differences have arisen.
