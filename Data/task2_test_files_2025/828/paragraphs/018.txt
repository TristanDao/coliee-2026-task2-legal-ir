Based on the above, I am of the view that the Prothonotary was correct when he struck paragraphs 24, 25, 26 and 27 of the statement of defence. As stated by Justice Richard in
Eli Lily and Co. et al.
above at paragraph 10: "The Court will only strike pleadings in plain and obvious cases where the case is beyond doubt . . .". I am of the opinion this is such a case; the extrinsic evidence on which the paragraphs rely is not admissible under Canadian patent law. There would be useful purpose to keep these paragraphs in the statement of defence.
