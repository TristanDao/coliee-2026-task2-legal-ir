It is interesting to note that the Ontario rule incorporates a test of "serious prejudice" as contrasted with the "compelling reasons" test referred to above. I conclude that the "serious prejudice" test is narrower than a "compelling reasons" test but included within the latter unless the latter is strictly limited to reasons directed to the minimization of the expense of the action and I do not read the foregoing cases as limiting "compelling reasons" exclusively to this objective.
