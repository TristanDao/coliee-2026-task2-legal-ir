Until 1953 the United States had made reports to the United Nations under paragraph 73(e) of the U.N.
Charter
which requires members
"which have or assume responsibilities for the administration of territories whose peoples have not yet attained a full measure of self-government ..."
to transmit regularly to the Secretary General information relating to the economic, social, and educational conditions in those territories. In January 1953, the United States notified the U.N. to the effect that it would no longer be appropriate for it to submit such information on Puerto Rico as the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico had achieved a full measure of local self-government. The U.S. representative stated that the authority of the Commonwealth is not more limited than that of any state and in fact in certain aspects goes beyond that of the states.
