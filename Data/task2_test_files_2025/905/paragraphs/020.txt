The two most pertinent Canadian cases involving the interpretation of "goods exported" or "goods ... for export", expressions used there to describe goods exempted from certain sales taxes, both expressed the view that "export" normally involves the transfer of goods from one country to another [see footnote 6]. The first of these cases cited in support a decision of the U.S. Supreme Court to the same effect [see footnote 7]. None of these cases involved the movement of goods to another territory subject to the same sovereign authority in an international sense.
