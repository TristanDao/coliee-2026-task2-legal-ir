On October 4, 1993, the Honourable Horace Krever, a judge of the Ontario Court of Appeal, was appointed under Part I of the
Inquiries Act
to be a Commissioner to review and report on the mandate, organization, management, operations, financing and regulation of all activities of the blood system in Canada, including the events surrounding the contamination of the blood system in Canada in the early 1980s, by examining, without limiting the generality of the inquiry,
- the organization and effectiveness of past and current systems designed to supply blood and blood products in Canada;
- the roles, views, and ideas of relevant interest groups; and
- the structures and experiences of other countries, especially those with comparable federal systems.
