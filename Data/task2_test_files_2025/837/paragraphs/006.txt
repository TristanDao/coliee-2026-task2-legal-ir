This issue was recently raised in
Qiu v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2000] 183 F.T.R. 14 (T.D.). In that case, counsel called my attention to a long line of jurisprudence that has held that the notes of a visa officer, in the absence of an affidavit from that officer attesting to the veracity of what is said therein, are not admissible as evidence.
