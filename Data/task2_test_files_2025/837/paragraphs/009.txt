There are many decisions that follow the
Wang
decision. These are listed in
Yan v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1999), 169 F.T.R. 139 (T.D.). See,
Fung v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
(1991), 121 N.R. 263 (F.C.A.);
Gaffney v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
(1991), 121 N.R. 256; 12 Imm. L.R.(2d) 185 (F.C.A.);
Qiu v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [1997] F.T.R. Uned. 235 (T.D.);
Ayubi v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1997), 38 Imm. L.R.(2d) 276 (T.D.); and
Patel v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1998), 155 F.T.R. 228 (T.D.). In those decisions, the visa officer did not file an affidavit attesting to the veracity of the notes or the conduct of the interview. Also, the decision in
Wang
was recently cited with approval by the Federal Court of Appeal in
Moldevenau v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1999), 235 N.R. 192; 1 Imm. L.R.(3d) 105 (F.C.A.).
