The question as posed is clearly relevant. The plaintiff, on this line of questioning, will be able to determine whether or not it should call the customers of the defendant as witnesses in order to establish whether or not a purchase of noninfringing tickets was connected to the purchase of infringing tickets. Since all profits directly or indirectly related to an infringement are recoverable, this question is relevant (
Beloit Canada Ltd. v. Valmet Oy
(1992), 144 N.R. 389; 45 C.P.R.(3d) 116, at p. 119 (F.C.A.), and
Ductmate Industries Inc. v. Exanno Products Ltd.
(1987), 12 F.T.R. 37; 16 C.P.R.(3d) 15, at p. 20 (T.D.)) The prothonotary was correct in finding that questions which asked "whether other sales were made because of the sale of infringing tickets" were irrelevant. The answer to those questions is properly in the minds of the defendant's customers and not in the mind of the defendant. However, question 1310 as posed is quite factual, relevant and will be answered. The appeal, in this regard, is allowed.
