It is well-established law that the discretion to defer a removal is very limited. It would be contrary to the purposes and objects to the
Act
to expand, by judicial declaration, a removal officer's limited discretion so as to mandate a "mini H & C" review prior to removal (
Davis et al. v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2000] F.T.R. Uned. 444; [2000] F.C.J. No. 1628 (T.D.), at para. 4;
John v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2003] F.C.J. No. 583 (T.D.)). It is simply not sufficient to put the blame on applicant's former counsel.
