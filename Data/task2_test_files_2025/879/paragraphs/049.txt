The conditions precedent for the issuance of declaratory relief were set out by the Court of Appeal in
Montana Indian Band et al. v. Canada
(1991), 120 N.R. 200 (F.C.A.). The Court of Appeal applied the test articulated in
Russian Commercial and Industrial Bank v. British Bank for Foreign Trade Ltd.
, [1921] 2 A.C. 438 (P.C.): "the question must be a real and not theoretical question, the person raising it must have a real interest to raise it, he must be able to secure a proper contradictor, that is to say someone presently existing who has a true interest to oppose the declaration sought." This test was adopted by the Supreme Court of Canada in
Solosky v. Canada
, [1980] 1 S.C.R. 821; 30 N.R. 380.
