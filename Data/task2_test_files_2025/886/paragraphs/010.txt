Following
Pushpanathan v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [see footnote 2] and
Baker
, the appropriate standard of review is to be determined by examining four factors: (a) the existence or absence of a privative clause; (b) the expertise of the board; (c) the purpose of the act as a whole, and the provision in particular; and (d) the "nature of the problem": a question of law or fact.
