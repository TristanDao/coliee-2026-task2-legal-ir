In
Khosa v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2009] 1 S.C.R. 339; 385 N.R. 206; [2009] S.C.J. No. 12; 2009 SCC 12, the Supreme Court of Canada found that considerable deference is owed to a tribunal's findings and that it is not the role of the Court to re-weigh the evidence where Parliament has granted the authority to make a decision on a salient issue to the tribunal.
