In the final resort, it falls upon the courts, not the Minister or his officers, to construe the
Act
. The officer's focus on matters which would not have been relevant had Mr. Sahakyan left in time, shows that he misconstrued the
Act
. This is not to say that Mr. Sahakyan's Canadian history is not relevant. What it does mean is that that history must be relevant to his late departure. The centrepiece of the officer's concern had to be the reasons why Mr. Sahakyan left in June, rather than in March.
