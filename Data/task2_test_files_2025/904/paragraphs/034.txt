The officer's interpretation of the
Act
is a question of law. The correctness standard applies. The exercise of his discretion is subject to the reasonableness simpliciter standard of review. I see no reason why a different standard should apply under s. 52 of the
Act
(
Ha v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2004] 3 F.C.R. 195 (permanent resident);
Yaghoubian v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2003] F.C.J. No. 806; 234 F.T.R. 76 (T.D.) (permanent resident);
Wang v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2001] F.C.J. No. 1940 (student visa)). The officer misinterpreted the law and exercised his discretion unreasonably.
