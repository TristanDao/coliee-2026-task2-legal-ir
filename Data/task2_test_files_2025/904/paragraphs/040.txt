"In the language of Lord Reid in
Padfield v. Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food
, [1968] A.C. 997 (H.L.), at p. 1030:
'... if the Minister, by reason of his having misconstrued the
Act
or for any other reason, so uses his discretion
as to thwart or run counter to the policy and objects of the
Act
, then our law would be very defective if persons aggrieved were not entitled to the protection of the court.' [Emphasis added.]
Lord Reid added that 'the policy and objects of the
Act
must be determined by construing the
Act
as a whole and construction is always a matter of law for the court' (p. 1030)."
