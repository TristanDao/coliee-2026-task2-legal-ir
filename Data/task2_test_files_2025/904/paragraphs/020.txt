Part 13 of the
Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations
, SOR/2002-227, commencing at s. 223, deals with removal orders. There are three types: departure orders, exclusion orders and deportation orders. A putative refugee, whose claim was not accepted, is subject to a departure order. Under Rule 224, a foreign national subject to an enforced departure order does not need to obtain authorization under s. 52(1) of the
Act
in order to return to Canada. Thus, despite the officer's concerns about Mr. Sahakyan's motives, he could not have prevented Mr. Sahakyan return had he left Canada in March rather then in June. Since Mr. Sahakyan did not leave Canada within 30 days after the departure order became enforceable, it became a deportation order and under s. 226 of the
Regulations
he cannot return except with written authorization.
