While the presumption of state protection may be rebutted, this will only be the case where a refugee claimant proffers "clear and convincing" evidence confirming the state's inability to provide protection. As Mr. Justice La Forest stated at pages 724-725 of
Ward
, such evidence can include the "testimony of similarly situated individuals let down by the state or the claimant's own testimony of past personal incidents in which state protection did not materialize."
