The applicants proposed two questions for certification about the state of the jurisprudence with respect to state protection - whether state protection must be "effective or adequate" and how that relates to the jurisprudence that it does not have to be "perfect". The respondent opposes the certification of these questions because the issue has already been decided by the Federal Court of Appeal. I find that these questions are not determinative of the case before me, and accordingly ought not to be certified. I will not comment on whether these issues have already been addressed by the Federal Court of Appeal since such a finding is not necessary in this case.
