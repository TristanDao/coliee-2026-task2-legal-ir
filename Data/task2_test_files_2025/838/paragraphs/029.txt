As I stated above, none of these issues were canvassed or considered by the Board in its decision. Accordingly, to the extent that these concerns formed part of the PIF narratives of the principal applicant and his wife, the Board was required to consider the effect that such treatment had on the children. In failing to do so, the Board's decision was unreasonable and cannot stand up to a somewhat probing examination.
