The Board's decision does not address how these problems impact upon the effectiveness of the protection allegedly available to the applicants in Guyana. Given that this evidence contradicts the Board's ultimate finding, such a consideration was warranted in the Board's analysis: see
Cepeda-Gutierrez v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1998), 157 F.T.R. 35 (T.D.) per Evans J. (as he then was). On this basis, the Court concludes that the Board ought to have addressed this evidence before its conclusion. This is a reviewable error and, accordingly, the Board's decision must be set aside for these reasons.
