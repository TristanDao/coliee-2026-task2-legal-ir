The applicants state that the most serious incident of persecution occurred in May 2006 when the principal applicant was accosted at his auto repair garage by five Afro-Guyanese individuals who demanded all his money and jewellery. The principal applicant states that the five men forced themselves into his home, which is attached to the auto repair garage, and that the men beat him, fondled his wife, and hit his children. After the principal applicant gave the men jewellery and between 200,000 and 300,000 Guyana dollars, the thieves bound the applicants' hands and feet and threatened the family before leaving and allegedly firing several shots into the house.
