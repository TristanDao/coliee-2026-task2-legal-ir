While it is generally desirable to leave issues of relevance and admissibility to the judge hearing the application, here the CRA complains that it does not know the case it has to meet and cannot properly frame its own response. Because of the large volume of material filed, there does appears to be some merit to this submission and I will direct McKellar to refile its affidavit materials strictly limited to evidence that is relevant to the issues arising in the proceeding. I do not intend to interfere with the ultimate authority of the hearing judge to resolve issues of admissibility of hearsay evidence. However, McKellar runs the risk of losing such evidence if it uses it inappropriately or in substitution for evidence that is within the personal knowledge of its deponents. I will also order the removal of all legal arguments from McKellar's affidavits. McKellar shall refile its revised affidavits within 14 days of this Order, and the CRA shall then have 30 days to file its own affidavits in response. To the extent that McKellar files an amended affidavit in compliance with this direction, the corresponding affidavit shall then be removed from the Court file.
