Two of my colleagues have adopted the test of unreasonableness simpliciter as appli­cable to the review of a visa officer's deci­sion:
Zheng v. Canada (Minister of Citi­zenship and Immigration)
, [2000] F.T.R. Uned. 28 (T.D.) and
Lu v. Canada (Minis­ter of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1999), 176 F.T.R. 263 (T.D.). In
Lu
, Mr. Justice Muldoon, in weighing the factors, noted that questions of mixed fact and law and the absence of a privative clause weigh against the Court showing deference to the visa officer's decision; however, the exper­tise of the visa officer and the fact that the issues raised were not polycentric favoured deference by the Court.
