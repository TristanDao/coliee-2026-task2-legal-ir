To accept this argument, in my view, would be to apply too formalistic an approach to the conduct of an interview. The visa officer's concerns are reflected in the questions she asked the applicant during the interview. This is not a case where a visa officer relied on a concern that was com­pletely divorced from the subjects covered at the interview, or on evidence not disclosed to the applicant. Nor is it a situation in which the applicant was left with the impres­sion that the visa officer had reached a certain conclusion on a matter, only to find out later that the conclusion was exactly the opposite.
