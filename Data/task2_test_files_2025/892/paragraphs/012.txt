Despite the fact that this application was adjourned to allow the applicants to file further and better evidence, I conclude that they have failed to make out a case that would justify the orders requested. There is no evidence before me that would indicate that the proposed substitute affiant is as well versed in the matters that are the subject of his affidavits and of those of Mr. Henein as is Mr. Henein himself. There is no evidence before me that Mr. Henein has concerns with respect to being cross-examined on his affidavits. There is no evidence before me that his new employer would be concerned if he were to be interviewed by counsel for the applicants. There is no evidence that anyone other than the applicants' solicitors would be offended if contact were to be made between the solicitors and Mr. Henein in order to prepare Mr. Henein for cross-examination. There is no evidence, other than inferences drawn from the title of Mr. Henein's new position, that his new responsibilities are such as to cause embarrassment or prejudice to anyone if he were cross-examined on his affidavits. Finally, there is insufficient evidence to satisfy me that all other alternatives to withdrawal of Mr. Henein's affidavits have been fully explored and are unworkable.
