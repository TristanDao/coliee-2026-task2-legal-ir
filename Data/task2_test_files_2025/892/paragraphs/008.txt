While I am satisfied that the principles reflected in the foregoing quotations should, in general terms, apply in this court, I am not satisfied that they should govern in these matters. These are clearly not matters where affidavits are proposed to be withdrawn solely for the purpose of preventing cross-examination. The fact that substantially identical affidavits are proposed to be filed in their place is substantial response to the concern regarding the integrity of the justice system. In
Bhatnager v. Minister of Employment and Immigration et al.
, [1986] 2 F.C. 3; 2 F.T.R. 18 (T.D.), Strayer, J., as he then was, stated at p. 12 F.C.:
"While I am aware that in some other courts it has been held that an affidavit once filed on a motion cannot be withdrawn, I could find no reason in principle why this should be so. It appears to me that the closer analogy is that of a respondent deciding not to call a witness where viva voce evidence is being taken."
