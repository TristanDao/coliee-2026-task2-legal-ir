The rule being relied on in this regard was formulated by the Federal Court of Appeal in
Szczecka v. Ministre de l'Emploi et de l'Immigration
(1993), 170 N.R. 58 (F.C.A.). In that case, the court, per Létourneau, J.A., held that unless there are special circumstances, there should not be any immediate judicial review of an interlocutory decision. The decisions in respect of which judicial review is available are those that make a final ruling on the merits of a case.
