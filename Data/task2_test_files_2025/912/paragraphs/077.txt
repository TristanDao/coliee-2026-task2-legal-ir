In
R. v. Borden (J.R.)
, [1994] 3 S.C.R. 145; 171 N.R. 1; 134 N.S.R.(2d) 321; 303 A.P.R. 321, the Supreme Court made a definitive ruling on the question of what conditions must be met for a waiver of the s. 8 guarantee to be valid. It confirmed the approach put forward by the Saskatchewan Court of Appeal in
R. v. Nielsen
(1988), 66 Sask.R. 293; 43 C.C.C.(3d) 548 (C.A.), namely that the waiver of the s. 8 right must, at a minimum, be subject to the conditions applicable to the waiver of a statutory procedural right like that in issue in
Korponay
.
