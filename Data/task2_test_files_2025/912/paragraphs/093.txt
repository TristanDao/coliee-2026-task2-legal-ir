The
Johnson
test must, however, be applied only in cases in which the significance and severity of the intrusion so require. The reasonable grounds requirement will not be applicable when the case involves monitoring-type measures such as those discussed by Arbour, J.A., namely checks and inspections that a person engaged in a regulated activity must reasonably expect. Likewise, the test will not be applied when doing so would be unjustified given the low degree of "intrusion".
