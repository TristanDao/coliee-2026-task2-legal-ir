In defining the terms "search" and "seizure" in s. 8, reference must be made to the Supreme Court of Canada's decision in
R. v. Dyment
, [1988] 2 S.C.R. 417; 89 N.R. 249; 73 Nfld. & P.E.I.R. 13; 229 A.P.R. 13. In that case, the court held that the essence of a seizure under s. 8 is the taking of a thing from a person by a public authority without that person's consent.
