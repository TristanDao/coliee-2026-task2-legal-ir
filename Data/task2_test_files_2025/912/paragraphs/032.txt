I agree with the applicants that the statutory discretion exercised by the Superintendent is not absolute. The courts have held on numerous occasions that every discretion is subject to certain general legal limitations. (In this regard, see
Davidson v. Slaight Communications Inc.
, [1989] 1 S.C.R. 1038; 93 N.R. 183) For example, it is established that a discretion cannot be used for improper purposes that are contrary to the applicable legislation. Furthermore, a discretion cannot be exercised in bad faith or in an arbitrary, unfair or unreasonable manner.
