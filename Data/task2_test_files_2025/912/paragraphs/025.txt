In my view, the Deputy Superintendent's decision was not an interlocutory decision within the meaning that the courts have given that term. It was not a decision made in the course of an action by an administrative tribunal. It was a decision that had a final effect on the applicants' rights, because the seized records were administered by a representative of the Superintendent and could not, in all likelihood, be returned to them until the end of the disciplinary process. Moreover, in most instances, the administration of the seized records will probably be complete by the time the disciplinary process ends. For these reasons, it seems to me that the
Szczecka
rule is not applicable to this case.
