Section 11(b) of the
Charter
states that "any person charged with an offence has the right ... to be tried within a reasonable time" and applies to purely criminal and penal proceedings. (
R. v. CIP Inc.
, [1992] 1 S.C.R. 843; 135 N.R. 90; 52 O.A.C. 366; 12 C.R.(4th) 237; 71 C.C.C.(3d) 129;
R. v. Wigglesworth
, [1987] 2 S.C.R. 541; 81 N.R. 161; 24 O.A.C. 321; 61 Sask.R. 105; 60 C.R.(3d) 193; [1988] 1 W.W.R. 193; 28 Admin. L.R. 294; 45 D.L.R.(4th) 235; 32 C.R.R. 219; 37 C.C.C.(3d) 385.)
