Section 7 of the
Charter
guarantees everyone the "right to life, liberty and security of the person". These three rights imply protection of individuals in a physical sense and do not extend to economic and property rights, except perhaps for fundamental economic rights to life and survival. It is quite clear that there is no question of these fundamental rights being infringed in the case at bar. (See
Rodriguez v. British Columbia (Attorney General) et al.
, [1993] 3 S.C.R. 519; 158 N.R. 1; 34 B.C.A.C. 1; 56 W.A.C. 1, and
Irwin Toy Ltd. v. Québec (Procureur général)
, [1989] 1 S.C.R. 927; 94 N.R. 167; 24 Q.A.C. 2.)
