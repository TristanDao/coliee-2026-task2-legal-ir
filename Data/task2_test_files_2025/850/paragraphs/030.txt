In
Boateng v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
(1993), 64 F.T.R. 197 (T.D.), Mr. Justice Noël overturned a decision of the Convention Refugee Determination Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board because the Board had referred to changes in the applicant's country of origin but appeared not to have made any evaluation of them (p. 3):
"In my view, when one says that 'change' in circumstances is an important consideration, one is not speaking of any change. The Board must not be content in simply noting that changes have taken place, but must assess the impact of these changes on the person of the applicant."
The Federal Court of Appeal decision in
Mileva
was relied upon.
