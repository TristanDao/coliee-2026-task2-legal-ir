Section 2(1) of the
Immigration Act
, R.S.C. 1985, c. I-2, states that for the purposes of the
Act
a "Convention refugee":
"... does not include any person to whom the Convention does not apply pursuant to s. E or F of art. I thereof, which sections are set out in the schedule to this Act,"
The relevant part of art. I(F) of the
Convention
, set out in the schedule to the
Act
states:
"F. The provisions of this convention shall not apply to any person with respect to whom there are serious reasons for considering that:
(a) he has committed a crime against peace, a war crime, or a crime against humanity, as defined in the international instruments drawn up to make provision in respect of such crimes;"
