The issue of statutory interpretation was clearly outlined by the Supreme Court of Canada, in the case of
R. v. McIntosh
. The Supreme Court affirmed the "golden rule" of interpreting statutes in a manner consistent with their plain meaning.
"A statute should be interpreted in a manner consistent with the plain meaning of its terms. Where the language of the statute is plain and admits of only one meaning, the task of interpretation does not arise. [see footnote 11]"
