I turn then to s. 3(e). The export of logs was placed on the Export Control List "to ensure that there is an adequate supply and distribution ... [of logs] in Canada for defence
or other needs
" (emphasis added). The respondent's argument is that "other needs" allows control of export in order to accord with provincial policies of restricting export to ensure a supply within the province, or to limit export for environmental purposes, or for reasons of international trade. Reference is made to the decision in
Teal Cedar Products (1977) Ltd. v. Canada
, [1989] 2 F.C. 158; 92 N.R. 308 (F.C.A.).
