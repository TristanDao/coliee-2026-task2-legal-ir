The Respondent relies on the definition of "trade secrets" as discussed in
Société Gamma Inc. v. Canada (Secretary of State)
(1994), 79 F.T.R. 42 (T.D.). In that decision, the Court concluded that a trade secret must be something probably of a technical nature which is guarded very closely and is of such peculiar value to its owner that harm to the owner is presumed by its mere disclosure (at 45).
