However, there is another line of authority which takes a different view. In
Canadian Football League v. Canada (Minister for Fitness and Amateur Sports) and Shoalts
, [1989] 2 F.C. 480; 24 F.T.R. 62 (T.D.), Strayer, J, as he then was, held that the voluntarily submitted material marked "confidential" remained subject to the right of access in the
Act
. He said as follows at 487:
"... it is not enough to state that their submission is confidential in order to make it so in an objective sense. Such a principle would surely undermine much of the purpose of this
Act
which in part is to make available to the public the information upon which government action is taken or refused. Nor would it be consistent with that purpose if a Minister or his officials were able to exempt information from disclosure simply by agreeing when it is submitted that it would be treated as confidential"
