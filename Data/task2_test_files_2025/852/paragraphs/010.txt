The law is clear that in a judicial review in these circumstances I must defer to the Board as to its findings of fact and not interfere unless I find them to be patently unreasonable. (see e.g. Khella v. Canada (MCI), 2006 FC 1357 at para 12). However where there has been an error of law the standard is that of correctness (see e.g. Mugasera v.
