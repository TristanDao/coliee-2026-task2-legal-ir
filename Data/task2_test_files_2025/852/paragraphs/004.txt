This provision was recently considered by Justice Mosley of this Court in Donkor v. Canada (MCI) 2006 FC 1089 as there were changes to the provision since it was last substantively considered in Horbas v. Canada (MCI), [1985] 2 FC 369. Justice Mosley in paragraphs 18 and 19 of his Reasons determined that this new section 4 requires a two pronged test wherein:
1. The genuineness of relationship must be considered in the present tense such that a relationship that may not have been “genuine” at the outset may have become genuine; and
2. Consideration must be given as to whether the relationship entered into primarily for the purpose of acquiring any status or privilege under the Act.
