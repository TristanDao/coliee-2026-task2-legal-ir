Mr. Cartagena claims that he then spent until May 2003 working on farms around the small towns of El Rancho and El Chilamate. He asserts that, during this time, he was hiding and waiting for a friend of his mother, with whom he now lives in Toronto, to raise funds to help him leave El Salvador with the help of a "coyote", or smuggler. In May 2003, he left his country, travelling overland through Guatemala, Mexico and the United States before claiming refugee protection at Fort Erie in August, 2003.
