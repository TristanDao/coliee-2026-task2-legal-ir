In
Ramadani v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2005] F.T.R. Uned. 135; 137 A.C.W.S.(3d) 383 (F.C.), my colleague Madam Justice Carolyn Layden-Stevenson articulated the Board's duty, before denying an application to adjourn, to turn its mind to the relevant factors set out by the Federal Court of Appeal in
Siloch v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
(1993), 151 N.R. 76 (F.C.A.). At paragraph 11 of
Ramadani
, Layden-Stevenson, J., stated:
"¶ 11 In my view, the RPD must, at a minimum, indicate that it has had regard to the relevant factors enumerated in
Siloch
, supra, before arriving at a negative decision. Its failure to do so constitutes a reviewable error. I note that my colleagues Madam Justice Heneghan and Mr. Justice O'Keefe arrived at a similar conclusion in
Dias v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2003] F.C.J. No. 125, 2003 FC 84 and
Sandy v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2004] F.C.J. No. 1770, 2004 FC 1468."
