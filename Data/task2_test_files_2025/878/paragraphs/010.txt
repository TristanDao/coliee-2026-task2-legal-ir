It further submits that the third-party proceedings by Canada against the Province of Manitoba would involve claims for indemnity and possibly a claim for contribution under provincial joint tort feasor legislation. Accordingly, the crown's case against Manitoba is based on the common law and provincial legislation providing for joint liability in the case of joint tort feasors. Furthermore, it is argued that the question of damages to the hunting, fishing and traditional pursuits of the plaintiffs is a matter of property and civil rights which is clearly provincial in nature and accordingly, not within this court's jurisdiction.
