The Province of Manitoba has enacted the
Federal Courts Jurisdiction Act
, R.S.M. 1987, c. C-270; C.C.S.M., c. C-270, s. 1 of which provides as follows:
"1. The Supreme Court of Canada and the Exchequer Court of Canada, or the Supreme Court of Canada alone, according to the provisions of the Acts of the Parliament of Canada known as the Supreme Court Act and the Federal Court Act have or has jurisdiction in cases of
(a) controversies between Canada and the Province of Manitoba;
(b) controversies between any other province of Canada, that may have passed an Act similar to this Act, and the Province of Manitoba;"
