The very essence of the dispute here involves lands reserved for Indians within the meaning of the
Indian Act
and s. 91(24) of the
Constitution Act, 1867
. The fact that provincial law may also be involved in the dispute does not preclude the jurisdiction of this court. In
Montana Band v. Canada
, [1992] 2 C.N.L.R. 134, the Federal Court of Appeal stated at p. 135:
"It cannot be presumed that the cause of action advanced by the Crown lies in provincial tort law and engages pure common law concepts. As mentioned above, the very special body of law governing the relationship between aboriginal people, the Indian Bands and the federal authorities is directly implicated."
