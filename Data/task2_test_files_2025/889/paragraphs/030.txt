On the basis of this distinction, one who is the object of violence at the hands of street gangs may fail in proving lack of state protection whereas another claimant from the same country might succeed in arguing lack of state protection from terrorists. To some extent, this is consistent with the distinction I have drawn with respect to statewide absence of protection and local lack of protection which is amenable to administrative correction. Terrorism is generally directed to destabilizing the state apparatus in all or a significant part of its territory. An inability to combat it effectively is a state failure, not a local failure.
