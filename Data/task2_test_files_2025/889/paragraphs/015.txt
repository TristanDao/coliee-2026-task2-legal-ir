The issue of state protection was considered by the Supreme Court of Canada in
Canada (Attorney General) v. Ward
, [1993] 2 S.C.R. 689; 153 N.R. 321; 103 D.L.R.(4th) 1, in which Ward claimed refugee status in Canada on the ground of the inability of the government of Northern Ireland to protect him from persecution by the IRA. The case raised a number of issues including the question of whether state complicity in the persecution was necessary in order to establish a claim as a convention refugee. The Supreme Court ruled that state complicity was not a necessary element of persecution. While the issue of state protection did not arise as a consequence of the Court's ruling on state complicity, the two are related issues.
