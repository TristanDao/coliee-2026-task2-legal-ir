Pursuant to the decision in
Dunsmuir v. New Brunswick
, [2008] 1 S.C.R. 190, administrative decisions are reviewable upon either the standard of correctness or reasonableness. Guidance as to the applicable standard to be applied to an issue may be found in the existing jurisprudence:
Dunsmuir
at paras. 54, 57. In view of this direction and the nature of the issue raised here, that is an assessment of establishment in the context of subsection 25(1) of the Act, I am satisfied that reasonableness is the appropriate standard of review; see
Buio v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(2007), 60 Imm. L.R. (3d) 212 at para. 17.
