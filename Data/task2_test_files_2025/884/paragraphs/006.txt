The Applicant challenges the decision on the grounds that the Officer failed to properly consider the best interests of her Canadian born children, in particular the fact that two of the children are enrolled in school and would be adversely affected by being relocated to Libya since they do not speak Arabic. She also argues that the Officer committed a reviewable error as a result of the manner in which he assessed the Applicant's establishment in Canada, including the fact that she is employed and has bought a house with her husband.
