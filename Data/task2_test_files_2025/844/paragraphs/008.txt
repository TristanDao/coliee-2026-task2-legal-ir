The respondents/defendants submit firstly that the court is without jurisdiction to make an order pursuant to rule 337(5) because it requires that such an application be brought within 10 days of the pronouncement of the judgment; and, secondly, that it may be reconsidered only before the court "as constituted at the time of the pronouncement". In support of this argument counsel for the defendants referred me to the following jurisprudence:
Malinge Building Ltd. v. The Queen
, [1983] 2 F.C. 301 (T.D.);
Crabbe v. Minister of Transport
, [1973] F.C. 1091 (F.C.A.); and
Steward v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, 84 N.R. 240; [1988] 3 F.C. 452 (F.C.A.).
