It is submitted by the plaintiff-applicant that the bail bonds not having been condemned was an oversight in the judgment of Addy, J.; that the oversight has been extremely costly to the plaintiff since it will be entitled to both pre and post judgment interest. Had the bail bonds been condemned and the monies paid into court there would have been an accrual of interest in order to help offset the amount to which the plaintiff and others will be entitled. He argues therefore that pursuant to rule 337 of the
Federal Court Rules
this court has the authority to reconsider the terms of the pronouncement or correct any errors arising from any accidental slip or omission.
