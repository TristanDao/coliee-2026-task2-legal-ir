Counsel for the defendants also refers me to the case of
Asbjorn Horgard A/S v. Gibbs/Nortac Industries Ltd.
(1987), 14 C.I.P.R. 17 (F.C.A.). This was a situation in which the applicant sought to vary a pronouncement. It was dismissed since the court concluded that "nothing had been overlooked or accidentally omitted". The court went on to say that there was "no discrepancy between the reasons for judgment and the pronouncement".
