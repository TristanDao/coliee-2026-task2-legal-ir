In
Centre Ice Ltd. v. National Hockey League et al.
(1994), 166 N.R. 44; 53 C.P.R.(3d) 34 (F.C.A.), the Federal Court of Appeal, in a trademark infringement case, allowed an appeal from the grant of an interlocutory injunction until trial on the basis irreparable harm, that is harm in respect of which damages recoverable at law would not be an adequate remedy, had not been established by the evidence adduced.
