Novopharm first argued LifeScan Canada had no standing to bring a trademark infringement proceeding because the
Trade-marks Act
provides that only the registered owner may bring such a proceeding. Novopharm further argued LifeScan Canada could not bring a statutory passing-off action because the trademark owner, Johnson & Johnson, is not a party to the proceedings and LifeScan Canada does not meet the conditions of section 50 of the
Act
