The decision of the Supreme Court of Canada in Baker v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), [1999] 2 S.C.R. 817, remains the leading case for the standard of review of decisions made on H&C grounds. In Baker, above, Justice Claire L’Heureux-Dubé engaged in a detailed pragmatic and functional analysis before deciding on reasonableness simpliciter as the proper standard. As stated by Justice Frank Iaccobucci in Canada (Director of Investigation and Research) v. Southam, [1997] 1 S.C.R. 748, at paragraph 56, “an unreasonable decision is one that, in the main, is not supported by any reasons that can stand up to a somewhat probing examination”.
