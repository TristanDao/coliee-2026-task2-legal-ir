The Board's decision, then, was based on its finding that the objective documentary evidence did not support a finding that the Egyptian government, either tacitly or overtly, tolerated the harassment which Islamic fundamentalists perpetrated. The applicant has a different view. But the CRDD does not have to agree with that view. It is entitled, in law, to assess the applicant's perceptions in the context of the evidence which he adduced of actual physical abuse and the documentary reports which exist concerning the government's activities and attitudes.
