[1] Reed, J.
: The applicant, Mohamed Basyony (hereinafter "the applicant"), filed a motion in the following terms:
"The motion is for the purpose of correcting any defect or error in the document initiated by notice of motion filed under section 24(1) of the
Charter
on February 2, 1994, and had been prepared (in form 7.1) pursuant to rule 319 of the
Federal Court Rules
, and for the purpose of determining the real question of law in a controversy that has been come into existence by an unauthorized act of the court's Registry Officers. It is also for receiving the direction and the order of the court provided in rule 303 of the
Federal Court Rules
.
"The grounds of this motion are:
1. That insofar as
(a) section 24(1) provides a remedy for alleged denials of constitutional rights directing complainants to apply for relief to 'a court of competent jurisdiction'.
(b) the jurisdictional competence of a court is not the issue that can be argued or decided by a registry officer of that court and
(c) the requirement for judicial review is also not the issue that can be argued or decided by one of the registry officers of this court, and having regad [sic] to the fact concerning the applicants' application filed on February 2, 1994, this court should rule that, acting on the authority that contravene the applicants' right to apply to this court under constitutional provisions, ignoring responsibilities under the
Charter
, or behaving in a manner that violates the
Charter
all qualify as a form of guilt, reviewable by this court.
"2. That the requirement to the court's direction and order under rule 303 of the
Federal Court Rules
is, extremely needed since the applicants' legal right to apply to this court for relief under the constitutional provisions, has been controled [sic] or almost abrogated by unauthorized act of the court's Registry Officers."
