A publication by Amnesty International dated October 23, 1991, also forms part of the record. The report is entitled
Egypt: Ten Years of Torture
. It notes, at page 2, "[M]ost political detainees subjected to torture and ill-treatment are alleged members or sympathizers of Islamic groups, or their relatives." With respect to the reports concerning specific individuals, that relating to Dr. Ahmed Ismail Mahmoud states, "He was arrested on 17 October 1990 when mass arrests followed the assassination of the Speaker of the People's Assembly, and [he] was accused of having links with the Muslim Brothers." With respect to another (Mohammad Khalaf Youssef) he is reported in the Amnesty International report as having said "They kept repeating that I would forget the names of everyone I knew, and accused me of being a Muslim Brother, ... They said the Muslim Brothers could do nothing to help me ...".
