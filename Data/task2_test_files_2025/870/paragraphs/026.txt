The onus was on the applicant to prove that his service as a dental assistant had caused, or aggravated, his lung condition. There was no onus on the Panel to establish an alternative cause for the current condition, such as smoking, in order to reject the applicant's appeal. In
Wood v. Canada (Attorney General)
, [2001] F.C.J. No. 52; 199 F.T.R. 133 (T.D.), above, Justice Andrew MacKay, at paragraph 24, commented on the necessity of a claimant to establish a causal link between the disability and military service:
"Sections 3 and 39 of the
Act
do not, however, relieve the applicant of the burden of proving that his low back pain arose out of or in connection with military service. The applicant must still establish on a balance of probabilities, with the evidence considered in the best light possible, that his disability is service-related." (citations omitted)
