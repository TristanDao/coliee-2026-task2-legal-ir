The first factor - the age difference - should not be considered, as a factor in favour of, or against, the legitimacy of the marriage. The age difference, in and of itself, does not indicate that the marriage is illegitimate. If the applicant had wanted to enter into a valid but illegitimate marriage, the applicant can argue that he would not have chosen a woman who is much older. The argument can be made that the age difference is a factor mitigating in favour of a legitimate marriage. The only way to properly assess the legitimacy of the marriage is through an interview.
