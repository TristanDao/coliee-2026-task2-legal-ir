The officer explained that the misrepresentation of the Applicant reflected poorly on his character and contributed negatively to the application. With respect to the marriage, the officer wrote that he was satisfied that they were married to each other. Concerning their purchase of a restaurant, he noted that it demonstrated a business relationship and not necessarily a personal one. Then, the officer pointed out some questionable elements about their marriage: their age difference, different cultural background and the fact they were married so quickly. These elements combined with the Applicant's previous history with Immigration in Canada and in the USA led the officer to conclude the marriage was for the purpose of immigration and to boost his chances of remaining in Canada. The marriage does not warrant an exception under IRPA.
