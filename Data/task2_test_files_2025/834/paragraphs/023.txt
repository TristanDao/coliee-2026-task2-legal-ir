The same argument can be made for the second factor, the religious difference. Had the applicant desired to form a valid, yet illegitimate marriage, he could have found a young Shia Muslim woman to marry him. In the opinion of the officer, a marriage to a young Shia woman would be more legitimate. The age and religious factors cannot be considered as against the legitimacy of the marriage. The religious difference should not be considered a factor against the legitimacy of the marriage, especially in a multicultural Canadian society that pledges to uphold the credo of 'unity in diversity'. Without an interview, the religious difference is not a valid concern in the present matter.
