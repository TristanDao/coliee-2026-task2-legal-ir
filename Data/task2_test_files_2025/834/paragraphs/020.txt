However, despite the fact that an interview is not necessary in each case, the impugned decision raises some issues that were not reasonably addressed by the Officer. I am of the opinion that the applicant was not afforded "meaningful participation" (Baker, supra) with respect to defending the legitimacy of his marriage. In the present case, the officer dealt with the evidence that before him in a perverse and capricious manner by speculating on the validity of the marriage. He questioned the timing and circumstances of the applicant's marriage using four main factors: (i) the age difference, (ii) the religious difference, (iii) the speed of the marriage, and (iv) the business (rather than personal) nature of the co-owned restaurant.
