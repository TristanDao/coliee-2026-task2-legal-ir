Rule 488 referred to by defendant in support of its motion has no bearing on the matter. We are not here dealing with two or more causes of action but merely with a variety of defences raised against one action for infringement of patent; it would not appear to be desirable to deal with it piecemeal, even though time would be saved if defendant's contentions on this issue were sustained. In any event there would most likely be an appeal resulting in further delay of the action for infringement. The case of the Clarkson Company Limited v. R., [1978] 1 F.C. 481, can also be distinguished. In that case Justice Mahoney allowed the parties to apply under Rule 483 to set down a special case for hearing in lieu of trial. This was to be on the basis of an agreed statement of facts and issues.
