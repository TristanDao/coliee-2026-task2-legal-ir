Before mandamus will issue, there must be a finding that the delay complained of is so excessive as to be unreasonable and inconsistent with the rules of natural justice. The test is articulated in
Conille
, supra, and all branches of the test must be satisfied. The requirements are:
(1) the delay in question has been longer than the nature of the process requires, prima facie;
(2) the applicant and his counsel are not responsible for the delay; and
(3) the authority responsible for the delay has not provided satisfactory justification.
