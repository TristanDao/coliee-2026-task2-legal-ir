There is no basis upon which to find that the delay is systemic. There is no evidence except for the chronology of events and the visa officer's statement that the work was "on-going". I agree that the application was in motion, but it was not going anywhere. There was no progress being made and Mr. Hanano was caught in a cycle. That is what distinguishes this matter from
Singh
, supra. There is no indication that the workload in Damascus is excessive, although given Mr. Justice Kelen's comments in
Dragan
, supra, justification on that basis would be difficult. There is no evidence to suggest that there is a backlog of cases in Damascus. There, quite simply, is no evidence before me that provides justification for the excessive delay in rendering a decision in relation to this application.
