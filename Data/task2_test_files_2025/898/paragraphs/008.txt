The standard of review applicable to the Board's findings of fact, and assessment of credibility is patent unreasonableness. The Court will not substitute the Board's decision with its own, except the Board's decision is clearly wrong. See Aguebor v. Minister of Employment and Immigration (1993), 160 N.R. 315 (F.C.A.), and De (Da) Li Chen v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) (1999), 49 Imm. L.R. (2d) 161 (F.C.A.). It is open to Board to make a negative credibility finding based on omissions within a claimant's evidence.
