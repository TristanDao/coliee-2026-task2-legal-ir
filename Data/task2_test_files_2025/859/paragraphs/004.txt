By statement of opposition dated March 31, 1983, the appellant (opponent) opposed the above-noted application for registration of the trademark "Olympian" on the following grounds:
"I.(a) the application does not comply with the requirements of section 29. The applicant was at the time of the application aware of the opponent's marks and could not have been satisfied as to its entitlement to use the mark which is the subject of this opposition, in breach of section 29(i);
"(b) the applicant alleges use of the trademark in association with the wares set forth in the application since at least as early as July 3, 1977 whereas in fact and the fact is that the alleged trademark has not been used in association with the wares set forth since the indicated date;
"(c) the applicant is not entitled to obtain registration of the trademark set forth in the application as it is confusing with the trade name of the opponent 'Canadian Olympic Association' which has been used in Canada since at least as early as January 3, 1952.
"II. The applicant is not entitled to registration of the mark in view of sections 9, 11 and 12(1)(e) of the Trade Marks Act and in view of the public notice given by the Registrar in the Trade Marks Journal for various official marks as annexed to the statement of opposition.
"III. The trademark is not adapted to distinguish the services in association with which the applicant intends to use the trademark from the wares and services of others and more particularly the wares and services of the official marks referred to above."
