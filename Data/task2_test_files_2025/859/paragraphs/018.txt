There is no need to deal with the latter two grounds in view of my findings regarding registrability. I was, however, satisfied that in law the material date is the date of filing of the opposition and that, although the party seeking to register a trademark need not support the date alleged as first use, if this is challenged then the registrant must provide evidence of that fact. However, as indicated earlier, I accept without question that an application for a trademark which resembles (and here the word is Olympian) a s. 9 prohibited mark is not registrable over a prohibited or official mark no matter when adopted or used.
