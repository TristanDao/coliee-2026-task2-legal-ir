Ms. Dubois argues that the Commission should have dealt with these allegations in spite of the delay because the Treasury Board harassment policy encourages employees to seek internal redress before seeking other recourses. The Court notes that the Treasury Board policy merely advises employees that they have the right to file a complaint with the Commission if it is based on a prohibited ground of discrimination. However, if the employee chooses to file a complaint with the Commission, the complaint process under the Treasury Board policy will be stayed. This policy avoids duplicative procedures, but does not encourage one particular recourse over that of another.
