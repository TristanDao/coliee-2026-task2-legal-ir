The applicant has relied on a number of cases which, in his view, stand for the proposition that work experience on the job is relevant to whether a person is qualified for the job and that a person should not be found to be unqualified for a job solely because the person does not hold a formal qualification established by the employer: see, for ex.,
I.M.P. Group Ltd. v. National Automobile, Aerospace, Transportation and General Workers Union of Canada (CAW-Canada), Local 2215 et al.
(2002), 205 N.S.R.(2d) 179; 643 A.P.R. 179 (S.C.);
Montreal Children's Hospital v. Federation of United Nurses Union, Local 220
(1974), 8 L.A.C.(2d) 17;
Sunbeam Home v. London and District Service Workers Employees' Union, Local 368
(1977), 14 L.A.C.(2d) 350.
