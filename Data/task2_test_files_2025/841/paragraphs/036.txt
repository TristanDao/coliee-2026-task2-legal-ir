Implied in the power to classify is the power to determine the classification standard and minimum qualifications for positions. As noted by the British Columbia Supreme Court in
Babcock et al. v. Canada (Attorney General) et al.
, [2005] B.C.T.C. 513; 2005 BCSC 513, at para. 174:
"The
FAA
authorizes TBC to unilaterally create terms and conditions of employment, classify positions, set rates of pay and to administer the salaries of unrepresented or excluded public service employees."
