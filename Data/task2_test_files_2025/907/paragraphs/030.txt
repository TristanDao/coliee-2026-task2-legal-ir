In any event, I find no ambiguity in the regulation requiring that the
Charter
be enlisted to arrive at a purposive interpretation to accommodate the applicant. Nor do I need to consider whether the amended regulation should be given retrospective effect as, in my view, neither the amendment nor the text of the
Operations Manual
bear the meaning the applicant would have me give to them. They do not support the interpretation that non-accompanying family members did not have to be declared, but rather clarify that as such non-accompanying family members did not need to be examined under the former legislation, they should not now be penalized under
IRPA
if the choice to examine or not was made by officials. I cannot read in words to change what appears to me to be the unambiguous intent of the regulation.
