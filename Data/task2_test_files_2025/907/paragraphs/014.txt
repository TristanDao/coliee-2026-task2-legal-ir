The applicant submits that the standard of review for the decision of the IAD should be correctness. The respondent made no submissions on this question. Considering the four factors that the Court must take into account in determining the standard of review of any administrative decision, I conclude that for questions of mixed law and fact, the standard of review should be reasonableness:
Ly v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2003] 4 F.C. 658; 232 F.T.R. 270 (T.D.);
Collier v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(2004), 260 F.T.R. 266; 2004 FC 1209. For questions of interpretation of the law, the standard is correctness:
Pushpanathan v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [1998] 1 S.C.R. 982, addendum [1998] 1 S.C.R. 1222; 226 N.R. 201; 160 D.L.R.(4th) 193.
