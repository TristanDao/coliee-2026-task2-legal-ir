Upon close review of the Henderson Report, I conclude that its contents are generally political in nature and better suited to a forum other than a court of law. Indeed, that part of the Henderson Report which deals with the RCAP Report appears to be more along the lines of a summary, rather than expert opinion. And while such an undertaking may be helpful, as the RCAP Report consists of five volumes, it is not necessary and is more properly a function of counsel in argument.
