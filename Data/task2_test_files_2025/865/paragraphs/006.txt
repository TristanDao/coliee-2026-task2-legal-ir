Mr. Hobbs has never been qualified as an expert witness. Nor have plaintiff's counsel provided any evidence suggesting that Mr. Hobbs' alleged field of expertise is one that has received acceptance by any court. However, there is no finite range of topics about which an expert may testify and the field is in a continual state of flux - see Sopinka, Lederman, and Bryant,
The Law of Evidence in Canada
(1992), at p. 534. That he has never before been qualified as an expert or that no one else may ever have been qualified in his field does not preclude him from being considered a properly qualified expert.
