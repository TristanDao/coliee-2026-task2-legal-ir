In these circumstances, if I were to issue special directions concerning costs after March 30, 2000, in view of the unaccepted offer to settle, it would be that costs after that date, excluding disbursements, be awarded at 1.33 units, or 133%, of the units for party-and-party costs prior to that date. That difference recognizes the principle purpose of Rule 420(1), to benefit a party that makes a serious offer to settle an action before trial, but at a level less than that suggested in Rule 420(1), a level this court considers more appropriate in this case. In
Apotex Inc. et al. v. Wellcome Foundation Ltd.
(1998), 84 C.P.R.(3d) 303 (F.C.T.D.), Mr. Justice Wetston, in the circumstances of that case, allowed costs subsequent to the date of an unaccepted offer to settle, at 150 percent.
