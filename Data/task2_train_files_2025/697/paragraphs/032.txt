The Board's decision is consistent with Justice Michael Phelan's decision in
Usta v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2004] F.T.R. Uned. 961; [2004] F.C.J. No. 1832; 2004 FC 1525, which addressed whether Turkey's requirement to complete military service, even after serving prison time for refusing to serve, is contrary to section 97 of the
Act
. Justice Phelan concluded, at paragraph 16, that the requirement to meet one's citizenship duties, even after imprisonment, does not, in and of itself, rise to the level of torture or risk to life and is not inherently cruel.
