Contrary to Mr. Ozunal's submissions, the Board specifically assessed the possibility that he would be submitted to a sentence of up to three years imprisonment. Nevertheless, it concluded that the possibility of being imprisoned for up to three years is not an excessive or over-harsh sentence. This conclusion is consistent with the decision of this Court in
Moskvitchev v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [1995] F.C.J. No. 1744, at paragraph 7, in which a sentence for draft evasion of between six months and five years was not considered to be inhuman or extreme.
