The Board noted that while Mr. Ozunal had clearly expressed his aversion to military service, his explanations were not sufficient to lead to the conclusion that he is a conscientious objector. Refusal to serve because one bears aversion to combat or military action cannot in and of itself justify granting refugee status. (
Musial v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1982] 1 F.C. 290; 38 N.R. 55 (F.C.A.);
Popov v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1994] F.C.J. No. 489; 75 F.T.R. 90 (T.D.)).
