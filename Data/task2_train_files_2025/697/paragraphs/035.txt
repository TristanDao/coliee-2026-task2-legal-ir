It is well established that as a specialized tribunal, the Board is entitled to assess the documentary evidence and to draw conclusions from it. In
Ganiyu-Giwa v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [1995] F.C.J. No. 506, at paragraph 2, Mr. Justice Wetson, citing
Tawfik
, held as follows:
"With respect to the objective basis for determining the country conditions in Nigeria, it is clear that the Board did examine the situation and found that there was only a mere possibility of persecution. While there is evidence which appears to support that there was not a promising change in Nigeria, there is also evidence to support that there was only a mere possibility of persecution, should the applicant return. In
Tawfik v. M.E.I.
, 93-A-311, August 23, 1993, MacKay, J., noted that the Board often refers to documents that contain both supporting and non-supporting references. The Board may select, as part of its role and part of its expertise, the evidence that it prefers. That is what was done in the case."
