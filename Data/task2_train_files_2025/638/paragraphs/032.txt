Case law has many examples of situations in which investigations were considered deficient because of a lack of thoroughness. The failure to question one or several key witnesses who would have had a fundamental impact on the resolution of the initial complaint and the failure to deal with an important aspect of that complaint were considered by this Court and by the Federal Court of Appeal as being breaches of the duty of procedural fairness:
Kollar
at paragraph 39, supra;
Thamourpour
at paragraph. 40, supra; and
Grover
v.
Canada (National Research Council)
, 2001 FCT 687.
