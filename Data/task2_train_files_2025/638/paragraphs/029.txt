It is also settled law that the Commission must act fairly in handling the complaints it receives. In the context of investigations, this duty of procedural fairness dictates that two requirements be met: neutrality and thoroughness. These requirements were developed by Nadon J., as he then was, in
Slattery
, supra, and were subsequently confirmed on numerous occasions: see the recent example of
Gravelle
v.
Canada (Attorney General)
, 2006 FC 251, rendered by my colleague Mr. Justice Edmond Blanchard.
