The adjudicator then turned to consider whether Ms. Nutbrown had been dismissed. The adjudicator determined that the employer had unilaterally made substantial changes to the contract of employment, given that it had removed a "substantial number of duties" and left tasks of a clerical nature. The adjudicator rejected Mr. Lepitre's evidence that he had offered Ms. Nutbrown a full-time job in Montréal, since this evidence had not been mentioned in testimony-in-chief and yet would have been an important element of the employer's argument that there had been no dismissal.
