As noted by the Supreme Court in
Flieger
, supra, in
Mudarth v. Canada (Minister of Public Works)
, [1989] 3 F.C. 371; 22 F.T.R. 312 (T.D.) (referred to in the above quotation), the plaintiff was a secretary whose work was parcelled out to other, existing, employees. Cory, J., stated, at 661:
"The approach taken in
Mudarth
relates the word 'function' to the 'office' held by the plaintiff. Her office consisted of the bundle of tasks and responsibilities which had been undertaken and performed by her as a secretary. This bundle of tasks was no longer being performed. Thus her office had ceased to exist and there was accordingly a 'discontinuance of a function'."
