It is trite law that a tribunal is presumed to have considered and weighed all of the evidence that was before it unless there is clear and persuasive evidence to the contrary. Moreover, it is a well accepted principle of law that a tribunal is not under a general duty or obligation to summarize all of the evidence that was considered and weighed in coming to its conclusions. The applicant has offered no persuasive evidence to demonstrate that the IAD ignored the evidence submitted by the applicant. Furthermore, that evidence is at best inconclusive as to the length of time the applicant effectively resided in Canada and is certainly not sufficient to show that the IAD's conclusion is patently unreasonable.
