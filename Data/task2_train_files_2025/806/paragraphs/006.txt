A visa officer has authority to determine whether an alleged dependant child has been enrolled and in attendance as a full-time student in a genuine, meaningful and bona fide respect. This determination is in most cases a pure question of fact, and is thus entitled to a significant amount of deference by a reviewing court (
Sharma v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2002] F.C.J. No. 1178; 223 F.T.R. 152 (T.D.);
Kainth v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2002] F.T.R. Uned. 858; [2002] F.C.T. 1296 (T.D.)). As a consequence, this Court will intervene only in those cases were the decision under review is patently unreasonable. This is most certainly the standard applicable with respect to the decision affecting Shirina.
