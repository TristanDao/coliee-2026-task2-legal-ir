Justice Sexton, speaking for the court in that case, then went on with a series of factors that can be considered in making such a determination. What clearly emerges from that decision is that as difficult as it may sometimes be to determine whether a person is a bona fide student, it will at the very least be essential to show a regular physical attendance to the various courses required by the academic program in which that person is enrolled. More may be required to demonstrate a real commitment to a course of studies, but going to classes will in all cases be the bare minimum.
