Relying on
Ali v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [1998] F.C.J. No. 948; 151 F.T.R. 1 (T.D.), the Applicant contended that they should have been given the opportunity to alleviate the Visa Officer's concerns stemming from the email. In that case, my colleague Justice Teitelbaum did mention that the visa officer should inform the applicant of his concerns when he has obtained extrinsic evidence. But he then goes on to add:
"In essence, where an interview is necessary to assess an applicant, the duty of fairness requires that the visa officer thoroughly interview the applicant on factors relevant to the claim and give the applicant an opportunity to respond to allegations or assumptions of which the applicant could not be reasonably aware."
