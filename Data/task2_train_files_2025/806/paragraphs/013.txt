As for the alleged breach of the duty of fairness and the visa officer's failure to give the applicant and the dependants an opportunity to provide additional information with respect to their status as full-time students, I can find no merit with this argument. It is true that the visa officer took into consideration an email that was sent to her by a Migration Integrity Officer, who reported after having visited the institutions where the dependants were enrolled that Alamgir was a "private student" and did not attend classes, and that Shirina had indeed two gaps in her academic curriculum. But this did not amount to the breach of a principle of natural justice.
