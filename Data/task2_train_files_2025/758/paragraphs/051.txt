The alternative remedy proposed by Mr. North is that the matter be referred back to the Adjudicator for reconsideration. While the more usual remedy would be to refer this matter back to a different decision maker, I believe that the errors identified in these reasons relate to the Adjudicator's failure to provide a proper assessment of the record and his failure to address certain key issues before him. In this case, Adjudicator Paterson is in the best position to reconsider his decision. I would leave it up to the Adjudicator to establish a procedure that would best serve the interests of the parties in reaching an expeditious outcome of this already too lengthy process. This would include a decision by the Adjudicator as to whether he needs to hear further submissions or evidence by the parties or whether he can fulfil his obligations based on the record already before him. In the event that Adjudicator Paterson is unable to reconsider his decision, the matter should be remitted to another adjudicator for redetermination.
