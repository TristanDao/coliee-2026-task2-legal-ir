The second issue relates to the grounds for Mr. North's dismissal. Put simply, why did the Employer terminate Mr. North's employment? This is a determination of fact that requires the Adjudicator to analyze the evidence before him. In my view, the standard of patent unreasonableness is applicable to this question. On questions of fact, I can intervene only if I consider that the Adjudicator "based its decision or order on an erroneous finding of fact that it made in a perverse or capricious manner or without regard for the material before it" (Federal Court Act, R.S.C. 1985, c. F-7, s. 18.1(4)(d)).
