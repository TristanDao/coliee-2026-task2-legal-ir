More recently, in Baldrey v. H & R Transport Ltd., 2005 FCA 729, the Federal Court of Appeal visited the standard of review applicable to decisions of an adjudicator under the Canada Labour Code. The issue in that case was a determination of the common law principles applicable to the determination of whether an employee has been dismissed. The Court of Appeal concluded, at para. 7, that the appropriate standard of review was correctness since the question required an interpretation of the "law of the land as opposed to the law of the parties, a domain where, in general, no deference is owed".
