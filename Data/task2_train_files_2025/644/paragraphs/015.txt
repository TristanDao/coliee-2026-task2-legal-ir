There have been a number of decisions of this court which have dealt with the incompetence of counsel:
Gyamfi v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration)
(92-T-776, August 9, 1993, F.C.T.D.);
Sarafimoski v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration)
(1990), 12 Imm. L.R.(2d) 5 (F.C.A.);
Sheikh v. M.E.I.
(1990), 112 N.R. 61; 11 Imm. L.R.(2d) 81 (F.C.A.);
Shirwa v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration)
(A-1290-90, December 16, 1993, F.C.T.D.) [(1993), 71 F.T.R. 136]. In
Shirwa
, for example, it was held that the incompetence of the applicant's representative resulted in a breach of the rules of natural justice, since these dictate that an individual is entitled to a competent and careful representative.
