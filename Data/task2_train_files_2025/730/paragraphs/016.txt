The plaintiffs argue that the existence of documentary evidence related to the testimony of an absent witness has been considered sufficient in certain circumstances to lessen the prejudice caused to a defendant (see
Nova Scotia v. Foundation of Canada Engineering Corp. et al.
(1981), 56 N.S.R.(2d) 624; 117 A.P.R. 624; 22 C.P.C. 239 (T.D.)). However, each case must be judged on its facts. In the instant situation, there exist serious questions not only as to the content of Mr. Petticrew's conversations, but also with respect to the motives and assumptions underlying his actions. These cannot be fully explored through a perusal of the transcript of communications he had with the plaintiffs' representatives.
