This court has held that the loss of the testimony of important witnesses constitutes sufficient grounds for the dismissal of an action. In
Kanematsu-Gosho (Canada) Ltd. et al. v. Midas Navigation Corp.
, T-4200-75, May 15th, 1984, unreported (F.C.T.D.), for example, Rouleau, J., dismissed the action after a delay of six years because the cargo officer and the office manager of the defendants were no longer in their employ and their wherabouts were unknown. In the case at bar, I am satisfied that Mr. Petticrew had important evidence to give, evidence which could have been obtained before his death had the plaintiffs not delayed the proceedings for so long. As such, I conclude that the loss of this evidence has seriously prejudiced the defendants.
