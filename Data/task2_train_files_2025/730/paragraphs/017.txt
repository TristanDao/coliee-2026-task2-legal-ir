Finally, I do not consider it necessary to decide whether the informal nature of the proceedings after 1991 are of an inculpatory or exculpatory nature with respect to the present motion. The fact remains that the plaintiffs' original delay has affected the fairness of the trial. Though I am mindful of depriving the plaintiffs of their substantive rights on procedural grounds, I find that the following excerpt from the decision of Muldoon, J., in
Waterside Cargo Co-Operative v. National Harbours Board
(1986), 3 F.T.R. 189 (T.D.), is applicable in this instance. He states that:
"... justice implies some balance or some equalizing. The plaintiff did itself an essential injustice, by its solicitor, in letting an essential time run against itself and against the defendant. The equitable rule which comes to mind is that 'Equity aids the vigilant'. Here the plaintiff slept on whatever rights it had for almost all of the past five years."
