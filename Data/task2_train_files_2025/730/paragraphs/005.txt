In response to the plaintiffs' request relating to discoveries, the solicitor for the defendants indicated on December 9, 1991 that the plaintiffs would be required to provide security for the defendants' costs before the defendants agreed to proceed any further. The plaintiffs' solicitor indicated to counsel for the defendants by letter, dated January 3, 1991, that he was instructed to meet them on an informal basis in order to avoid, among other things, having to pay the security into court. The solicitors for the parties met informally on April 14, 1992. There is some disagreement as to what informally meant. It is alleged by the plaintiffs that the parties agreed to proceed with discoveries, disclosures and settlement discussions on an informal basis.
