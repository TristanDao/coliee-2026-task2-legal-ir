In
Nichols v. Canada et al.
(1990), 36 F.T.R. 77 (T.D.), Dubé, J., recognized that the longer the delay, the more prejudicial its effect especially with respect to the recollection, and by extension, the presence of key witnesses. The defendants argue that Mr. Petticrew is a key witness in this action since he authorized the tow to proceed. This contradicts the defendants' allegations that negotiations were still underway between the parties and is contrary to the actions of Captain Ken Raisbeck, who was the individual in direct contact with the plaintiffs' representatives on the day of the incident until Mr. Petticrew intervened. Without the latter's viva voce testimony, the defendants claim that it will not be possible to have a fair trial of the issues.
