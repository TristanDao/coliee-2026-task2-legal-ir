In this case, the Board wanted to satisfy itself that the applicant was indeed the daughter of Umba-Di Lutete, the former official of the Mobutu regime. In this regard, we can understand that the best way to prove the applicant's parentage would have been by producing a birth certificate on which the names of the applicant's father and mother appeared. As the Board observed, the birth certificate filed was lacking in this respect. As it then was a matter of assessing the credibility of the applicant's testimony, the Board was in a better position than the Court to determine, absent satisfactory documentary evidence, if she was indeed, as she claims, the daughter of Umba-Di Lutete.
