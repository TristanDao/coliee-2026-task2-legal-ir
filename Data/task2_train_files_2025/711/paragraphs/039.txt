In
Hunter v. West Midlands Police (Chief Constable) et al.
, [1982] A.C. 529 (H.L.), the House of Lords considered whether a litigant could raise in a civil action the identical question that had already been decided in a criminal court of competent jurisdiction. In its analysis at p. 543, the House of Lords noted that
Hollington v. Hewthorn & Co.
, supra, was "... generally considered to have been wrongly decided ...", and referred to the statutory provisions enacted to overrule it. At pp. 543 to 545, the House of Lords concluded that, as a general rule of public policy, the use of a civil action to initiate a collateral attack on the final decision made by a criminal court of competent jurisdiction should be treated as an abuse of the process of the court. The sole exception countenanced by the House of Lords to that general rule was the existence of "fresh evidence" obtained following the criminal trial that "... entirely changes the aspect of the case."
