In
Ahani v. Canada
, [1995] 3 F.C. 669; 100 F.T.R. 261 (T.D.), affd. (1996), 201 N.R. 233 (F.C.A.), leave to appeal to the Supreme Court of Canada refd. July 3, 1997, I considered the constitutional validity of s. 40.1 of the
Immigration Act
. Under the terms of that provision, a designated judge is required to determine, on a reference, the reasonableness of a ministerial certificate identifying a named person as a member of certain inadmissible classes of persons. At p. 685 [F.C.], I noted that the s. 40.1 proceedings were directed solely and exclusively to determining the reasonableness of the certificate. In the event that a designated judge determined the certificate to be reasonable, a further decision would have to be made by the Minister under s. 53 of the
Immigration Act
before the person could be deported from Canada.
