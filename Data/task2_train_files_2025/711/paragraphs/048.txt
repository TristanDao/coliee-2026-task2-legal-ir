I am satisfied, on the basis of my review of the jurisprudence, that the evidence of the respondent's conviction under s. 29(2)(a) of the
Citizenship Act
for the offence of knowingly concealing material circumstances for the purposes of obtaining citizenship constitutes prima facie proof in these reference proceedings of the fact of his guilt on that charge. Given that s. 18(1)(b) of the
Citizenship Act
requires a decision to be made on this reference on the question of whether the respondent obtained citizenship by knowingly concealing material circumstances, I must decide, on the basis of the civil standard of proof, precisely the same issue that has already been determined by the Provincial Court judge according to the criminal law standard. In other words, there is a clear identity of issue in the circumstances of the present case.
