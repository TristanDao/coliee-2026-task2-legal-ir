In the circumstances, I am satisfied that the basic interpretive principles enunciated in
Ahani v. Canada
, supra, are applicable to citizenship matters. I have therefore concluded that the scope of the proceedings under s. 18 of the
Citizenship Act
must be analysed in the context of principles and policies underlying immigration and citizenship law, and not in the criminal law context. Indeed, as indicated earlier, a judge conducting a reference under s. 18 of the
Citizenship Act
makes only a factual finding concerning the circumstances under which a person obtained his Canadian citizenship. To paraphrase my words in
Ahani v. Canada
, supra, that factual finding is purely and simply a citizenship matter. In the circumstances, I agree with Collier, J., in
Canada (Secretary of State) v. Luitjens
, supra, that a reference conducted under s. 18 of the
Citizenship Act
is in the nature of a civil proceeding in which the civil standard of proof applies.
