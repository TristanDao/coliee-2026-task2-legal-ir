The Prothonotary found that the Defendants had mischaracterized the Plaintiffs' pleadings. He held that the action was not for the improper issuance of export licenses, the failure to properly account for export sales, or an accounting for monies not entered into the pooled account, as alleged by the Defendants. Rather, the Prothonotary stated that the action was for an alleged improper deducting of expenses from the pooled account. He further held that "at this juncture there is reasonable doubt that the Plaintiffs have any case to answer on this motion". However, he proceeded to consider the Defendants' motion to strike.
