Concerning the second notice of allega­tion, the issue was whether a claim for a pharmaceutical composition is a "claim for the medicine itself" as defined in s. 2 of the
Patented Medicines (Notice of Com­pli­ance) Regulations
. That issue was resolved affirmatively by the Court of Appeal in
Nu-Pharm Inc. v. Hoffmann-La Roche Ltd.
[see footnote 4] and Apotex has since dis­continued its appeal under court file no. A-788-95 from the second order of MacKay, J., of November 22, 1995 [see footnote 5] which prohibited the issuance of a Notice of Compliance to Apotex until after the expir­ation of patent '334. Bayer no longer relies on the issue of res judicata concerning this prohibition order of MacKay, J., and the third Apotex notice of allegation which is the subject matter of this proceed­ing. It is now settled that "... successive allegations are possible and each one must be treated independently provided it is separ­ate and distinct from the others ...", [see footnote 6] even where the allegation has been made after the issuance of a prohibition order.
