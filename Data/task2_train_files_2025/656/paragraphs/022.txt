Neither party filed the '067 patent. Bayer strongly urged that there is no evi­dence in this proceeding as to the contents of the '067 patent. In my view, however, the relevant extracts of the '334 patent, the notice of allegation, the Dedication to the Public and the Apotex affidavit establish, if there was any doubt on the issue, that both patents concern the same compound ciproflo­xacin hydrochloride. Bayer also acknowl­edges that the parent application was subject to s. 41(1) of the
Patent Act
. [see footnote 21] From this, one can only con­clude that the claims in the '067 patent are compound by process. The first claim in the '334 patent is for a composition comprising as an active ingredient a pharmaceutically effective amount of ciprofloxacin hydrochloride with a pharmaceutically accep­table diluent or carrier. I have not under­stood what informa­tion may be contained in the '067 patent, not otherwise disclosed in the parties' evidence, which is necessary from Apotex' point of view to support its allegation of invalidity. This is particularly so in view of Bayer's acknowledgment of no new inventiveness in the '334 patent other than that in the com­pound itself. [see foot­note 22] The issue here is the validity of the composition claims in the '334 patent, not the process-dependent compound claims in the '067 patent.
