Hoechst, in its February 10, 1997 statement of claim attacked the Commissioner's awards in respect of conflict claims C-18, C-19, C-20 to C-24, C-26, C-28 and C-31 and 32 on various grounds including (a) allegations the Commissioner was in error in awarding those claims to others than Hoechst because Hoechst was the first inventor; (b) the disclosure in the specification for the conflict claims are directed or do not support the invention claimed; (c) the awards should be restricted to certain limited and specific compounds and, (d) in the alternative, that the court approve certain claims not awarded to Hoechst as substitute claims.
