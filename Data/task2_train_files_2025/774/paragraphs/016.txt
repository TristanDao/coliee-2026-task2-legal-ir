The Applicant argues that the IAD unreasonably fettered
its discretion by focusing on its “one-step abandonment
policy” and s.168 of the IRPA. An administrative decision which is the
result of fettered discretion “draws upon something
other than the law—for example a decision based solely upon an informal policy
statement without regard or cognizance of law…” (Stemijon, at
para 24).
