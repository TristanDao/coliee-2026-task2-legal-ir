The Applicant relies on a number of cases in the
refugee context to argue that to be in default of proceedings, “it must be clear that…an applicant’s behaviour evidences, in
clear terms, a wish or intention not to proceed” (Cabrera Peredo v
Canada (Citizenship and Immigration), 2010 FC 390; Emani v Canada (Citizenship
and Immigration), 2009 FC 520, at para 20). The Applicant argues that he
should have been given an opportunity to explain his circumstances in a show
cause hearing.
