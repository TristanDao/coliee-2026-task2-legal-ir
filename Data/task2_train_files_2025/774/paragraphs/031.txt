There is no duty on the IAD to pursue the
Applicant. As noted in Dubrézil v Canada (Minister of Citizenship and
Immigration), 2006 FC 142 at para 12 [Dubrézil]:
If the applicant's reasoning were followed,
it would imply that each time a person is absent, lacks diligence or acts in
such a way that clearly suggests that the appeal has been abandoned, the IAD
would be bound to investigate to find those persons, to remind them of their
obligations and to summon them to a new hearing before deciding that the
proceedings are abandoned. I cannot accept such an interpretation, especially
because in this case the applicant did not advise the IAD of the change in his
contact information, so that in any event the IAD would not have been able to
contact him to summon him to a new hearing if it had had such an obligation.
