A few words are in order concerning motions to strike in NOC proceedings. Prothonotary Aalto recently reviewed the jurisprudence of this Court relating to motions to strike affidavit evidence in this context: see
Proctor & Gamble Pharmaceuticals Canada Inc. v. Canada (Minister of Health),
2009 FC 113. I agree with his observation that such motions must be the exception and that complaints about the evidence are best left to the hearing judge. In my view, it is only where the evidence challenged is clearly improper, in the sense that it is scandalous or vexatious, or it is without doubt abusive or irrelevant, that the evidence ought to be struck prior to the hearing.
