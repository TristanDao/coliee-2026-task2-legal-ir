Although an application for leave to appeal is "a first, and lower, hurdle for the applicant to meet than that that must be met on the hearing of the appeal on the merits", the applicant must still satisfy the Court that there is some arguable ground upon which the proposed appeal might succeed. Before granting leave, the Court must be able to see a specific question of law, or fact, or jurisdiction, the answer to which may lead to setting aside the decision under attack
