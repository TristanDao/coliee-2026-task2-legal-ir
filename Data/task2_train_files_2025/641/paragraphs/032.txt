It was also open to the Board to reject the Applicant’s explanation on why the POE notes make no reference to the Applicant’s fear of her brother-in-law upon return to Sierra Leone, a central aspect of her claim. This is particularly so in circumstances where the explanation itself is based on contradictory evidence. At the outset of the hearing the Applicant explained that she did mention her brother-in-law at the border, and that the Immigration Officer simply did not take down this information in his notes. Later, in response to a question from the Board Member, the Applicant stated that, “…the lady [Immigration Officer] did not ask me…”. The evidence also indicates that the Applicant’s own written statement at the POE did not refer to her brother-in-law. In my view it was open to the Board to make an adverse credibility finding with respect to the central aspect of the Applicant’s claim from its absence in the POE. There is no evidence to suggest that the Board failed to consider the Applicant’s explanation. In these circumstances it was sufficient for the Board to simply state that it did not accept the Applicant’s “explanation” for the omission. The Board committed no reviewable error in making this finding.
