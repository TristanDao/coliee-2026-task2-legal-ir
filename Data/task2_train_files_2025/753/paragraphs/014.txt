Despite the able argument of counsel for the respondent, I am satisfied that this matter falls directly within the exception enunciated by Mr. Justice Hugessen. The immigration officer here relied on extrinsic evidence, not brought forward by the applicant (as to the meaning of "extrinsic evidence", see
Dasent v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1994), 87 F.T.R. 282 (T.D.), cited by me in
Garcia v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
(1994), 87 F.T.R. 268). In relying on that evidence, she may have completely misinterpreted the basis of the applicant's claim. She gave the applicant no chance to respond to the evidence and therefore to correct any misapprehension as to the basis of the claim.
