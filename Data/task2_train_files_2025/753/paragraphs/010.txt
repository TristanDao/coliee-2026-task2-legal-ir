In argument before me, counsel for the applicant took the position that the immigration officer misinterpreted the basis for the applicant's request for humanitarian and compassionate relief. He argued that the basis was not her in-vitro fertilization, but rather the fact that in-vitro fertilization was accomplished through the use of donor semen. He did not dispute the fact that in-vitro fertilization is an accepted practice in Iran. He argued that, by contrast, the use of donor semen in in-vitro fertilization is considered to be equivalent to adultery and both morally offensive and a crime.
