There is no evidence suggesting that the search was carried out in an unreasonable manner. As to the reasonableness of the seizure and retention of records, the records were detained in anticipation of and preparation for the criminal proceedings. Ms. David claimed that one reason for Olympia's inaccurate returns after the seizure was her inability to access her records and that the loss of records caused damages to Olympia. But access to the records was provided to Ms. David on request and some of the records were copied for her. Retention of the records for some months following the stay of prosecution proceedings was not unreasonable in the circumstances since the stay did not preclude an application to resume proceedings. I conclude there is not evidence that the search or the resulting seizure violated Ms. David's or Olympia's rights under s. 8 of the
