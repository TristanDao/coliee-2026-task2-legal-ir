The plaintiffs adduced no evidence said to be specifically related to the rights secured by s. 11(b) and (d). Nevertheless, I review the evidence that is before me. In regard to s. 11(b), though the Supreme Court of Canada has refused to set a fixed period of time within which a trial must be held, it has ruled there are four factors to weigh in determining whether a delay was unreasonable: the length of delay, whether the accused waived any time periods, the reasons for the delay and the prejudice suffered by the accused. [see footnote 32] The length of the delay is calculated from when the charge is laid so that delay arising in the investigatory stage, before charges are laid, is generally irrelevant.
