In oral submissions at the conclusion of trial Ms. David based Olympia's claim to harm or damages in tort substantially upon the difficulties it faced after seizure of its records and commencement of prosecution. In my opinion, whether damages lie for those difficulties depends upon whether they can be found to have been caused by some wrong committed by the servants of the Crown for which the defendant is liable under the
Crown Liability and Proceedings Act
[see footnote 7]. Since I find no such wrong, there is, in my opinion, no liability of the defendant for any harm or loss experienced by Olympia after its records were seized and prosecution commenced.
