In
Nelles v. Ontario et al.
[see footnote 8], the Supreme Court of Canada confirmed that, in addition to proving damages, a plaintiff must prove four necessary elements to maintain a successful action for malicious prosecution. Two of those elements are not here in dispute, that is that the proceedings were initiated by the defendant, and that they terminated in favour of the plaintiff. Here it is conceded that the second element is established by the stay of the criminal proceedings. The two other necessary elements are in dispute. The defendant urges that the plaintiffs have not proven either the absence of reasonable and probable cause for bringing the action, or malice, i.e., a primary purpose other than that of carrying the law into effect.
