In
Francoeur
, the court dismissed the plaintiffs' claim for abuse of authority, concluding the tort was not established. The customs officer had the authority and a duty, pursuant to the
Customs Act
, to seize the forfeited goods and therefore did not act outside of his authority. Moreover, there was no element of malice proven. Rather, the official's motivation for seizure were his reasonable grounds for believing that there was a statutory violation, and there was no evidence of an improper motive.
