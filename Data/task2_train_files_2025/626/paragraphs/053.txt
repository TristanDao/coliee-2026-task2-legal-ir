In
Hijos (Jose Pereira E) S.A. et al. v. Canada (Attorney General)
[see footnote 9], in accord with the decision in
Nelles
, I struck out an allegation of malicious prosecution because the plaintiff did not plead the absence of reasonable and probable cause for bringing the action. Even if it were pleaded, the tort could not have been established because during the disputed incident, the defendant acted pursuant to the
Coastal Fisheries Protection Regulations
[see footnote 10], legislation that had not been found invalid.
