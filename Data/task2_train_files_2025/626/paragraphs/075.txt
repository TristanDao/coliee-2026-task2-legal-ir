In
Reynen v. Canada et al.
[see footnote 17] the motions judge struck out a claim for negligence in a statement of claim alleging negligence by Customs and R.C.M.P. officers, comparing them by analogy to prosecutors who owe no duty of care to an accused. The Federal Court of Appeal reversed the ruling in that respect but recognized, albeit as obiter, the immunity of Crown prosecutors against claims for negligence in the conduct of a prosecution. Here, while the plaintiffs allege negligence on the part of Ms. Woolcott, no facts are pleaded and none were established in evidence as a basis to find that a duty of care was owed to the plaintiffs, or that any duty of care owed to them was breached. There is no evidence of negligence by prosecutor Woolcott.
