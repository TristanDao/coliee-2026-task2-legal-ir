The law also limits the interests protected under s. 7, which protects an individual's physical liberty rather than her or his economic liberty. In
MacPhee et al. v. Pulpwood Marketing Board (N.S.) et al.
[see footnote 25], the Nova Scotia Court of Appeal held that s. 7 did not apply to economic or proprietary interests. In
Reference Re Section 94(2) of the Motor Vehicle Act (B.C.)
[see footnote 26] Lamer, J. (as he then was), found that a law imposing merely a fine rather than imprisonment was not subject to s. 7 scrutiny because it does not deprive an offender of liberty.
