The federal sales tax under the
Act
at all material times was a tax assessed against and payable by manufacturers in relation to their sales. The tax system depended upon voluntary compliance. The calculation of sales and of the taxes owing, as well as accounting for components on which tax was already paid, and for freight and installation costs paid by a manufacturer, a licensee under the
Act
, was the taxpayer's responsibility. A manufacturer was required to submit, monthly, a federal sales tax ("FST") return reporting its taxable sales for the preceding month and the tax payable on those sales. Failure to report taxable sales or FST owed constituted an offence under the
Act
.
