Ms. David says that in 1982 she or her staff telephoned Revenue Canada with inquiries regarding the appropriate installation deduction rate for its products, including new products it had introduced. She testified that she was busy in Calgary that year, and that representatives from Revenue Canada, some time late in 1982, told her to file "nil returns" pending the determination of the deduction values. However, the individuals who Ms. David alleged told her to file nil returns each testified that they had not done so or they would not advise a taxpayer to file nil returns unless they had no sales.
