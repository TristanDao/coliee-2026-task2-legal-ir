The evidence here does not support a claim for abuse of authority. The claim is based on alleged improper conduct of Mr. Schwantes. There is not evidence to prove that Mr. Schwantes acted maliciously or took action without authority. He acted pursuant to the
Department of National Revenue Act
[see footnote 16] and the
Excise Tax Act
. Mr. Schwantes' testimony at trial was that he had reasonable grounds to conduct the search and seizure and reasonable and probable grounds to believe that the plaintiffs had violated the
Excise Tax Act
. In my view, those conclusions, which I have found were reasonable in the circumstances, rather than any malice, motivated Mr. Schwantes' behaviour.
