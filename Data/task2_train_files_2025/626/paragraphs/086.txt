Section 7 provides:
"Everyone has the right to life, liberty and security of the person and the right not to be deprived thereof except in accordance with the principles of fundamental justice."
Section 7 is the first and most general of the "legal rights" protected by the
Charter
. The subsequent provisions, up to s. 23, address specific deprivations of the right to life, liberty and security of the person enunciated in s. 7. [see footnote 23]
