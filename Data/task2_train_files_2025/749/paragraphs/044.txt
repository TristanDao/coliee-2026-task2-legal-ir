More recently the question was considered by Jerome, A.C.J., in
Teknion Furniture Systems et al. v. Artopex Inc.
(1992), 56 F.T.R. 204; 44 C.P.R.(3d) 504 (T.D.). In
Teknion
the defendants wanted to know whether the plaintiffs were claiming damages based on reasonable royalty, loss of sales or any category of damages. The defendants also made the same request with respect to the plaintiff's claim for accounting of the defendant's profits, as well as the claim for exemplary and punitive damages. Jerome, A.C.J., indicated at p. 506 that he was not prepared to grant the defendant's request for particulars relating to the plaintiff's claim for damages. In his view, the pleadings met the requirements as set out in
Canadian Olympic Assn. v. National Gym Clothing Ltd.
and concluded at p. 507:
"After reviewing the statement of claim, including the prayer for relief, I am satisfied that the defendant does not require any further explanation of what damages are being claimed. In my opinion, it has everything it possibly needs to know at this stage of the proceeding for the purposes of pleading."
