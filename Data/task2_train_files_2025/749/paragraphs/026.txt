The defendants request information regarding the nature of the goodwill depreciated; when the depreciation of the value of goodwill took place; how the depreciation occurred and where the depreciation occurred, in Canada and/or elsewhere. In support of their claim that the defendants require this information, counsel submitted the case of
American Optical Corp. v. Allergan Inc.
(1985), 3 C.P.R.(3d) 229 (F.C.T.D.).
American Optical
deals with an application pursuant to Rule 419 to strike out a paragraph of the Statement of Claim alleging the defendants depreciated the value of goodwill attaching to American Optical registered trademarks. It did not deal with an application for particulars. In
American Optical
, Dube, J., noted there was no allegation in the Statement of Claim that the defendant used American Optical's registered trademarks. The allegation was to the effect that the defendant used another mark which was alleged to be confusingly similar to American Optical's marks. Dube, J., noted there could only be a cause of action under s. 22(1) of the
Trade-marks Act
when it is alleged that the defendant used the plaintiffs' registered mark and concluded at p. 231 "that, in the absence of any material facts showing a cause of action under s. 22(1), the allegation relating to that section must be struck out." Therefore, in
American Optical
the allegation was struck out not because the plaintiff failed to allege the nature of the goodwill depreciated or when the value of the goodwill was depreciated or even how it was depreciated, but because there was no allegation in the Statement of Claim the defendant ever used American Optical's trademarks. The application before me deals with particulars, not striking out an allegation in the Statement of Claim; the plaintiffs' have alleged use of their trademarks by the defendants and as such,
American Optical
is not applicable to the motion before me.
