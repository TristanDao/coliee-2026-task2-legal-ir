In light of the comments in
United States Natural Resources
and
Telemedia
that in situations of continuous infringement a defendant is taken to know what business it carried on in Canada, what products and equipment it sold and under what trade, it is my view that in the application before me the defendants are taken to know what they are doing, including if there has been a request to cease doing something. Further, as noted by counsel for the plaintiffs, if the defendants have no knowledge of the request to cease they merely have to plead in the Statement of Defence that they have no knowledge or deny they have any knowledge. As such, I agree with the plaintiffs' position and no particulars with respect to para. 38 are required.
