The defendants also filed an amended application to Rules 408, 419 and 420.1 to strike out certain paragraphs of the Statement of Claim. The defendants argued that the allegations in the Statement of Claim relating to the issues of trade dress are a matter of civil law and are not within the jurisdiction of Federal Court. The defendants submit that the amended Statement of Claim contains no allegation that the trade dress of the products allegedly sold by the defendants in any way infringed a trademark and therefore the paragraphs dealing with trade dress should be struck out. The issue of jurisdiction was resolved by the plaintiffs agreeing that the trade dress would be considered a trademark.
