Abbott told us he was 28 years old and had been in and out of prison for various offences since he was 16. He was an excellent witness, seemed quite bright and was for the most part credible. His knowledge or remembrance of the facts was understandably clearer than that of other witnesses because he was the central character and lived each incident as described by him and other witnesses. His recollections had flaws, vagueness and errors as might be expected almost eight years after the events but most of his evidence rang true.
