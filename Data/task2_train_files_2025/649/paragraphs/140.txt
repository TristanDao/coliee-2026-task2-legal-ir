Moreover, Decker's action would have been unacceptable outside the penitentiary. In my view, a reasonable and prudent person would not accept the prospect of an officer of the law in a public street firing a shotgun into or near a group of innocent bystanders attempting to apprehend the guilty person who was somewhere in the crowd. Such action would be unacceptable outside the penitentiary and in this situation where there was no riot or insurrection in the penitentiary, it was unacceptable action within the penitentiary. As I indicated earlier, Decker had to know the effect of firing the shotgun because the gun was used specifically for its spread and as such it would be obvious that the two inmates involved in the incident would not have been the only individuals that could have been injured.
