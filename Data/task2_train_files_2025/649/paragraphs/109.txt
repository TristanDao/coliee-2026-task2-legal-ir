At this juncture, we should also note Wileman's comments on the Special Handling Unit:
"A.	The Special Handling Unit is a separate institution of higher security, movement and contact is minimized. The Special Handling Unit is required in order to deal with problematic-type offenders. [That word again - read inmate].
Q.	What level of security classification is the Special Handling Unit?
A.	Well, at the time of these directives, it was considered what we called an S-7, which is Special Handling Unit Maximum Security.
Q.	What level of security classification is Millhaven Institution?
A.	It would have been S-6.
Q.	What level of security classification would Collins Bay Institution have been?
A.	It would have been S-5."
Also the grievance procedure:
"Q.	Could you just briefly explain what the internal grievance procedure is?
A.	Well, the grievance procedure commences in the institution with the complaint which is dealt with within the institution. The second step following that would be what we call a level 2 grievance and that goes outside of the institution to Regional Headquarters. Then each progressive step goes to the next level of authority. Level 3 would be a National Headquarters' issue.
Q.	In what situation might an inmate follow the internal grievance procedure?
A.	Well, in most instances, you can complain and go the grievance procedure on almost any issue. If we were talking in terms of this specific issue, yes, you could, if you desired to do so."
