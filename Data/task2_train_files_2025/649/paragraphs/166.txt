Writing for the Court of Appeal in
LeBar
, MacGuigan, J.A., at page 618 F.C., after citing Lord Devlin in
Broom v. Cassel & Company Ltd.
, [1972] A.C. 1027 (H.L.), noted that "outrageous" or "oppressive conduct" on the part of the Government is quite different from similar conduct by powerful corporations or individuals, and that is much more serious for the servants of the Government are also servants of the people and the use of their power must always be subordinate to their duty of service. Both the Trial Division Judge and the Court of Appeal recognized that "malice is not necessary for the existence of oppressive, arbitrary or unconstitutional action by the servants of the Government".
