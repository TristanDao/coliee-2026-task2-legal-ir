Fontaine was an inmate of Collins Bay at the time of the incident. He was in the pill parade lineup on November 17, 1986, about 10:30 p.m. and at the front of the line. He got his pills, turned around to go back to his cell block 1 on the second floor.
"A.	I turned around and I was leaving. I think I was maybe ten feet from the barrier. Two guys came running up the strip. I should say, maybe one guy towards me and there was another guy on the other side of the corridor and I didn't see, but the guy running towards me had a knife in his hand. The guy running towards me.
Q.	Who was that?
A.	I think his name was Chater. He had a knife in his hand, he was running towards me and he had his head down and he put his head up and said, 'Excuse me Larry'. He knew me. I just watched him go around me and he went at the guard that was at the barrier.
. . . . .
He went around me and I was sort of amazed, what the hell is going on here? I don't know who the other guy was. He was a dark fellow, or a dark-complexioned guy was on the other side.
Q.	Are you talking about the other inmate?
A.	Yes. He was on the other side of the strip. Then there is another guard on that side; right?
. . . .
Q.	What happened next, after what you have described?
A.	They were sort of slashing knives at the guards, yelling at them. Nothing physical was really happening. The guards were backed up and yelling at the guard. Like, there is a tower, what we call a tower there, a control booth. There was a guard in there and they were yelling at him."
And later, back to the inmates:
"A.	The nurse is on that side, this side and we were on the other side and they were yelling for the guard in the tower to open up that barrier.
Q.	When you say they were yelling, who are you referring to?
A.	The two guys with the knives.
Q.	Was there anyone else causing a disturbance, other than these two inmates?
A.	No."
He also confirmed there were all kinds of people on the strip.
