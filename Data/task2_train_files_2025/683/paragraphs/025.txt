There are several flaws in Novozymes’ argument. First, it incorrectly assumes that this additional evidence was considered but not ruled upon by the Board in reaching its decision, and that it can be used to support or justify the Board’s decision on other grounds. The record before me shows clearly – whether the Board was procedurally permitted to do so or not – that the Board effectively rendered its decision in two stages, and that it in fact concluded that the other prior art did not render the claims unpatentable.
