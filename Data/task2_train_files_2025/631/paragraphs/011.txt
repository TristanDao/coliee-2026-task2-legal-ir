That case was not decided in the context of the tripartite test for a stay. Although the discretion of a removals officer under section 48 of IRPA is narrow, it has been established that factors outside travel arrangements may be relevant, such as school years (Wang v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) [2001] 3 F.C. 682). In Simoës v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) (2000), 187 F.T.R. 219, Nadon J. as he then was, stated that a removals officer may consider "pending H & C applications which were brought on a timely basis but have yet to be resolved due to backlogs in the system."
