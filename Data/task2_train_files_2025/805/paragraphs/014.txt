There does not appear to be any additional inculpatory evidence in this case that would support a conclusion, beyond a reasonable doubt, that the Applicant had control or knowledge of the contraband. In
Smith v. Canada (Attorney General)
, 282 F.T.R. 91, the Court found that the applicant did not have exclusive opportunity to possess the contraband and he presented plausible alternatives to explain its presence in his cell. In the present case, the evidence shows that the Applicant had only recently occupied the cell, after the prior occupation of someone else with a similar name, to whom the Offence Notice was delivered in place of the Applicant.
