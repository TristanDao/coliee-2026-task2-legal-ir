According to the recent decision of the Supreme Court of Canada in
Dunsmuir v. New Brunswick
, 2008 SCC 9, decisions of administrative decision-makers are to be reviewed on either the standard of correctness or that of reasonableness. The principal issue in this case is one of mixed fact and law, involving the assessment of the evidence in light of the relevant statutory provisions. According to the decision in
Dunsmuir
at para. 53, the standard of reasonableness will apply. The standard of correctness will apply to any breach of procedural fairness.
