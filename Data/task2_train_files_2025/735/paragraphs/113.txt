In
Katz v. Vancouver Stock Exchange
, supra, the British Columbia Court of Appeal considered whether there was a reasonable apprehension of bias on the part of a hearing panel appointed by the Vancouver Stock Exchange. In determining that there was no reasonable apprehension of bias, the court considered the legislation and bylaws of the Exchange, as well as the practice. The court also noted that the self-regulatory function of the hearing panels appointed by the Exchange was not analogous to the adjudication function of the property assessment tribunals in
Canadian Pacific Ltd. v. Matsqui Indian Band
, supra.
