In
2747-3174 Québec Inc. v. Régie des permis d'alcool du Québec et autres
, [1996] 3 S.C.R. 919; 205 N.R. 1; 140 D.L.R.(4th) 577, the Supreme Court of Canada considered whether the tribunal which revoked the appellant's liquor licence was institutionally independent and impartial. At the outset of his reasons, Gonthier, J., writing for the majority, noted that the case underscored "... the need to reconcile the imperatives of administrative convenience with the principles of impartiality and independence, which cannot readily be reconciled" (page 9).
