In relation to the question of institutional independence, Lamer, C.J., confirmed that the principles developed in
R. v. Valente
, supra, in the context of a s. 11(d)
Charter
challenge, applied to administrative tribunals. He stated as follows, at page 48:
"I begin my analysis of the institutional independence issue by observing that the ruling of this court in
Valente
, supra, provides guidance in assessing the independence of an administrative tribunal. There, Le Dain, J., considered whether provincial court judges were independent. He pointed to three facts which must be satisfied in order for independence to be established: security of tenure, security of remuneration and administrative control."
