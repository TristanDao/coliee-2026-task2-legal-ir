In further describing the concept of institutional impartiality, Lamer, C.J., noted at page 140 that "... whether or not any particular judge harboured pre-conceived ideas or biases, if the system is structured in such a way as to create a reasonable apprehension of bias on an institutional level, the requirement of impartiality is not met. As the court stated in
