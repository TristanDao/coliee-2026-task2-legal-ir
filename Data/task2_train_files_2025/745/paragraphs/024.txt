I also find that the IAD did not err in its determination of the Applicant's establishment in Canada. The IAD accepted the Applicant's evidence regarding her steady employment, her involvement in the community, and that her Canadian friendships demonstrated some degree of establishment in Canada. However, the IAD found that the Applicant's degree of establishment and the difficulty she would face if returned to India were not sufficient to justify an exemption to the Applicant from the provisions of the Act. This finding was not unreasonable and therefore does not constitute a reviewable error.
