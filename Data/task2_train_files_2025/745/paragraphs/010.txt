Although the Applicant has framed the issues in this case as ones of procedural fairness (arguing that the IAD ignored or misconstrued evidence before it) I find that her submissions challenge the IAD's findings of fact. It is well-settled that the factual findings of the IAD in relation to a discretionary decision under subsection 61(1) of the Act are to be afforded significant deference by the reviewing Court. The Court will not interfere with the IAD's Decision as long as it has exercised its discretion in good faith and without regard to extraneous or irrelevant considerations (
Chang v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, 2006 FC 157 at para. 21).
