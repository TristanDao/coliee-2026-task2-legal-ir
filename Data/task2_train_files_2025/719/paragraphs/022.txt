In my opinion, this argument must fail. Subsection 220(2.01) of the
Act
grants the Minister the power to delegate to an officer or class of officers the authority to exercise the powers and perform the duties of the Minister under subsection 220(3.1). It must be recalled that IC 92-2 provides information and guidelines and, moreover, paragraph 14, cited above, does not say "shall", but rather "may". Exhibit C to the affidavit of Donald Voth (Applicant's Record, tab 2, pp. 13-14) is the letter by which the applicant requested a waiver of interest. Nowhere in that letter did the applicant specifically request that a director of a district office or taxation centre review the situation. The review by Ms. Bemister, the Chief of Appeals, was appropriate. It cannot be implied from the letter by which the applicant requested a waiver of interest that the applicant requested the director of the office of taxation to make the review.
