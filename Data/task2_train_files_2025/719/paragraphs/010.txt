The
Income Tax Act
provides no process or factors that must be followed or considered by the Minister in the course of rendering a decision under subsection 220(3.1). However, information circular 92-2 sets out guidelines, with examples of circumstances where cancelling or waiving interest may be warranted. Paragraph 3 of the guidelines emphasizes that these are only guidelines and are not meant to be exhaustive. Paragraph 5 lists examples of extraordinary circumstances, beyond the taxpayer's control, where interest may be cancelled or waived. Paragraph 6 lists instances where the interest arose primarily because of the actions of the CCRA. Finally, paragraph 7 lists examples where there is an inability to pay on the taxpayer's part. Again, the examples given in IC 92-2 are not meant to be exhaustive.
