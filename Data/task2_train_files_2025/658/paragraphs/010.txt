Counsel for Mr. Ates submits that the Board member got her law wrong; that she so intermingled the concept of "balance of probabilities" with the concept of "mere possibilities", that she wrongly held that the test was that the objective fear of persecution must be established on a balance of probabilities. Although both terms are mentioned more than once in the same paragraphs, she concluded by saying:
"I find that there is not more than a mere possibility that the claimant, Erkan Ates, would face persecution should he return to Turkey by reason of any Convention ground."
