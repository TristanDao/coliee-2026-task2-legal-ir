She got her law right. Although any applicant must prove his or her case on the balance of probabilities, the "fear" portion thereof is reduced to a "mere possibility". As MacGuigan, J.A., speaking for the Court of Appeal in
Adjei v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1989] 2 F.C. 680; 132 N.R. 24 (F.C.A.), said at page 682:
"It was common ground that the objective test is not so stringent as to require a probability of persecution. In other words, although an applicant has to establish his case on a balance of probabilities, he does not nevertheless have to prove that persecution would be more likely than not."
