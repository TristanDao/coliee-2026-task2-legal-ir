In this case the Superintendent delegated his investigatory and reporting role to the SDA. The report became the basis for the hearing conducted by the Superintendent who, on the evidence presented by the SDA's report and the testimony and evidence of the trustees, reached decisions on the merits and ultimately on appropriate penalties. The Superintendent participated in one phase only and the various roles for which the office was responsible were not improperly confused. There was no inherent bias in the process. Moreover, the process was not criminal in nature and it did not lead to deprivation of basic rights or liberty. It was concerned with assessing and regulating the practices of licensed trustees. In my opinion, the structural framework established by the statute, as it was applied in this case did not constitute a breach of the appropriate standard of fairness.
