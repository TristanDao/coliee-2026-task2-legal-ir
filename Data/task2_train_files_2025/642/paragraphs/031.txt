In review of those decisions the Court owes deference to the conclusions reached by the Superintendent. Unless they are clearly unreasonable in light of the evidence considered by the Superintendent, the Court will not disturb his decisions on the merits and on penalties. That is particularly the case where in the course of a hearing the Superintendent "is not bound by any legal or technical rules of evidence" (the Act, subsection 14.02(2)(b)).
