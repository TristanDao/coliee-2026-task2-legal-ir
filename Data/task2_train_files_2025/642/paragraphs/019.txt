By his decision the Honourable Fred Kaufman, acknowledging that he was dealing with a different case than this, found that delayed disclosure, and some uncertainty whether all relevant information had been disclosed, put in question the integrity of the process in regard to the second report and so too the right and ability of the trustees to make full answer and defence in regard to that report. He found the wording of the Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act authorized him to grant a stay, and he stayed "the proceedings initiated against [the trustees'] by the Senior Discipline Analyst".
