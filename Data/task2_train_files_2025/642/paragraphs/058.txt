In my view, none of these claims raises a serious issue about the penalty decision. Even if A's conduct had played a major role in the administration of the estates in question, an inference the Court is invited to draw, there is no evidence, and none was considered by the Superintendent to provide a basis for intervention by the Court. The conduct of the trustees in the course of the investigation is no more than would be expected of a licensed trustee. There is simply no basis for finding the penalty decision, made within the exercise of statutory discretion vested in the Superintendent, was unreasonable.
