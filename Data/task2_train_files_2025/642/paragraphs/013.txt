As for the failure to disclose the correspondence from the second corporate trustee, the Superintendent's decision found that the SDA had failed to meet appropriate standards for disclosing information that could have been relevant and of interest to the applicant trustees. Nevertheless, that "information would not itself have produced any different result, and in the circumstances of the case, on a balance of probabilities there was no reasonable possibility that the trustees were denied the opportunity to present a full answer and defence or that their right to a fair hearing was violated as a result of the failure to disclose the other trustee firm's report."
