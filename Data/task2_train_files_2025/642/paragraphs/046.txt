Their concerns are based on principles concerning disclosure that have evolved in criminal law, now established by the decision in R. v. Stinchcombe, [1991] 3 S.C.R. 326 and the jurisprudence flowing from that. The proceedings here do not result in criminal penalties, they did not result in deprivation of the professional standing of a licensed trustee though the terms of their licenses were varied. I am not persuaded that in these disciplinary proceedings, to review the practices of licenses, criminal law standards for disclosure are necessary or appropriate.
