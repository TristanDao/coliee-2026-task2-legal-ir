As to the Minister's contention that Vlug had an obligation owed to the Minister to notify the company, I reject that proposition. Vlug has his first and primary obligation to his client and cannot owe any such duty to a party adverse in interest to his client. The Minister's argument would erode the nature of the solicitor-client relationship. Any legitimate limitation on this aspect of the solicitor-client relationship is covered by ETA subsection 293(15) under which the solicitor is merely required to advise the Minister of the client's last known address. (I have assumed, without deciding, that this subsection is valid).
