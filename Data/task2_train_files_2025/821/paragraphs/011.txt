In a decision dated October 20, 1998, the applicant's grievance was denied. In the reasons for decision, the adjudicator addressed some but not all of the issues and objections raised by the applicant during the hearing. He did address the amendments that the applicant had sought to adduce in respect of the relief sought, describing the $10,000,000 as a "contradiction" and "a ridiculous abuse of the grievance process". He rejected all of the proposed amendments on the basis of
Burchill v. Canada (At­torney General)
, [1981] 1 F.C. 109; 37 N.R. 530 (F.C.A.). He also rejected the applicant's claim that the Ministry had breached his
Charter
rights, writing (ap­plicant's record at 63):
"However, the grievor has failed to satisfy me on the evidence that in terminating his employment the employer engaged in any violation of either the
CHRA
or the
Char­ter
."
