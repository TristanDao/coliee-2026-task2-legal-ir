The applicant submits that many of the adjudicator's actions "were biased against me and were prejudicial to my case" (paragraph 149 of the applicant's memorandum). This court understands this as a reference to an apprehension by the applicant that the ad­judicator was personally biased against him. This must be distinguished from the institu­tional bias which the applicant's written submissions do not support.
