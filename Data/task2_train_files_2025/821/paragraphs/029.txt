The adjudicator's written reasons reveal little about how he accommodated the ap­plicant's needs. They do imply that two requests for adjournments were rejected and that the third day of hearings was held, as scheduled, despite that the applicant ob­jected. The court can find no fault, however, with these decisions. The reasons also reveal that the applicant's oral representations, made in lieu of written submissions (due to his disabilities), were accepted by the ad­judicator despite objections from counsel for the Ministry.
