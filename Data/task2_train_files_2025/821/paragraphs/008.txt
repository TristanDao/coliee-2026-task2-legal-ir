During the hearing, counsel for the Ministry adduced over thirty exhibits. The applicant objected on various grounds to most, if not all of them, being entered but could not persuade the adjudicator to refuse any of them. One of these pieces of evidence was a letter written by the applicant, a copy of which was given to him by counsel for the Ministry just prior to the first day of hearings. The adjudicator entered the letter, stating that it was clearly relevant. Another piece of evidence consisted of a Canadian Human Rights Commission complaint form completed earlier by the applicant and making allegations concerning the Ministry. The adjudicator entered the complaint form and stated that he "would weigh it ac­cordingly".
