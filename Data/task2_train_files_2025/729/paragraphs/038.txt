The most authoritative source with respect to procedural representations about inland humanitarian and compassionate applications is the immigration policy manual, IP 5, entitled "Immigration Applications in Canada made on Humanitarian or Compassionate Grounds." The manual does not represent that applicants will be asked to provide updated submissions. Section 5.26 instructs officers that they are not required to elicit information on humanitarian and compassionate factors and that the onus is on the applicants to put forth factors that they feel exist in their case. That instruction should not lead an applicant to believe that he or she will be contacted and asked for further or updated information.
