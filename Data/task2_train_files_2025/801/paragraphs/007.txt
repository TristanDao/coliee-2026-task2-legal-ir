For the respondent it was urged that the applicant here did not act within the 15 day limitation period to take either of the steps provided in s. 49(1)(c)(i), i.e., to file an application for leave to commence a judicial review proceeding or to signify in writing to an immigration officer an intention to file such an application, that section was not applicable to this case. The phrase "until the application for leave has been heard and disposed of or the time normally limited for filing an application for leave has elapsed", including alternative modifying conditions, is applicable to either course of action set out in the introductory phrase of s. 49(1)(c)(i). Thus, the applicant who files his application for leave out of time is not entitled to the benefit of the statutory stay even if the application includes a motion for an extension of time.
