Even though a late application requires an application for an extension of time to commence the process, determination of whether the extension sought shall be granted is a preliminary question clearly intended by Parliament to be determined by a judge in light of the requirement for special reasons in s. 82.1(5). Under the court's
Immigration Rules
, rules 6(1) and (2) provides that a request for an extension of time under s. 82.1(5) shall be included in the application for leave and for judicial review. While s. 82.1(5) speaks of "an extended time for filing and serving an application ... for leave to commence an application for judicial review", the extension of time cannot, in my view, formally relate to the filing of an application for leave and for judicial review for that latter application is filed in conjunction with the application for an extension of time. Rather, it relates to the necessity for a judge to be satisfied that "special reasons" warrant commencement of the proceedings even though they were not initiated by filing the necessary application within the time limited by statute.
