The facts of the applicant's situation are these. He learned on April 11, 1994 of the C.R.D.D.'s negative decision in regard to his claim to be a Convention refugee. Counsel who had represented him at the C.R.D.D. hearing with support of Alberta Legal Aid was not authorized under the legal aid plan to represent him in further proceedings at that stage and declined to assist in seeking judicial review then. The applicant's affidavit sets out, in part, as follows:
"5. As I did not have funds to retain counsel privately for any appeal I attended at the Registry of the Federal Court in Calgary and obtained as much information as possible in order to commence application for judicial review, however, on 2nd May 1994 I was arrested and detained by the Immigration authorities and have been in custody continuously since that date.
"6. Upon arrival at the Calgary Remand Centre where I am being held, I contacted the representative of the Legal Aid Society of Alberta who requested counsel to provide a legal opinion for them on my case. Mr. Charles Darwent, a barrister and solicitor, attended at the Remand Centre to interview me for the first time on 9th May 1994 and I was advised that I could still apply for leave and judicial review in the Federal Court even though 15 days had passed since I received the decision, by including an application for an extension of time. Counsel was successful in obtaining emergency cover through the Legal Aid Society of Alberta on 10th May 1994 and my notice of application for leave and for judicial review and for extension of time was filed by him on 10th May 1994.
"7. At a detention review hearing in Calgary on Wednesday, 11th May 1994 the case presenting officer advised the adjudicator that my removal from Canada would be effected on Friday 13th May 1994, notwithstanding the fact that I have applied for judicial review in the Federal Court."
