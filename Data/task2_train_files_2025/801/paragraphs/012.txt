Counsel for both parties were agreed that the issue involved in this case was a serious issue of general importance, of concern to those responsible for administration of the
Immigration Act
and to those whose claims to be Convention refugees are denied. Counsel were also agreed that the issue may warrant more extensive consideration and argument than was possible in preparation for and in argument during a telephone conference hearing conducted on an urgent basis before scheduled removal of the applicant from Canada the following day. It was agreed by counsel, and the court was persuaded that the question, earlier set out in these reasons, should be certified in accord with s. 83(1) of the
Act
, for consideration by the Court of Appeal, if the respondent sees fit to appeal my determination.
Order accordingly.
