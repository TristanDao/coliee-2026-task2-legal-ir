Parliament, by enacting s. 82.1(5) has recognized that there may be special cases which warrant proceedings for leave and for judicial review to commence, even though the applicant has not met the time ordinarily limited for filing. In my view, it would be inconsistent to construe s. 49(1)(c)(i) as not providing for a stay of execution of a removal order, once an application is before the court by filing, pending determination of whether special reasons for an extension warrant commencement of proceedings. Otherwise, even the most deserving of cases which may be initiated by late filing with an application for an extension of time would not have the benefit of the statutory stay under s. 49(1)(c)(i).
