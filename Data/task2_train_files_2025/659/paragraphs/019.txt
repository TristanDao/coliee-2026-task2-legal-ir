I simply am not satisfied that amendments to the
Act
made since the
Mossop
decision justify, of themselves, a variation in the standard or standards of review enunciated by the Supreme Court of Canada in that decision. In the foregoing quotation from the reasons of the then Chief Justice in
Mossop
, the "superior expertise" of a human rights Tribunal relating to fact-finding and adjudication in a human rights context was acknowledged. The limitation on "superior expertise" was stated to be to "general questions of law" involving "concepts of statutory interpretation and general legal reasoning". I find nothing on the face of recent amendments to the
Act
which would lead me to the conclusion that the Tribunal and its members are now more competent to perform the latter function than members of the judiciary.
