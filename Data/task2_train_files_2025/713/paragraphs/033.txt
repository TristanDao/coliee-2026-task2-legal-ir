However, I have determined that the board did not provide an explanation for concluding that Mr. Han was complicit in these abuses. As I stated earlier, the RPD did not provide a full assessment of Mr. Han's assertion that he had no knowledge of these abuses. For the RPD to conclude that an applicant has been complicit in human rights abuses, it must find personal and knowing participation. In Sivakumar, the court acknowledged that a position of authority alone is not sufficient to meet this standard. Therefore, Mr. Han's oversight of the camps is not, in my view, a sufficient basis for a finding of complicity. Absent adequate reasons from the board to support its finding that Mr. Han had knowledge of the abuses in the camps, its finding of complicity in this respect cannot be sustained.
