Additionally, the RPD found that Mr. Han had continued in his position within the regime and had accepted a number of promotions. The consequences of failing to obey orders, or of refusing a promotion, might have included being discredited or dismissed. Nonetheless, the board determined that these consequences were far less serious than the abuses committed by the regime. Regarding Mr. Han's oppositional activities, the RPD concluded that they were essentially unrelated activities and did not reflect a planned attempt to reform the Chinese penal system. It noted that Mr. Han left China and his position only when he felt personally at risk. Despite his ideological concerns, he had continued to support the regime until 2000.
