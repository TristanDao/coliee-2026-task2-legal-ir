It is trite law that the Board is entitled to choose, in context, the evidence that is more fitting to the particularities of each given case. It is not up to the Applicant, nor the Court (
Starcevic v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, 2008 FC 1370 at par. 18) to reweigh the evidence or otherwise dictate the elements to which the Board should have attributed more weight
