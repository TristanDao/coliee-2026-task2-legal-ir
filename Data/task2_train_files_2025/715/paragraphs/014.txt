Contrarily to the applicant's contention that the Board did not consider the totality of the evidence before it when it decided as it did, it is evident from the reasons of its decision that it did recognized the many incidents that the applicant experienced while residing in the Czech Republic. But in considering such, the Board was enabled to conclude that the applicant's evidence was not only inconclusive but it could not establish a link between the fear of persecution and one of the five grounds of the Convention refugee definition.
