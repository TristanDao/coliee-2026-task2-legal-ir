The applicant had to produce concrete evidence of nothing less than the existence of such adverse conditions which would jeopardize his life and safety in relocating to a safe area, as suggested by the Board (
Ranganathan v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2001] 2 F.C. 164 (C.A.)). The onus of proof rested at all time on the applicant to show that it would be objectively unreasonable for him to reside in Prague. Unfortunately for him, the applicant has not discharged this onus and as a result was unable to convince the Board of the absence of a flight alternative in his own country. Moreover, he failed to show this Court that the Board ignored or misconstrued any evidence in this regard or misapplied the legal test in its IFA analysis or made any perverse or capricious findings in this regard. As a consequence, the intervention of this Court is not warranted.
