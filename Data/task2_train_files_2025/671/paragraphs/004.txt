The decision of the designated member to extend time and to grant leave is highly discretionary. The Applicant submits that, in spite of this discretionary nature of the decision, I should apply a standard of review of reasonableness simpliciter (Callihoo v. Canada, [2000] F.C.J. No. 612 (T.D.)). In this case, I do not need to reach a conclusion on the appropriate standard since I am satisfied that, on any standard of review, the decision should not stand.
