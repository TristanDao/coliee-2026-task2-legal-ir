To rebut the presumption of state protection, an applicant must present clear and convincing proof of the state's inability to protect. The evidence must be relevant and reliable, and convince the trier of fact that the state protection is inadequate (
Ward
;
Carrillo v. Canada (M.C.I.)
, 2008 FCA 94, [2008] 4 F.C.R. 636).
