The panel is in the best position to assess the explanations submitted by applicants for any perceived inconsistencies and implausibilities, and the role of this Court is not to substitute its judgment for the panel's on findings of fact relating to the applicant's credibility (
Singh v. Canada (M.C.I.)
, 2006 FC 181, 146 A.C.W.S. (3d) 325 at para. 36;
Mavi v. Canada (M.C.I.)
, [2001] F.C.J. No. 1 (QL)). Here, the applicant's explanations for the lack of evidence corroborating his narrative are not reasonable.
