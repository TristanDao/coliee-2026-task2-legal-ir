On this basis, it was open to the panel to draw a negative inference, first, regarding the applicant's credibility, because of the lack of corroborating evidence and, second, because of the lack of reasonable and credible explanations for his failure to produce that evidence. It is trite law that the panel may make adverse findings based on the fact that applicants failed to produce evidence corroborating their testimony or make any efforts to obtain such corroborating documentation (
Muthiyansa v. Canada (M.C.I.)
