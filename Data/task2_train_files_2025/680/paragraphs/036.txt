Returning to the particular issue of interest on costs, it is noted that the provisions of s. 34 of the Expropriation Act of Ontario, R.S.O 1980, c. 148, as regulated by s. 1, R.R.O 1980/317, provide for taxation of costs as between a solicitor and his own client. The case of WMI Management v. Metropolitan Toronto (1981), 34 O.R.(2d) 708, seems to decide that no interest is payable on assessed costs. On the other hand, the decision in Stanton v. Board of Education for Borough of Scarborough (No. 3) (1983), 26 L.C.R. 292, indicates that interest charged on the unpaid account of an appraiser may be allowable when the expropriated party was not put in sufficient funds to pay the account on time and when the expropriated party is under an obligation to pay.
