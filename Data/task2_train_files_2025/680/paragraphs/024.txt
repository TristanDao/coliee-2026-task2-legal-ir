This is why I should hesitate, for purposes of the issues before me, to endorse wholeheartedly what has been said by this court that s. 27 costs are costs between a solicitor and his own client whereas costs under s. 36 or under the order of Rouleau, J., are solicitor-client costs. In both cases, the Crown pays the bill. In both cases, as will be seen on reading s. 27 and on reading the order of Rouleau, J., only those legal, appraisal and other costs reasonably incurred in asserting a claim for compensation are taxable.
