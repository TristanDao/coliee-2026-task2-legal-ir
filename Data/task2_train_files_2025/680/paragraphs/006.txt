In disposing of the interest charged by the appraisers Strung Real Estate Limited, the learned Prothonotary had this to say:
"Interest has been claimed on the amounts charged for appraisal. The Minutes of Settlement provide for interest. That, however, is an agreement to which the defendant was not a party. Section 27 of the Expropriation Act indicates that costs incurred before the commencement of an action are payable in any event. Whereas section 36 indicates that costs of the action are in the discretion of the court. While section 27(2) indicates that costs under that section may be determined by taxation I do not take that to mean that the monies eventually found to be payable on a taxation were not payable at an earlier time. The compensation eventually found to be payable bears interest from an earlier date. I therefore conclude that the account for appraisal services rendered before action commenced should bear interest from the date the account was rendered at a rate determined in accordance with section 33. With regard to the portion of the appraisal fees incurred after institution of the action under section 29 these fees are not payable by the defendant until they have been taxed."
