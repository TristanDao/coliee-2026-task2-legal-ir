Recent developments, I might observe, indicate a more bottom-line approach to services, no matter what might be the occupation involved. Increasingly, the cost of money is reflected in these services, with the cost of money being equated with the assumed return on the same money which the debtor enjoys while his bills pile up. It will be noted, for example, that interest on solicitors' accounts are provided in s. 35 of the Solictors' Act in Ontario. I might add to this that the section also provides that on taxation of a solicitor's bill, the taxing officer, when he considers it just to do so, may disallow the interest or fix a rate which is less than the maximum rate authorized.
