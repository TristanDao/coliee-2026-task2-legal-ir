The author also refers to various attempts to codify costs or to classify them according to abstract scales. He cites at pages 1-4 the case of Re Solicitors, [1967] 2 O.R. 137 (H.C.J.), at pages 139-140, as follows:
"There are three modes or scales of taxation of costs.
1. Party and party.
2. As between solicitor and client:
(i) where payable by another party or out of a fund of other parties
(ii) where payable out of a fund in which the party taxing his costs has a common interest with other parties.
3. As between solicitor and client and payable out of a fund belonging to the party whose bill is being taxed or payable by a client to a solicitor who is taxing his bill to the client.
"An award of costs in mode 3 is usually distinguished by the words 'as between a solicitor and his client' whereas the words are 'as between solicitor and client' in the case of mode 2."
