This is not to suggest that a taxing officer's discretion is beyond appeal or review. His discretion must still be exercised judiciously. In that respect, an aggrieved party can enjoin the court either by way of review or by way of appeal (see rules 346(2) and 349(4) of the Rules of this court), to find that the exercise of that discretion has been founded on improper grounds or that the allowances or disallowances are "so unreasonable as to suggest that an error of principle must have been the cause".
