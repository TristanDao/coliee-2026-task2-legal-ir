The learned Prothonotary allowed interest charges accruing prior to the commencement of proceedings. His refusal to allow interest subsequent to these proceedings appears to be grounded on the principle that no monies are owing until they are taxed and that consequently, no interest on these costs is chargeable until they have been fixed. Although that principle might apply in some taxation proceedings, it may be argued that it does not apply when dealing with expropriation proceedings under the federal statute. It may be argued that the rationale behind the Ontario experience should be equally applicable in expropriation matters under the federal statute.
