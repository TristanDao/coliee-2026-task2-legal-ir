I should permit myself to make a final observation. If s. 27 of the Expropriation Act charges the Crown with costs up to the commencement of the action and provides that these costs are payable when claimed or taxed, it might be prudent for owners' counsel to engage that procedure without waiting for trial or final judgment. It would considerably simplify the taxing officer's role in his assessment and reduce the field of enquiry in which he must become engaged.
