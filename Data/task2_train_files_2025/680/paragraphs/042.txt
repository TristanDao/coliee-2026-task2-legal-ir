There is left to consider interest accruing on these some s. 27 costs and which are later to be taxed under s. 36. I have mentioned before that under s. 27, costs are payable forthwith, the owner requiring neither an order nor a judgment of the court to have them taxed should the owner be unable to reach agreement on them with the Crown. These costs are in effect a statutory debt of the Crown. It might be argued that interest charges otherwise chargeable should continue to accrue until the debt is paid. It could also be argued with equal force that should the Crown be obliged, ready and able to pay these costs immediately upon the commencement of the proceedings, it should not be charged on accruals if the owner should not proceed in a timely manner.
