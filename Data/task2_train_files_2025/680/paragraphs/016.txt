Costs of proceedings under s. 36 are another matter. These costs must necessarily be limited to what the order of the court might be in any particular case and the court has discretion under s. 36(2) to determine whether party and party costs or solicitor and client costs are to be awarded. In my mind, this is the only difference between the provisions of s. 27 and s. 28 and the only reason why the owner's right to costs is found in two different provisions.
