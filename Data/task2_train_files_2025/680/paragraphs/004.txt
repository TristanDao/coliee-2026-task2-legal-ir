As will be noted from the style of cause, the events which led to the Associate Senior Prothonotary's order on taxation began with an expropriation notice by the Crown which was confirmed on May 31, 1973, more than fourteen years ago. The forcible taking was of several leasehold interests situated on the Toronto waterfront. As will also be noted from the court's record, the case over the intervening years had a somewhat checkered career. There were numerous interlocutory proceedings. There was the setting down and hearing of a special case. There was an appeal therefrom to the Federal Court of Appeal as well as a number of applications to fix a date for trial. The actions were eventually set down to be heard consecutively on November 19, 1985, at which time the parties amicably settled their differences. It was on that date that Rouleau, J., endorsed a formal judgment awarding a compensation amount to all the plaintiffs in the lump sum of $659,000 and signed his order for costs to which I have already referred.
