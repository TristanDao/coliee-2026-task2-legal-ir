Consonant with the observations I have previously made and with the conclusions I have previously drawn, I must perforce return the matter to the learned Prothonotary for purposes of disposing of the interest charges above-mentioned in accordance with the provisions of s. 27 of the Act and of the order of Rouleau, J. All these charges are, in my view, subject to the "reasonableness" test and to which a taxing officer must exercise his discretion. In so doing, the taxing officer may consider all of the several factors which enabled him to arrive at the basic quantum of the costs on which the interest charges are calculated. More specifically, however, he will have to consider:
(1) the reasonableness of the interest charges whether accruing prior or subsequent to the commencement of the action;
(2) the obligation of the owner to pay these charges;
(3) whether the interest charges are in accordance with the creditor's usual charges to his customers or clients;
(4) whether the interest charged complies with the provisions of the Interest Act;
(5) whether, in all circumstances of the case, the owner proceeded in a timely manner to have his s. 27 costs taxed and paid;
(6) whether or not, by reason of any partial payment on account of compensation, the owner would have been expected to pay these accounts either when they fell due or at a date earlier than the taxation date.
(7) any other matter relevant to the issue.
