I must therefore conclude that under either s. 27 or s. 36, there is no legal impediment to the taxation of interest charges on costs. It should not matter in principle whether such interest charges accrued prior or subsequent to the institution of an action under s. 29. In my respectful view, the indicia which a taxing officer must apply to these interest charges are no different than those applicable to any of the other several items of costs disclosed in a bill. If a taxing officer should have discretion to reduce an item of costs as being unreasonably high, or to tax it off altogether because it was irrelevant or superfluous, or because it had nothing to do with "asserting a claim for compensation (s. 27) or was not a cost "incident to the proceedings" (s. 36) or to the terms of any judge's order in that respect. I see no reason to limit a taxing officer's discretion in the matter of interest charges.
