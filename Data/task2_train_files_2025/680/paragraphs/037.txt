From the many cases referred to in Orkin's The Law of Costs, supra, especially at pages 2-82 et seq., it appears that only certain costs incurred in compensation proceedings are allowed. Other costs incurred in resisting an impending expropriation, in attacking the validity of the expropriation or in transferring title from a deceased owner to his estate and other similar matters have been refused. The list is certainly not exhaustive but it does point out that the discretion of the taxing officer is paramount in deciding which particular items of cost are properly included under the aegis of "reasonable legal, appraisal and other costs actually incurred by the owner for the purposes of determining the compensation payable". It also indicates that where s. 36 of the federal statute speaks of solicitor-client costs, and the provincial statute refers to costs as between a solicitor and his own client, I fail to see where the enquiry conducted under either is in any way different or is subject to different considerations.
