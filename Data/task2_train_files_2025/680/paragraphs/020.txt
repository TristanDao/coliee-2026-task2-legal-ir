Yet, the author shows remarkable caution. He must also cite at pages 1-5, Singer v. Singer (1975), 11 O.R.(2d) 234, where the taxing officer breaks the categories down from three to two and concludes at page 243:
"Party-and-party costs awarded on the solicitor-and-client scale, or to be taxed on the solicitor-and-client basis, are intended to result in full indemnity to the beneficiary of the award in respect of his legal costs, that is, fees and disbursements, excluding only the costs incurred in respect of services not reasonably necessary to permit him to fully and fairly prosecute or defend the action. ..."
