The argument supporting the learned Prothonotary's position to allow interest under s. 27, i.e. prior to the institution of proceedings, is that it reflects the obvious intention of the legislator to fully indemnify the owner for the costs he has incurred. If certain of these costs bear interest and it is established, in accordance with the Stanton case, supra, that the cost itself, the interest rate charged and the owner's obligation to pay interest are established, there should be no reason in law to disallow it. I see no reason why the same principle should not apply to s. 36 costs.
