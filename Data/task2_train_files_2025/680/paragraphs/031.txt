In his reasons for order, the learned Prothonotary determined that the interest rate allowable under a s. 27 taxation should be in accordance with s. 33 of the statute which fixes a "basic rate" of interest on what is substantially the yield on Treasury Bills. It will be noted, however, that s. 33(2) limits the application of this basic rate to interest on "the compensation", and not on costs. It is difficult on reading the Prothonotary's reasons to discern whether he felt bound in law to apply this basic rate, or whether, in the exercise of his discretion to determine the reasonableness of the rate charged, he might not have simply concluded that what was good enough as interest on compensation was good enough as interest on costs. I will come back to this later in these reasons.
