I can only conclude that the intent of the legislation is to wholly indemnify an expropriated party for costs incurred prior to the commencement of proceedings. Section 27 charges the Crown with these costs, the role of the taxing officer being limited to testing their reasonableness and their relevancy. It would perhaps be deemed unreasonable for a party to charge to the Crown the cost of six appraisal reports when two would obviously have been sufficient. Otherwise it would leave an expropriated party in possession of a Crown cheque made out in blank.
