The test of "reasonableness" under either section provides the taxing officer with sufficient discretion to deal one way or the other with difficult cases. He might find that the cost itself is unreasonable or that the service performed is abusive, or that the interest charged is too high or that the owner has no legal obligation to pay it because it does not conform to s. 4 of the Interest Act, R.S.C. 1970, c. I-18. Finally, the taxing officer might find that an amount of partial compensation paid to an owner was more than sufficient to enable him to pay his accounts when they became due.
