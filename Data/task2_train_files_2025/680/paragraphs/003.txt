I should, as a preamble, observe that the court's jurisdiction to intervene in a taxing officer's decision does not extend so far as to enable the court to substitute its own judgment on the facts for that of the taxing officer's. Any taxed item should not likely be interfered with if it only touches upon quantum but does not involve a question of principle unless of course, in the words of Cattanach, J., in Camp Robin Hood Ltd. v. The Queen (1981), 21 L.C.R. 219, at 223:
"... the amount can be shown to be so unreasonable as to suggest that an error in principle must have been the cause."
