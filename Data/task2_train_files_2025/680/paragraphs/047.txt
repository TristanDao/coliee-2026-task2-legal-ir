It will be noted that the court has full discretionary power over the award of costs (rule 344). In the exercise of this discretion, rule 344(3) lists some sixteen indicia which a court may consider, the last of them being "any other matter relevant to the question of costs". There is a similar kind of discretion given to the court in awarding prejudgment interest and in setting the rate. The same rule should apply to the taxing of "reasonable costs" to include any interest-added charges.
