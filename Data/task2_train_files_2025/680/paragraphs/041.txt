All of these, in my view, are only a few of the many elements which a taxing officer might consider in order to fulfil his discretionary role in applying the "reasonableness" rule. In that respect, a court should refrain from applying legal rules with respect to any of them and should be loath to fetter the taxing officer's discretion. After all, the essential function of the taxing officer is to reimburse the owner for all of his costs. Yet, he must necessarily keep in mind that the authority charged with these costs has not been privy to when, why and with whom they were incurred in the first place. This requires, in my view, a judgment call by the taxing officer and that should, in most cases, be the end of the matter.
