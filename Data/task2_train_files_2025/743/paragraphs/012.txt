Moreover, I am not persuaded that there is evidence before me of irreparable harm if the adult applicant is removed from Canada. Her expressed fear of persecution if she were returned to Pakistan is related principally to the situation of her husband as she perceives it in that country. In her own case the credible basis tribunal found there was no credible basis to her claim to Convention refugee status and a similar finding was made in the case of her husband's claim. At this stage mere assertion of a fear of persecution on her part cannot be considered to constitute irreparable harm. The inconvenience to the family, the reliance of the younger son, now some nine years of age, upon the care of his mother because of his health circumstances, described by counsel as an asthmatic condition, are not issues in this case which, in my view, establish irreparable harm. The inconvenience and disruption that may result from separation of the spouses, and one of them from their children depending on whether the children leave or remain in Canada, are not, on the evidence as it is before me in the affidavits, irreparable harm.
