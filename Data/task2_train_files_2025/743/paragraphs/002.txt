The applicants are a mother and her two infant children. They are all citizens of Pakistan who, after their arrival in Canada, claimed to be Convention refugees. On September 4, 1991 a first-level panel consisting of an adjudicator and a member of the Convention Refugee Determination Division, after a hearing concerning the claim of the applicants, determined they did not have a credible basis to their claim for Convention refugee status. On that same day the adjudicator determined that a deportation order should be made against the applicant, Nasreen Naseem, the adult applicant, the mother, but not against her minor children.
