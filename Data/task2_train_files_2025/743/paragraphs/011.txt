In my view, the applicant here fails to meet the requirements outlined in
Toth
for a stay. There is no serious issue yet before the court for the applicants' application for leave and for judicial review is itself subject to an application for an extension of time to apply. While some explanation is offered for the applicants' delay, within the application for leave and in the affidavit of the husband, Syed Mohammed Nasim, the court does not at this stage have the benefit of representations by the respondent on the question of delay and cannot be satisfied that there is yet any satisfactory explanation of this that would warrant leave to extend the time to proceed. The suggestion of the applicants' counsel that the husband's application for leave and for judicial review also raises serious issues which have not yet been resolved is not a basis on which it can be said there is a serious issue before the court in relation to the applicants' claim. The claim of the husband was dealt with separately from that of the mother and two children of this family and the husband's application for leave and for judicial review is also separate from the application now made by the applicants in this case.
