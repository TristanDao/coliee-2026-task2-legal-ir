In relation to irreparable harm there was before me affidavit evidence as follows. In the affidavit of Mrs. Naseem
"7. I am still fearful for my husband, for my two sons and for myself returning to Pakistan. The situation in Pakistan is extremely unstable and given my husband's background and the problems that my family and I had prior to our departure from Pakistan, I believe that my life and my husband's and two son's lives would be in danger if we return to Pakistan."
In the affidavit of the husband, Mr. Nasim, it is averred
"12. My youngest son is 9½ years old and needs the care and love of his mother.
"13. There would be considerable harm to my children if they are separated from me or from their mother.
"14. There would be considerable inconvenience to deport my wife and children and then to bring them back if my appeal for leave and judicial review is determined positively by the Federal Court."
Counsel argued also that if they are deported, Ministerial approval for return of the applicants to Canada would be required and if that were not readily granted, should the husband's application for judicial review be allowed, it would constitute irreparable harm. Moreover, separation of the mother and wife, with or without her children, from the husband would constitute irreparable harm by reason of separation since all were dependant upon him, and since care of one child, because of health conditions, was a major concern for the family, particularly if separation of the parents were to occur. Furthermore, if the two minor children were allowed to stay in Canada their separation from their mother, by her removal from Canada, would constitute irreparable harm.
