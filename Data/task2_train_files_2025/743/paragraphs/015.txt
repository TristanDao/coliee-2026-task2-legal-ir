Having so concluded, I noted that while it is not within the court's power to direct, I urged counsel for the respondent to raise with senior officers of the respondent the possibility of review of the removal action proposed in light of the family circumstances here involved. It appears that until recently the respondent was content to leave the family as a unit and not to execute the deportation order issued against the adult applicant, seemingly until the situation of the husband and father was resolved by consideration of his application for leave and for judicial review. In light of the importance of maintaining family units under policies adopted within the
Immigration Act
, there was, in my view, merit in further review of the applicants' situation before they were removed from Canada.
Application dismissed.
