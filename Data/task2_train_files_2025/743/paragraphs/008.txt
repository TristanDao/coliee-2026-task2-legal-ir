On behalf of the applicants it was argued that the removal order was invalid insofar as it purported to relate to the infant children of the adult applicant, since they had not been included in the deportation order issued on September 4, 1991. It was urged that the test set out in
Toth v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration)
(1988), 6 Imm. L.R.(2d) 123; 86 N.R. 302 (F.C.A.), was here met. It was said that serious issues are raised by the applicants' application for an extension of time, for leave and for judicial review and that it would be unfair to the applicants to remove them from Canada pending consideration of those issues, despite the delay in their application for leave. Counsel acting for the applicants had not been counsel at the credible basis hearing, indeed he had been retained only on September 6, 1993, and he required tapes or a transcript of that hearing in order to properly prepare an application record in relation to the application for leave. Moreover, it was urged that the husband's application for leave and for judicial review, still pending before the court, also raised serious issues for consideration of the court.
