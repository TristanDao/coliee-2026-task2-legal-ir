The board's decision refers not only to Atyanski, but also to his associates. Thus, it cannot be said that it considered the matter only in the context of Atyanski as a political figure. Mr. Justice Marceau, in Mileva v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration), [1991] 3 F.C. 398 (C.A.), defined "change in circumstances" as significant social or political changes in the country of origin on the day of the decision. Yusuf, supra, and Zdjelar, supra, do not assist Mr. Kudar. In Yusuf, the Court of Appeal held that a changed political situation is only relevant to a claim if it helps to determine, at the date of the hearing, an objective threat of persecution. It is a matter to be determined factually.
