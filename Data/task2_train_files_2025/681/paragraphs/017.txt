In addressing her mind to the question of whether or not the respondent's termination of employment was by reason of a discontinuance of function, it was incumbent on the Adjudicator to determine what his duties were at the time. As noted in a quotation from the Adjudicator's preliminary decision on that issue, she determined that even the respondent's job title was at issue, and that the evidence did not establish whether a position description has ever been developed or not. She noted that though a position description was not necessarily determinative of the respondent's duty, office, function, set or bundle of tasks, the failure to establish his job title gave rise "... to the inference of a fundamental lack of information on the part of the witnesses ..." as to what the respondent did in the last position that he held before his employment was terminated. I am satisfied that the same failure must logically give rise to an inference of a fundamental lack of information on the part of the witnesses who appeared before the Adjudicator as to whether the respondent was a manager.
