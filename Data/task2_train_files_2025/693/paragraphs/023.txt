To obtain an extension of time, a party must satisfy the four part conjunctive test set out in
Canada (Attorney General) v Hennelly
, [1999] FCJ No 846 (FCA) by demonstrating: (1) a continuing intention to pursue the application; (2) that the application has some merit; (3) that no prejudice to the other party arises as a result of the delay; and (4) that a reasonable explanation for the delay exists.
