It is clear from the refusal letter that the visa officer relied upon her conversation with the peon as one of the reasons for her decision. The content of that conversation was not put to the applicant and the failure to do so, in accordance with the above noted authorities, constitutes a breach of procedural fairness. It may be that the officer would have come to the same conclusion in any event, but it is not evident or certain that such would be the case. Therefore, it cannot be said that this is one of those infrequent instances in which the breach was immaterial. Thus, the application for judicial review will be allowed. Counsel posed no question for certification. This matter raises no serious issue of general importance.
