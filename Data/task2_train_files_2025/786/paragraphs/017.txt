Relying on
Dizon v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2002] F.T.R. Uned. 58; [2002] F.C.J. No. 135 (T.D.), and
Seepersaud v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2001] F.T.R. Uned. 612; [2001] F.C.J. No. 1316 (T.D.), the respondent submits that the determination of whether a person has performed the duties of his intended occupation is a pure question of fact and subject to the standard set out in section 18.1(4)(d) of the
Federal Court Act
, R.S.C. 1985, c. F-7. Moreover, visa officers should be afforded deference in determining whether an applicant satisfied the requirements of a particular occupation:
Madan v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1999), 172 F.T.R. 262 (T.D.).
