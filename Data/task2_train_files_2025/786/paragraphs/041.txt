Finally, I am not persuaded by the applicant's arguments in relation to the technical questions asked by the officer during the interview. In my view, the visa officer was entitled to evaluate his responses to questions related to his field of employment. The six information technology questions asked by the officer were designed to test the applicant's knowledge and skills in his intended occupations. In his affidavit, the applicant claims that he was able to answer these questions with no difficulty whatsoever. However, according to the visa officer, although the applicant answered all six questions, he did not correctly answer four of the six. In my view, the duty of fairness did not require that the visa officer inform the applicant of her view that he had answered these technical questions incorrectly. The applicant should have reasonably expected that in such a testing situation, his answers would be scrutinized and determined to be right or wrong by the officer.
