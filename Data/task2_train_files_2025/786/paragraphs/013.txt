The applicant also submits that it was perverse for the visa officer to conclude that he had demonstrated no experience in his chosen occupations when she awarded him the requisite points under the Education and Training Factor ("ETF"). Awarding him the requisite units under this factor is consistent with a finding that he met the employment requirements of his intended occupations:
Dauz v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1999), 173 F.T.R. 288 (T.D.);
Osman v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(2000), 181 F.T.R. 304 (T.D.), and
Liu v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2000] F.C.J. No. 1408; 182 F.T.R. 251 (T.D.). The applicant further argues that it was incumbent on the visa officer to assess his experience in each of the various responsibilities in his occupation in order to award units of assessment for experience and the officer erred by failing to do so:
Hajariwala v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1989] 2 F.C. 79; 23 F.T.R. 241 (T.D.).
