To demonstrate that the Board's credibility findings were patently unreasonable, the applicants' must show that the decision was "clearly irrational". In other words, a "decision that is patently unreasonable is so flawed that no amount of curial deference can justify letting it stand": Law Society of New Brunswick v. Ryan, [2003] 1 S.C.R. 247 at para. 52.
