The applicant suggests that this error is fatal, citing Kitoko v. Canada (Citizenship and Immigration), [2004] F.C.J. No. 1819 (F.C.)(QL). However, in my view, Kitoko, supra does not stand for this proposition. There were several problems with the Board's decision in that case in addition to the failure to disclose its reliance on specialized knowledge, and MacTavish J. specifically concluded that the Board's decision could not stand because of the "cumulative effect of the errors": Kitoko, supra at para. 27.
