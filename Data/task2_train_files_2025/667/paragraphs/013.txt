I do accept, however, that this Court does have an inherent jurisdiction to control its own process, including the authority to strike out applications for judicial review in limited circumstances. That point is made in the David Bull decision where it was said that such cases will be "very exceptional" and do not include arguments about "the adequacy of the allegations in the notice of motion". This latter ruling disposes of the CRA's contention that McKellar's Notice of Application in this proceeding is insufficiently framed to support the proceeding: also see David Bull, above, at paragraph 15.
