While it is beyond dispute that the decision-maker has no obligation to list each and every piece of evidence brought before him or her, the decision-making process must encompass a fair and full review of all relevant considerations–and the reasons must reflect this. It is certainly within the purview of the officer to accept or reject information, but he or she cannot ignore information. If a particular document or piece of information is rejected, the applicant should be advised of the reasons why, especially when the document or information supports the applicant's position. In such an instance, the officer must, at the very least, comment on the document or information.
