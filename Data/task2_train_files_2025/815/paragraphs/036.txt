Persons not named in an injunction are not liable to be committed for breach of it, unless, with knowledge of the injunction, they chose to interfere, step into the place of the person who is named, and commit the act enjoined, in which case they are liable for contempt of Court (DeCosmos v. Victoria and Esquimault Telephone Co. Ltd. (1894), 3 B.C.R. 347 (Q.C.).
