It is my view that the law is settled that the doing of the act which is alleged to constitute contempt must be deliberate or willful, as opposed to casual, accidental or unintentional (Browne v. Britnell & Co. (1924), 27 O.W.N. 232). It must be done knowingly, that is, with full knowledge of the existence and terms of the injunction issued.
