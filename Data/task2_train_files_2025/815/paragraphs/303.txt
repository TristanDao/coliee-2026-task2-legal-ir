C.D. Jackson in Enforcement of Maritime Claims London Lloyds' of London Press Ltd. 1985, in speaking of "Mareva" injunctions states the following, which I believe applies in the present case, at page 196:
"It is clear that, whatever the relationship with the defendant, a third party, who knowingly acts contrary to an injunction directed at the defendant is liable for contempt of Court. The injunction, therefore, imposes a considerable burden on those who have little connection with the dispute and no responsibility for its existence."
