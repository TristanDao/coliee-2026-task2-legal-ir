In the case of Davis v. Barlow (1910), 15 W.L.R. 49; 20 Man. R. 158 (K.B.), it was said that any person, whether an agent or not, who, knowing of the injunction, aids and abets the party enjoined is liable to be committed, not for breach of the injunction, but for contempt of Court tending to obstruct the course of justice.
