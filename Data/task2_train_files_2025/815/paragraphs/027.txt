In the case Imperial Oil Ltd. v. Tanguay, [1971] Que. C.A. 109, which dealt with a show cause contempt proceedings as here, the Court held that it should be
satisfied beyond all reasonable doubt
that the provisions in the injunction had been violated. The case of Air Canada et al. v. Canadian Air Line Pilots Association (1977), 77 C.L.L.C. 14,076 (F.C.T.D.), confirms, at pages 103-4, that the same standard of proof is required when appearing before the Federal Court on a show cause hearing for contempt of Court pursuant to Rule 355.
