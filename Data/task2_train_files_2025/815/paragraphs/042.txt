The case of Lord Wellesley v. Earl of Morrington, 50 E.R. 785, is authority (English) for the proposition that one who willfully assists in a breach of the writ or order of the Court, or in defeating its purpose, or in preventing its execution, with knowledge of such writ or order, is, although not specifically within the terms of the writ or order, subject to liability for contempt of Court.
