The RPD rejected the Applicants' evidence of persecution, and in the course of doing so, made the following implausibility finding:
The principal claimant also told us that he was receiving very threatening phone calls. The first one would have started in October 1999, and according to him "it did not stop until July 2000". He added that he would receive about "3, 4, 5 phone calls a week", and that he asked his children not to answer the phone. He also said that the persons calling were threatening him of death, as well as his family. Notwithstanding all those threatening phone calls, the principal claimant and his family continue to live in the same house, moving only in July 2000. Also, he told us that he was afraid for his life and his family's life since July 2000. However, he continues to live in Peru up to November 2001, 16 months after starting to be so scared for his life and his family's.
All of this is not compatible with the behaviour of somebody who fears for his life and his family's life or who would be afraid to be subjected to a risk of cruel and unusual punishment if sent back to his country.
[Emphasis added]
(Tribunal Record, p. 9)
