When reviewing administrative decisions, the reasons provided are not to be read microscopically (
Boulis v. Canada (Minister of Manpower and Immigration)
, [1974] S.R.R. 875 at 885). The decision must be assessed as a whole and within the context of the evidence (
Miranda v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration)
, [1993] 63 F.T.R. 80, para. 3).
