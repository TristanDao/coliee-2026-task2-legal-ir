In
Kim v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2005] FC 437, [2005] F.C.J. No. 540 (QL), at paras. 8-22, after conducting a pragmatic and functional analysis, Mosley J. affirmed that "in the judicial review of PRRA decisions the appropriate standard of review for questions of fact should generally be patent unreasonableness, for questions of mixed law and fact, reasonableness
simpliciter
, and for questions of law, correctness."
