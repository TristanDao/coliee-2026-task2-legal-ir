The panel then considered an alternative argument, that is, if the 15 November panel had determined that it had no jurisdiction, the 29 May panel would have to re-open that decision in any event in order to examine the legal effect of the 10 May decision. In the result, the panel found that, since the applicant was accorded no natural justice in the rendering of the 15 November panel's decision, the proceedings were a nullity. The CRDD stated that it found the ruling of the 15 November panel "fatally flawed" by virtue of, inter alia, procedural ambiguity.
