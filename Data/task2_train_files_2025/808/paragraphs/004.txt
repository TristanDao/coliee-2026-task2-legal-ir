The Applicant worked on ships as a marine engineer for the government owned Vietnamese Ocean Shipping Company (VOSCO). In April 2001 he was contracted to a Japanese company NISHO. In early September 2001, while docked in Vancouver, the Applicant jumped ship to stay in Canada. Around the same time, the Applicant allegedly learned through a phone conversation with his brother that the Hai Phong government had ordered the Applicant's arrest in August for his role in the land dispute. In addition, VOSCO was now demanding the Applicant's wife pay a US $15,000 fine, allegedly levied against the company by the government of Canada when the Applicant jumped ship. The Applicant was also told he could expect to go to jail for betraying the country.
