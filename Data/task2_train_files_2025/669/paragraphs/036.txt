I disagree with the WCC's argument that the addition of features not detailed in the trade-mark registration to the HID-A-BAG containers detract from the mark's distinctiveness. When dealing with the question of distinctiveness, the test is the message given to the public:
White Consolidated Industries Inc. v. Beam of Canada Inc.
(1991), 47 F.T.R. 172; 39 C.P.R.(3d) 94, at p. 111 (F.C.T.D.). The addition of ornamental or functional features such as those identified by WCC should not negate the distinctiveness of the mark as long as the message given to the public remains clear. The features of the distinguishing guise highlighted in the trade-mark registration at issue remained easily identifiable in the respondent's HID-A-BAG containers even with the addition of other features.
