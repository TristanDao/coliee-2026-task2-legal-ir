WCC's final submission is a novel one that seeks to bar parties from obtaining a distinguishing guise trade-mark if the same design has previously been the subject of an industrial design registration. WCC argues that implicit in the grant of the 10-year monopoly under the
Industrial Design Act
is a promise to dedicate the design to the public following the expiry the design registration. The application to register a design as a distinguishing guise after it has been dedicated to the public is contrary to the legislative intent for the grant of a limited monopoly.
