In expungement proceedings before the Federal Court, there is an onus upon the party attacking the registration to show that it should be expunged. There is a corresponding presumption that the mark in question is valid, and if doubt exists then that doubt must be resolved in favour of the validity of the trade mark. See
Mr. P's Mastertune Ignition Services Ltd. v. Tune Masters
(1984), 82 C.P.R.(2d) 128, at p. 134 (F.C.T.D.);
Unitel Communications Inc. v. Bell Canada
(1995), 92 F.T.R. 161; 61 C.P.R.(3d) 12, at p. 27 (T.D.);
General Motors du Canada et al. v. Décarie Motors Inc. et al.
, [2001] 1 F.C. 665; 264 N.R. 69; 9 C.P.R.(4th) 368 (F.C.A.), at para. 31 (F.C.A.); and
Consorzio Del Prosciutto Di Parma v. Maple Leaf Meats Inc.
, [2001] 2 F.C. 536; 205 F.T.R. 176, at paras. 25 and 35 (T.D.), affd. (2002), 297 N.R. 135; 18 C.P.R.(4th) 414 (F.C.A.). The applicants have raised five grounds for expungement and I will deal each of the grounds for the benefit of the parties and, in the event of an appeal, the Court of Appeal.
