It is apparent that the error made by the respondent was an innocent mistake and not a wilful misrepresentation. Further, the error contained in the respondent's application does not fall within the category identified by Gibson, J., in
Unitel Communications
. That it was Haul-All Equipment Systems and not Haul-All Equipment Ltd. using the trade-mark between 1983 and 1985 is not fundamental to the registration of the trade-mark. Unlike
Standard Brands Ltd. v. Staley
(1947), 6 C.P.R. 27 (Ex. Ct.), which was cited by Gibson, J., at pages 53-54, this is not a situation where the respondent could only obtain the registration by making the false statement. The application was made in 1995 and the fact that it was supported by only 10 years as opposed to 12 years of exclusive use by the respondent would not have prevented the trade-mark's registration.
