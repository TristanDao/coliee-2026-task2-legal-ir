In its second submission, the applicant argues that the adjudicator lacked jurisdiction to rule on the cause of the respondent's disability, because s. 349 of the
Act respecting industrial accidents and occupational diseases
, R.S.Q., c. A-3.001, provides that the Commission de la santé et de la sécurité du travail has exclusive jurisdiction. The applicant also relies on the employment contract between the parties, in particular article 4.2, which states:
[TRANSLATION]
"4.2 In the event that one of the situations described below occurs, situations that the Employer recognizes as constituting serious grounds, the Employer may, at its discretion, terminate this agreement, effective immediately, without further notice or delay, subject to the application of the
Canada Labour Code
, where applicable:
. . . . .
(b) the Employee becomes permanently unable to perform his obligations as set out in this agreement because of illness or physical disability."
