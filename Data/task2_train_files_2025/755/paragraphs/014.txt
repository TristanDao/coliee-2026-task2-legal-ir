In this case, the applicant's first submission is that there is an apprehension of bias with respect to the adjudicator, which is a breach of a principle of natural justice and therefore goes to his jurisdiction. The applicant maintains that the fact that the adjudicator represents unions and employees in labour relations and employment law at the same time as he exercises his quasi-judicial functions gives rise to a reasonable apprehension of bias and of dependence.
