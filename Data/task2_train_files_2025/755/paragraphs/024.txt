Secondly, not only is article 4.2 of the employment contract between the parties subject to the
Code
, but an employer cannot hide behind such a clause in an employment contract to disguise a dismissal, which is the case where the evidence establishes that the employer itself has caused the situation that allows the employer to terminate the employment contract pursuant to that provision. In my view, by virtue of his powers under paragraph 242(3)(a) of the
Code
, the adjudicator was completely justified in making the ruling he did with respect to the cause of the respondent's disability. The following passage from the decision of Adjudicator DeBlois, at page 6, exhibits reasoning on this issue that I find completely fair and equitable:
[TRANSLATION]
"In fact, if the evidence were to establish that the employer is the cause of the situation that led to the disability and that the employer then decides to terminate the contract, specifically alleging the employee's disability, that would be a dismissal that is unjustified in the circumstances.
"It is therefore in this context that the undersigned adjudicator must determine whether the employer is really the reason for the complainant's inability to perform his duties, bearing in mind that, according to all the evidence, the employee was in fact disabled at the time when the employer gave him the notice of termination of employment. (P-1)"
