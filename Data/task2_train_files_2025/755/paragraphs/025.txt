Lastly, the final submission of the applicant concerns the adjudicator's assessment of the facts. In such a situation, this court cannot substitute its own assessment of the facts for that of the decision maker where, as here, a party challenging its decision fails to prove (1) that it is based on an erroneous finding of fact that it made in a perverse or capricious manner or without regard for the material before it (paragraph 18.1(4)(d) of the
Federal Court Act
, R.S.C. 1985, c. F-7) and moreover, (2) that the assessment of the facts by the adjudicator is patently unreasonable (see
Lamontagne
, supra, and
Nation Maliseet à Tobique
, supra).
