In the context of findings of fact, the Court has held that the discretionary decision of a visa officer should be accorded the highest level of deference, patent unreasonableness: Shi v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), 2005 FC 1224, [2005] F.C.J. No. 1490 at para. 3 (QL) [Shi] ; Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) v. Navarrete, 2006 FC 691, [2006] F.C.J. No. 878 at para 17 (QL). On this standard, the Court should not intervene unless it can be shown that the visa officer ignored relevant evidence or relied on irrelevant or extraneous considerations: Shi, above at para. 3.
