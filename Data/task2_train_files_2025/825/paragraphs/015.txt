In addition, where notice of a given concern only materializes during the interview itself, the visa officer must allow the applicant a reasonable period of time in which to meaningfully respond to the allegations: Khwaja, above at para. 17. In such a case, the duty to inform applicants of the case against them will be fulfilled where “the visa officer adopts an appropriate line of questioning or makes reasonable inquiries which give the applicant the opportunity to respond”: Liao v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), [2000] F.C.J. No. 1926 (T.D.) (QL) at para. 17.
