In support of its application the defendant argued that a corporation can only have one place of residence, namely the place where it has its head office, here in Delaware, and that for the purposes of rule 446 it is not relevant that it has a place of business in British Columbia. The plaintiff argued, on the contrary, that it cannot be regarded as a company which is "ordinarily resident out of the jurisdiction" because of its status in British Columbia and the fact that it has done business there for a long time and has assets there. In short, the plaintiff submitted that no evidence had been presented that it ordinarily resides out of the jurisdiction and that in addition the circumstances do not justify the imposition of security for costs.
