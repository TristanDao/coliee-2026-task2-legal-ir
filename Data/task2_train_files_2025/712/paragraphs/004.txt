Counsel for the defendant rested his argument on Quebec precedents regarding the concept of residence of a company. In
Belmoral Mines Ltd. v. The Royal Trust Company
, [1985] R.D.J. 597, the Quebec Court of Appeal said the following:
"A company can have no residence other than its domicile, that is, its head office as fixed by law, although it may have places of business elsewhere:
Canadian Bank of Commerce v. Brouillette
(1925), 39 B.R. 526, 531;
Spot Supermarkets Corp. v. Duro-Test Electric Ltd.
, [1968] B.R. 1003;
Apeco of Canada Ltd. v. République française
, [1977] C.A. 531;
Church of Scientology of St. George v. Société des quotidiens Québécor Inc.
C.A.Q. no. 200-09-000532-78, February 9, 1979 (J.E. 79-246)."
