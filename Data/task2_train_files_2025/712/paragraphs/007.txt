In the case at bar, insofar as the plaintiff is a corporation having its head office in Delaware, even though it is registered as an extra-provincial company in British Columbia, it is still a party which is "ordinarily resident out of the jurisdiction" of this court. However, I consider that the fact that it has long had a place of business in Canada where it maintains employees and that it keeps inventory there and accounts receivable for large sums operates in its favour and constitutes circumstances justifying this court to exempt it from providing security.
