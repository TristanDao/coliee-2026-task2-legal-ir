It is clear from this decision, and from others like it, that a Removal Officer does have a duty to be alive and sensitive to the short-term interests of children facing the removal of a primary caregiver from Canada: also see John v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) [2003] F.C.J. No. 583, 2003 FCT 420. If it is expected that children will remain in Canada, it is imperative to consider the adequacy of the arrangements that have been put in place for their care once the parent has left.
