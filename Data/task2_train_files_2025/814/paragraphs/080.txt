There being no procedural unfairness, that aspect of judicial review must be dismissed. On the question of the reasonableness of the ID’s decision, standing back and reviewing the decision as an organic whole, I am not persuaded that the Applicant has established the conclusions of the ID are unreasonable. The reasons are justifiable, intelligible and transparent. In addition, per Dunsmuir, the decision falls within the range of acceptable, possible outcomes that are defensible on the facts and law discussed above. Therefore, the challenge based on alleged unreasonableness fails.
I should add that at the outset of the hearing, the Applicant indicated one of his tasks was to “change my mind”, a comment made with reference to Gazi. As I said at the time, that is not the issue. Instead, the issue is whether the ID’s decision is reasonable and if the procedures followed were fair in the circumstances of this case. I said that I did not make “global” findings on the determinative issues in Gazi. Likewise, I make none here. This decision is based on the record before this Court.
