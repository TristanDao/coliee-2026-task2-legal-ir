The ID addressed the situation in January 2015:
[68] On the anniversary of the January 2014 elections, the BNP called for yet another hartal, this time an indefinite one, as it continued to demand the reinstallation of the caretaker system. On January 29, 2015, Amnesty International reported that since the BNP had imposed a transport blockade, more than two dozen people had been killed and hundreds injured due to supporters throwing petrol bombs at buses and vehicles. Amnesty urged that the BNP “should exhort their members and supporters to stop these politically-motivated criminal acts ...”. […] By March 10, 2015, the death toll had risen to at least 115 people, 60 of whom had been burned to death.[…] By the beginning of April 2015, the economic loss due to this hartal was estimated at 49 billion taka ($630 million) or 0.6 percent of the country’s gross domestic product.[…] It is unclear from the evidence when the hartal ended, but it lasted at least three months.
