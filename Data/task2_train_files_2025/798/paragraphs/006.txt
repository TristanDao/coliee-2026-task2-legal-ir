The definition of "crimes against humanity" set out in the
London Agreement
reads:
"
namely
, murder, extermination, enslavement, deportation, and other inhumane acts
committed against any civilian population
,
before or during the war
;
or persecutions
on political, racial or religious grounds in execution of or
in connection with any crime within the jurisdiction of the Tribunal
whether or not in violation of the domestic law of the country where perpetrated." (underlining added)
In the
Allied Control Council Law No. 10
, "crimes against humanity" are defined as:
"
Atrocities and offences, including but not limited to
murder, extermination, enslavement, deportation, imprisonment, torture, rape, or other inhumane acts
committed against any civilian population
, or
persecutions
on political, racial or religious grounds whether or not in violation of the domestic laws of the country where perpetrated." (underlining added)
The definition in the
Tokyo Agreement
does not differ from that in the
London Agreement
.
