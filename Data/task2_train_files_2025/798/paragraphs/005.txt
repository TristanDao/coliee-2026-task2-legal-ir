The
London Agreement
defines "war crimes" as "violations of the laws or customs of war", followed by a list of specific examples, i.e., "war crimes" are defined as:
"
namely, violations of the laws or customs of war
. Such violations shall include, but not be limited to, murder, ill-treatment or deportation to slave labour or for any other purpose of civilian population of or in occupied territory, murder or ill-treatment of prisoners of war or persons on the seas, killing of hostages, plunder of public or private property, wanton destruction of cities, towns or villages, or devastation not justified by military necessity." (underlining added)
The
Allied Control Council Law No. 10
and the
Tokyo Agreement
do not add to this definition.
