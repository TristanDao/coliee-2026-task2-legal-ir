There does not appear to be a Supreme Court decision that has interpreted Article 1F(a) of the
Convention
for the purposes of s. 2(1) of the
Immigration Act
. The recent decision in
Pushpanathan v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(1998), 226 N.R. 201 (S.C.C.), however, makes it clear that recourse to the relevant international documents is important when interpreting Art. 1F. That case dealt with a refugee seeker who was allegedly "guilty of acts contrary to the purposes and principles of the United Nations", and who therefore allegedly fell within Art. 1F(c) of the
Convention
.
