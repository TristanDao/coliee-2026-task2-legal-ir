On the other hand, it seems quite clear to me that a decision on the issue of confusion under s. 6(5) cannot be the sum of the individual tests in the paragraphs which follow. The opening words of s. 6(5) speak of "all the surrounding circumstances". The surrounding circumstances are overriding. It is in regard to those circumstances which are not didactically enumerated in the paragraphs that the case before me merits some findings which are not specifically analyzed in the impugned decision.
