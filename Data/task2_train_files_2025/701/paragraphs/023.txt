In any event, I should prefer to balance the weight to be given to the individual tests under the various paragraphs of s. 6(5) of the statute with the more unfettered consideration of all surrounding circumstances. In the result, it might be said to be the application of the well-known aphorism that on the issue of confusion, the general provision in s. 6(5) is greater than the sum of its individual parts.
