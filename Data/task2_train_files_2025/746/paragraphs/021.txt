It is by now well established that the proper standard of review for a PRRA decision, when considered globally and as a whole, is reasonableness:
Figurado v. Canada (Solicitor General)
(2005), 262 F.T.R. 219; 2005 FC 347. While agreeing with this standard, Justice Richard Mosley refined it somewhat in
Kim v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) et al.
(2005), 272 F.T.R. 62; 2005 FC 437. After conducting a pragmatic and functional analysis of the relevant legislative provisions, he concluded, at paragraph 19, that "the appropriate standard of review for questions of fact should generally be patent unreasonableness, for questions of mixed law and fact, reasonableness simpliciter, and for questions of law, correctness."
