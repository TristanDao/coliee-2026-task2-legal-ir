On February 9, 1993, the applicant received a 60-day sentence following his conviction for possession of a narcotic. The additional term was to run consecutively to his nine-year sentence, and, as a result, a new Eligibility Date had to be calculated. The Board set the applicant's new Eligibility Date at July 8, 1994. The Board determined that the new
Act
demanded that it combine the applicant's two sentences to form one aggregate sentence, and then calculate the applicant's new Eligibility Date under the provisions of the new
Act
. The effect of the change was to leave the applicant with an Eligibility Date one year later than it was prior to the imposition of the 60-day sentence.
