On November 1, 1992, the new
Act
came into force in Canada. The new
Act
altered the way in which a sentenced individual's Parole Eligibility Date is calculated. Under the provisions of the
Parole Act
, an individual was eligible for Day Parole after completing one-sixth of a fixed term sentence. Under the new
Act
, an individual becomes eligible for Day Parole only after six months prior to the completion of one-third of the fixed term sentence.
