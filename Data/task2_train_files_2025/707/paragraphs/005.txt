Simpson, J., considered the same issue in
Langard v. National Parole Board
(1993), 70 F.T.R. 140 (
Langard
), and decided to apply the
Parole Act
to the first sentence and the new
Act
to the second sentence, relying on s. 225(1) of the new
Act
. She states at p. 6 of the decision:
"In my view, both the Board and the applicant have given interpretations to sections 225(1) and 139(1) which they cannot bear. The language of section 225(1) is very clear in my view. It is a substantive transitional provision. Parliament clearly intended that the
CCRA
would not have retroactive effect and that sentences imposed under the
Parole Act
regime would, for the purposes of day parole calculations, be treated according to the
Parole Act
formula."
She continues at p. 7:
"In my view, the correct calculation in accordance with the
CCRA
will be achieved if a sentence or sentences imposed under the
Parole Act
have their eligibility dates calculated using the
Parole Act
formula and eligibility dates for sentences imposed under the
CCRA
are calculated using its formula."
