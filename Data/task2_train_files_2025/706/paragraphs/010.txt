I do not share the Ermineskin defen­dants' view that the prothonotary applied the wrong test. Some questions of jurisdiction cannot be easily decided until after all the evidence in a case has been adduced. On other occasions, such questions may easily be decided on a summary motion to strike. The "plain and obvious" test applies to the striking out of pleadings for lack of jurisdic­tion in the same manner as it applies to the striking out of any pleading on the ground that it evinces no reasonable cause of action. The lack of jurisdiction must be "plain and obvious" to justify a striking out of pleadings at this preliminary stage.
