Administrative bodies as such are subject to a duty to act fairly. Obviously, the requirements of fairness must be balanced by the needs of the administrative process in question. The degree and nature of the duty of fairness to be applied by any particular tribunal will vary in accordance with the legislation which created that tribunal, the nature of its authority, the extent of the power exercised by it and the consequences of the exercise of that power upon the individuals affected [see footnote 4].
