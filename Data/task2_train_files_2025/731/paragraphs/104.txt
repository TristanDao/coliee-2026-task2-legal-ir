In
Figliola
, the Supreme Court set out principles governing how one statutory administrative tribunal should exercise its discretion to dismiss a human rights complaint already dealt with by another statutory administrative tribunal. While
Figliola
dealt with the interpretation of paragraph 27(1)(f) of the
BC Human Rights Code
, which does not contain the phrase "vexatious grounds", the Supreme Court's decision provides general principles concerning the prevention of "abuse of the decision-making process" and therefore, could apply more broadly to the decisions of the Commission taken pursuant to paragraph 41(1)(d) of the Act. The phrase "vexatious grounds" in paragraph 41(1)(d) of the Act is, like paragraph 27(1)(f) of the
BC
Human Rights Code
, an amalgamation of principles underlying the common law finality doctrines of
res judicata
, issue estoppel, collateral attack, and abuse of process.
