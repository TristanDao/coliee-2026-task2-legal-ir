The dissenting judges in
Penner
were of the view that
Figliola
moved away from the approach to issue estoppel articulated in
Danyluk
, which had held that a wider discretion to relieve against the application of issue estoppel applied to administrative tribunals than to courts. The dissent held that
Figliola
remains the proper approach for both administrative tribunals and the courts when considering whether issue estoppel applied due to a prior decision of an administrative decision-maker. The dissent is critical of the majority for returning to the
Danyluk
approach and further expanding the consideration of the fairness of the previous decision.
