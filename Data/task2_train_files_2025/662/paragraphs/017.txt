In sum, the Board was not persuaded that the Applicant son-in-law had a well-founded fear of persecution by the FARC. Further, the Board questioned his subjective fear on the basis that the son-in-law had put his visa status in the United States at risk by visiting Catalina in Canada. The Board found that this behaviour belied the Applicant son-in-law's claim of a subjective fear of persecution.
