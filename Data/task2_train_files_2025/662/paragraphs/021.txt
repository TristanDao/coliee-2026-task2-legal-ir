The Applicants submit that the Board erred in applying the test under s. 97(1)(b)(ii) of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act, S.C. 2001, c. 27 which requires that, for a person to be in need of protection, they face a risk that is "not faced generally by other individuals in or from that country." In their submission, the Board erred by equating the phrase "faced generally by other individuals" with "faced generally by all parents." In support, the Applicants cite a decision of the Board where the Board found a claimant couple was at risk of cruel and unusual treatment because of the risk of their children being kidnapped (I.D.Q. (Re), (2002) R.P.D.D. No. 189).
