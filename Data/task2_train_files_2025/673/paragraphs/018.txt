It is clear that if the officer received and acted upon what has been described in some cases as "extrinsic evidence", then failure to draw this evidence to the applicant's attention and affording an opportunity to respond to it would constitute a breach of procedural fairness and give grounds for quashing the decision upon judicial review. This happened in the Ngo case, supra at paragraphs 53 to 59 where the officer apparently had a discussion with the interpreter after the hearing as to the nature of certain documents placed in evidence.
