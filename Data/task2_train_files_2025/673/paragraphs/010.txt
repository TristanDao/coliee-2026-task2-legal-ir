The
first issue
is the degree of deference that should be given to a decision of the Minister's officer in respect of a humanitarian and compassionate application made under section 25(1) of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act, particularly where the application is made from outside Canada and the applicant has never been to Canada. Section 25(1) provides:
25. (1) The Minister shall, upon request of a foreign national who is inadmissible or who does not meet the requirements of this Act, and may, on the Minister's own initiative, examine the circumstances concerning the foreign national and may grant the foreign national permanent resident status or an exemption from any applicable criteria or obligation of this Act if the Minister is of the opinion that it is justified by humanitarian and compassionate considerations relating to them, taking into account the best interests of a child directly affected, or by public policy considerations.
