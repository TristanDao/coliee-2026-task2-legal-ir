It is axiomatic that the first part, the first syllable, of a trademark is the dominant portion [see footnote 4]. Obviously, there is some degree of resemblance between "TRIO" and "TRIUS" as the first three letters are similar. "TRIO" is a known word in the English language, having a specific meaning and a known pronunciation, namely "TREE-O". Conversely, "TRIUS" is a coined word and has no known meaning and no clearly established pronunciation. "TRIUS" could conceivably be pronounced "TRY-US" as suggested in a wine article published in a newspaper filed in evidence, or "TREE-US" as suggested by Andrés.
