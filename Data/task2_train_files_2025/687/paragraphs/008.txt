It is trite law that registration of a trademark confers certain rights and benefits to the owner, including prima facie proof of the rights and ownership of the trademark. Thus, when a trademark is registered, there is a presumption of validity and the party seeking to expunge the registration has the onus of proving the invalidity. If any doubt exists regarding the validity of the trademark registration, the presumption of validity has not been rebutted and the doubt must be resolved in favour of the validity of the trademark registration [see footnote 2].
