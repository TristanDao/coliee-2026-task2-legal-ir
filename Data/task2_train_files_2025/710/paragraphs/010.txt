To qualify as a Convention refugee, claimants must establish that the persecution they fear is linked to one of the reasons listed in the Convention (
Rizkallah v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
(1992), 156 N.R. 1 (F.C.A.)). The Board found that the persecution suffered by the applicants at the hands of Kazakhs was related to organized crime, and not to one of the reasons listed in the Convention.
