Recent indications in the jurisprudence of the Federal Court of Canada show a certain inclination towards the applicability of the Supreme Court's decision in
Corbière
, supra, to custom band elections:
Gros-Louis et al. v. Nation Huronne-Wendat et al.
, [2000] F.T.R. Uned. 920; [2000] F.C.J. No. 1529 (T.D.), Pinard, J., at paragraph 4;
Hall v. Dakota Tipi Indian Band
, [2000] F.T.R. Uned. 209; [2000] F.C.J. No. 207 (T.D.), Pelletier, J. (as he then was), at paragraph 2; and
Gabriel
, supra, Tremblay-Lamer, J., at paragraphs 13-14. However, in none of these decisions did the court engage in any kind of extensive analysis of the "complex" legal issues involved in determining whether
Corbière
, supra, also applied to custom band elections. I think this question is still open to debate and the indications already given by the court are by no means definite.
