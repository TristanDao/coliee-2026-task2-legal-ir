According to the first McDonald affidavit, in the 1960's companies in the forest industry started to develop wafer board. A "wafer" is a flat particle of wood, square in shape, approximately 1 1/2 inches, shaved or cut from a log. Wafer board is manufactured using wafers. Wafers are dried, then a glue-like resin is applied to the wafers and the wafers are randomly spread on a forming line to produce a continuous mat. The mat is pressed and then heat is added to set the resin to form a board. Since the wafers are placed randomly, the board has limited strength for structural purposes in the building industry, for example, wafer board cannot be used in place of plywood for structural applications.
