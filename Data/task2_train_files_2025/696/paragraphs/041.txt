By way of background, I note that the utility of the patent is not an issue in the proceedings before me. I also note that Pallmann improved on its feed system and now uses a feed system which incorporates the hopper in a nonoverhead position. This improved feed system is the subject of Canadian Letters Patent No. 2,015,038, referred to as Pallmann Patent No. 2. Jager states the reason why Pallmann no longer uses the overhead hopper in its usual batch feed system for flakers is that in most cases, the flakers in the batch feeding system are installed in systems that do not have sufficient height to accommodate an overhead hopper.
