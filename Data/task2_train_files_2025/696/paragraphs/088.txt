Counsel for both parties provided a number of dictionary definitions of "hopper".
Webster's Ninth New Collegiate Dictionary
, (Springfield, Merriam-Webster Inc., 1983) defines hopper as "a usu. funnel-shaped for delivering material (as grain or coal); also: of various other receptacles for the temporary storage of material".
Webster's Third International Dictionary of the English Language Unabridged
(Springfield, Merriam-Webster Inc., 1986) defines hopper as a: "chute, box, or receptacle us. funnel-shaped with an opening at the lower part for delivering material (as grain, fuel, or coal)". The
Shorter Oxford English Dictionary
, vol.1 (3rd Ed. Oxford, Clarendon Press) defines hopper as a "receiver like inverted pyramid or cone, through which grain or anything to be ground passes into the mill; so called because it had originally a hopping or shaking motion".
