There is no dispute among the persons skilled in the art that the creation of a proper bundle is an essential element to the operation of the invention. The evidence of both McDonald and Jager is that it is important that a pile the shape and size of the mouth of the flaker, which is parallel along its length and the ends are abutted close to one another, be delivered to the mouth of the flaker. The reason is twofold: in the flaking process it is necessary that logs lie parallel to the knives so when they are cut they are cut along the grain and it is necessary to maximize the flaker so that the compartment is filled.
