OSB is an advancement to wafer board. It is manufactured using "flakes" rather than wafers. OSB is produced using flakes that are cut from wood. The wood is usually in the form of logs. The wood is cut parallel to the grain and the flakes that are produced are flat particles which are longer than they are wide. The width to length ratio is about 1:3. OSB is manufactured in a process similar to the process used in manufacturing wafer board. The significant difference between OSB and wafer board is the use of flakes rather than wafers and the fact the flakes are oriented to each other on the forming line whereas wafers in wafer board are placed randomly. As a result, OSB has significant strength and can be used in place of plywood for structural purposes in the building industry.
