McDonald also comments that in the patent, the words "loading magazine" and "hopper" are used interchangeably. He was unaware of the word "hopper" being used in the OSB Industry. In McDonald's view, the word hopper denotes loading from above, such as a grain hopper or the loading of chips from above. Loading magazine is defined at pages nine and 10 of the patent, by reference to notation 13 on the diagram. Notation 13 refers to a v-shaped overhead device. Figure two and figure three of the patent show different shaped flaps for this overhead v-shaped device. McDonald states that nowhere in the patent is there any reference to or description of any type of compartment where the logs are first bundled together, other than an overhead type of compartment, which is interchangeably referred to as either a "hopper" or a "loading magazine".
