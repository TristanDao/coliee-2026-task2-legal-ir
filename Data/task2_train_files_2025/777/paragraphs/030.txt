Further, the Supreme Court in
Rizzo
, supra, was guided by the provisions of the
Interpretation Act
, R.S.O. 1980, c. 219, in construing a statutory provision. Paragraph 22 of the Court's reasons for decision reads as follows:
"I also rely upon s. 10 of the
Interpretation Act
, R.S.O. 1980, c. 219, which provides that every Act 'shall be deemed to be remedial' and directs that every Act shall 'receive such fair, large and liberal construction and interpretation as will best ensure the attainment of the object of the
Act
according to its true intent, meaning and spirit'."
