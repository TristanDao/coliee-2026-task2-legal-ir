The Applicant submits that the Board erred in determining that it had no jurisdiction under IAD Rule 58 to extend time for filing an appeal when the time for doing so had expired. He relies on
Richardson v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1989] 3 F.C. 47 (F.C.A.), where the Federal Court of Appeal found that the Board had jurisdiction to extend time for the filing of an appeal. Richardson was decided under the
Immigration Act
, 1976 (the
Immigration Act
). The Applicant argues that the powers under that
Act
are similar to those under the current act, the
IRPA
.
