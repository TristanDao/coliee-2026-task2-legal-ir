The
IRPA
expressly provides for a right of appeal against a decision to make a removal order following an examination. There is no such specific provision under the
Immigration Act
. Further, paragraph 24(2((b) of the
Immigration Act
provides that permanent resident status is lost upon the making of the removal order "and such order is not quashed". Under the
IRPA
, permanent resident status is only lost upon the coming into force of the removal order (s. 46(1)(c)) which, in the circumstances of this case, would have occurred when the appeal period expired (s.49(1)(b)).
