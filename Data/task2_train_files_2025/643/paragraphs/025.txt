The omission of an acceptable affidavit is not fatal to the applicant's claim, but following this Court's decision in
Zheng v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, 2005 FC 619, [2005] F.C.J. No. 749 at paragraphs 22 and 23 (QL), the applicant is confined in both written and oral argument, to arguing from the record. Any error asserted by the applicant must appear on the face of the record.
