The Respondents say there is no copyright in facts, regardless of whether they are small or large. The Respondents submit there is no legal basis for the "small facts" theory advanced by the Applicants on the basis of Professor Granatstein's affidavit. They submit that the Applicants' plagiarism arguments do not apply in copyright law because plagiarism protects ideas, while copyright protects only the expression of ideas. According to the Respondents, only originality is protected by copyright, and whether a substantial part of a work has been copied is a determination that must be made "holistically and qualitatively."
