The Applicants' arguments based on differences between "small" and "large" facts, with the former deserving of protection in this case and the latter not so deserving, are without merit. Copyright law recognizes no such difference or distinction. Facts are facts; and no one owns copyright in them no matter what their relative size or significance. Any alleged distinction between small and large facts is an artificial division, one which obscures what is protected by the Applicants' copyright in the Documentary. The particular means, method, and manner the Applicants used to tell Mrs. Halamajowa's story is protected. Their particular phrasing of the words which tell her story is also protected, such that instances of verbatim copying - i.e. transcription of the Documentary - may well constitute copyright infringement.
