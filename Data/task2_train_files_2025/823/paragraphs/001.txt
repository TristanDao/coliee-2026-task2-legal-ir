Boswell, J.
: Francizska Halamajowa and her daughter, Helena, lived in the town of Sokal, Poland, during World War II when the town was occupied by German forces. She harboured and hid three Jewish families for nearly two years as well as a German soldier who had deserted as the war neared an end. Mrs. Halamajowa's story forms the basis of a documentary film, titled
No. 4 Street of Our Lady
, which the Applicants have produced and directed. This documentary is based in part on the diary of Moshe Maltz, who was one of those hidden by Mrs. Halamajowa and was also the grandfather of Judy Maltz, one of the three Applicants in this proceeding.
