The Respondents acknowledge that the Documentary is a dramatic work protected by copyright. However, according to the Respondents, the only similarities between the Book and the Documentary are the facts, and they do not relate to copyrighted expression. The protected expression, the Respondents argue, is the Applicants' skill and judgment used to shoot, compile and arrange the Documentary. Mrs. Halamajowa and the other persons involved in her story are factual, historical persons in respect of whom the Applicants do not have a monopoly.
