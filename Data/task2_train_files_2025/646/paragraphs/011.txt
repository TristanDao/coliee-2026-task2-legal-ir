The applicant argues that the visa officer failed to consider his abilities to speak and write in English, as required under Factor 8 of Schedule I to the
Immigration Regulations
. Notably, the applicant received 6 units for his knowledge of English at the paper screening, but at the actual interview he received only 2 units. According to Factor 8, points must be assessed for each of the abilities described therein (i.e. reading, writing and speaking). The visa officer's failure to consider the applicant's writing and speaking ability in English is allegedly borne out by his interview notes. Given the applicant's educational background, the visa officer should have tested the applicant's ability in these areas. In relation to the affidavit of Michael Boekhoven, the applicant submits that preference should be given to the interview notes, which were much fresher and probably more accurate as compared to Michael Boekhoven's much later affidavit. The applicant's counsel points to his ability to "read" English.
