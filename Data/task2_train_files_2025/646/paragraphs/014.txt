The respondent argues:
1) The visa officer provided the applicant with a full and fair review and assessment of his application pursuant to the
Immigration Act
. There is no evidence to suggest that any irrelevant factors were considered or that any relevant evidence was disregarded. The counsellor fully examined the skills and abilities of the applicant.
2) The visa officer properly assessed the applicant's language abilities by use of verbal questions and comprehension of written passages. He determined that the applicant's knowledge of the English language was poor and his knowledge of the French language was nonexistent.
3) The visa officer provided a thorough and fair assessment of the applicant's application. There is no error of law apparent on the face of the record, nor was there a breach of the duty of fairness which might warrant the intervention of the court.
4) The decision of the visa officer is a discretionary decision which requires the assessment of a number of diverse factors. There is no evidence that he failed to consider all relevant factors in assessing the applicant's claim. This court should not substitute its opinion for that of the visa officer. This application for judicial review of the decision of the visa officer should be dismissed.
