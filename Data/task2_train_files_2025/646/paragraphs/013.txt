The applicant argues that the visa officer had evidence before him, as mentioned in his interview notes, that in addition to tool and dye making, the applicant was also making moulding and was operating his own business. Accordingly, the visa officer should have assessed the applicant under the occupational factor "moulding and metal work", where a greater number of points would have been available. Thus, the visa officer failed to assess the applicant under an alternative occupation for which he was qualified and had experience. The visa officer further failed to consider whether the applicant could be a self-employed person in Canada, and whether bonus points should have been awarded to that end, as the applicant alleges.
