The applicant argues that he should have been considered under subheading (d) of Factor 3, as opposed to subheading (c) upon which the visa officer based his calculation. Notwithstanding that the time period spent by the applicant in his Specific Vocational Preparation (SVP) was exactly three years, five months, his work and school overlapped for a period of time, such that on-the-job experience and training should have been counted by the visa officer to establish four years or more of SVP. That being the case, the applicant should have been awarded 8 points, instead of 6, as the applicant alleges.
