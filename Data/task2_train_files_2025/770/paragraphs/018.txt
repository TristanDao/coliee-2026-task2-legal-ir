The plaintiff argues another point on the question of res judicata. He urges that because these proceedings are a matter of public law estoppel should not be imposed, because it is in the public interest that questions of vires should always be open for consideration by the court until finally resolved. This proposition is almost entirely true, except for one factor, at least. In matters of public law and the validity of regulations and statutes there is no shortage of contenders among the populace, because it is easy enough to acquire standing. Until consideration and resolution of questions arising from such matters be finally, precisely stated, the State may be often vexed for the cause asserted by this plaintiff.
