Indeed, as stated by the Supreme Court of Canada in
Reza v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1994] 2 S.C.R. 394; 167 N.R. 282; 72 O.A.C. 348; 21 C.R.R.(2d) 236; 24 Imm. L.R.(2d) 117, at p. 405 S.C.R.:
"... Parliament had created a comprehensive scheme of review of immigration matters ... ."
The observation applies not only as between the Federal Court and provincial superior courts, but as between those who, like the plaintiff, have their panoply of recourses in, say, the IRB's Appeal Division, and this court. It is an abuse of process to attempt to litigate in the Trial Division of this court that which could have been validly raised in that tribunal. It is a twice-vexing of the defendant for one and the same cause and amounts to res judicata. On this ground alone summary judgment will be pronounced dismissing the plaintiff's claim, with costs.
