The judgment of the Federal Court of Appeal in
Poirier v. Canada (Minister of Veterans Affairs)
, [1989] 3 F.C. 233; 96 N.R. 34 (F.C.A.), of which footnote 5 on p. 243 is the most pertinent passage here, was decided much prior to the Supreme Court's judgment in
Tétreault-Gadoury
, above cited. In
Poirier
, Mr. Justice Marceau acknowledged "the possibility of a legislative authority attributing to a tribunal which it creates the incidental responsibility of overseeing the constitutionality of statutes it is entrusted to administer."
