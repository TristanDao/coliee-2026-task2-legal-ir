The principle which the defendant invokes, in this motion for summary judgment, is of venerable vintage and although judge-made, it is so old as to have been born in Latin: "nemo debet bis vexari ... pro una et eadem causa". No one ought to be twice vexed ... for one and the same cause. An applicant or plaintiff asserting a claim must claim all of his or her relief in regard to such cause or claim and any second attempt to elicit the court's aid in or for the same cause or claim must be prevented. So it is virtually asserted in Sopinka & Lederman,
The Law of Evidence in Civil Cases
(1974), at p. 369. This is what the Alberta Court of Appeal meant (above) in denouncing "litigation by instalment".
