The facts are found to be as fully set out in the statement of defence which is confirmed by the reasons expressed by Mr. Justice Heald in dismissing the plaintiff's motion for judicial review, which reasons were dated June 27, 1995 in proceedings number IMM-4347-94 [See 98 F.T.R. 58]. The plaintiff, as an applicant before the Appeal Division (App. Div'n.) of the Immigration and Refugee Board (IRB), confirmed that he had no quarrel with the validity of the deportation order which had been made against him. His appeal was founded on s. 70(3)(b) of the
Act
. It was dismissed. The App. Div'n. found explicitly that the deportation order was valid in law. The question of the vires of regulations 6(1)(a), 9(1)(a) and 10(1)(a) was, of course, not raised in his application for leave, which was, however, granted.
