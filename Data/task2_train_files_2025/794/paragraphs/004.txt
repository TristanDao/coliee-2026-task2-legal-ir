Counsel for the judgment debtors, the Westbank Indian Band, referred to the affidavit of Allan Birchard, noted above, and to the affidavit of Norm Schwartz, administrator for the Westbank Indian Band to which was appended a copy of an "Alternative Funding Arrangements Transfer Payment Agreement" concluded between the Band and the federal government on March 1, 1989. Under that agreement the Band is granted funds necessary to provide certain services to members of the Band and services and facilities on reserve lands. The Schwartz affidavit also appended a cash flow statement, apparently prepared under the agreement with the federal government, indicating that the Band required the sum of $324,350 to provide certain services for the month of May 1990. Finally, appended to the Schwartz affidavit was a copy of the bank statement of the Bank of Montreal, similar to that filed with the affidavit of Birchard, which showed the deposit to the Band's account of the May A.F.A. funds received under the agreement with the federal government in the amount of $324,350 on April 25 at a time when the account was overdrawn in an amount exceeding $5,000. That statement also included the payment out by the bank as garnishee on the following day, April 26, of $35,448 paid into court. Counsel for the judgment debtor also referred to the affidavit of Stuart Hughes, Chartered Accountant, and a member of the firm of accountants for the Westbank Indian Band. That affidavit confirmed that the Band operated a general current account at the Westbank branch of the Bank of Montreal, that a statement of that account, similar to that appended to the affidavits of Schwartz and Birchard, in his view made it clear that prior to the deposit in the account of funds from the federal government the account was in an overdraft position so that the source of funds for the debit noting payment by the garnishee into court was the amount transferred into the account on payment from the federal  government.
