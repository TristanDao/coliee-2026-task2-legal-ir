The circumstances here are some-what unfortunate. The judgment creditors were once employed by the band and after termination of their employment, by settlement in an arbitration proceeding, arrangements were settled upon for payment of certain funds to the judgment creditors. Some of those funds were paid but not all. In some frustration the judgment creditors have registered the arbitration award with this court and have sought enforcement of the award settled upon. This is the second occasion upon which the court has granted a garnishing order only to subsequently dissolve that order when it was clear that the funds made available to satisfy the order were funds provided by the federal government under an agreement with the band. The judgment creditors have had difficulty determining other sources of funds which support the band and indicate some frustration in their efforts in seeking cooperation in obtaining information about such sources.
