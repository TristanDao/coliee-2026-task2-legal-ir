It is clear that the overseas claimants can expect a duty of fairness on the part of the officials. In the case at bar, the applicant argues that the failure to have access to an interpreter violated the principle of natural justice. She also submits that the visa officer should have "recommended the appointment of a legal counsel". She complains that she was not provided an opportunity to confront the interrogations of the visa officer. Furthermore, she argues that the interview was too short and that the visa officer did not give sufficient reasons.
