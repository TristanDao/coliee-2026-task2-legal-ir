In order to obtain a show cause order the applicant must demonstrate a prima facie case of wilful and contumacious contempt of the order in question, that being the standard set by Mr. Justice Muldoon in
Imperial Chemical Industries plc et al. v. Apotex Inc.
(1989), 26 F.T.R. 47 (T.D.), at 53. More recently Mr. Justice Pinard, in
Chic Optic Inc. et al. v. Hakim Optical Laboratory Ltd. et al.
, [2001] F.T.R. Uned. 384; 13 C.P.R.(4th) 283 (T.D.), at 286, relying upon
Imperial Chemical and Frank v. Bottle et al.
(1994), 68 F.T.R. 242 (T.D.), in which Associate Chief Justice Jerome issued a show cause order on the basis of wilful and contumacious conduct, set the standard as being a wilful refusal to comply with a court order. In effect the test is that of prima facie wilful disobedience.
