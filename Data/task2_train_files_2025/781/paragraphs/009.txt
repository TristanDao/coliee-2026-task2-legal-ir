Second, counsel for the Union touched upon but did not press the issue of whether a prothonotary may issue a show cause order. Counsel may have in mind
Nintendo of America Inc. v. 798824 Ontario Inc. et al.
(1995), 94 F.T.R. 138 (T.D.), which was decided under the old
Rules
which, depending upon whether one read the operative portion of the Rule conjunctively or disjunctively would, in the first instance, prevent a prothonotary from issuing a show cause order. The
1998 Rules
no longer make any reference, in Rule 467, to the involvement of a judge in the issuance of a show cause hearing. Moreover, Rule 50(1)(a) makes it doubly clear that, the right to issue a show cause order not being reserved to a judge in Rule 467, a prothonotary may issue a show cause order.
