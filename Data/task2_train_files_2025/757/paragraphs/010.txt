I confess to having difficulty reconciling the concept of indirect persecution as set forth in
Bhatti
with the provisions of the
Immigration Act
, R.S.C. 1985, c. I-2, and the principles that have developed in relation to the issue of Convention refugee status. In my respectful view, the
Bhatti
approach to indirect persecution unjustifiably broadens the Convention refugee basis for admission to Canada, to include persons who do not have a well-founded fear of persecution in their own right.
