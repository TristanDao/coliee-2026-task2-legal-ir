In
Montana Band of Indians v. Canada
, [1991] 2 F.C. 30; 120 N.R. 200 (leave to appeal to the Supreme Court of Canada denied), the Federal Court of Appeal held that rule 1723 allows the court to grant a declaration simpliciter, but it nonetheless repeated the words of Lord Dunedin in
Russian Commercial and Industrial Bank v. British Bank for Foreign Trade
, [1921] A.C. 438 (H.L.), at p. 448, with respect to this type of judgment:
"The question must be a real and not a theoretical question; the person raising it must have a real interest to raise it; he must be able to secure a proper contradictor, that is to say, someone presently existing who has a true interest to oppose the declaration sought." (p. 39)
