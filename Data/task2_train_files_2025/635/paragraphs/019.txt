In
Canadian Union of Postal Workers v. Canada (Attorney General)
(1978), 93 D.L.R.(3d) 148, Walsh, J., of this court held that in spite of rule 1723,
"[d]eclaratory judgments are a matter of discretion, however, and in the present case I cannot find that the declaration sought by the plaintiff would serve any useful purpose, save for constituting some authority for use by plaintiff or others in other proceedings attacking similar legislation. This alone is sufficient to justify granting defendant's motion." (p. 151)
