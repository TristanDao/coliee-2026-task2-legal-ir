Having found the Applicant not to be credible, the Board was not obligated to go through the fruitless exercise of considering the objective basis of fear. If there were errors (and I do not believe there were any) with respect to the Board's findings that the claim was not objectively well-founded, these errors are immaterial to the decision and, therefore, insufficient to be the basis for obtaining leave (Yassine, supra at paras. 8-11; Patel v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) (2002), 288 N.R. 48 (F.C.A.) at para. 5).
