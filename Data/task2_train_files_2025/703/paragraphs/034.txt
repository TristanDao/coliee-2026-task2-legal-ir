The Respondent holds that the duty of fairness is eminently variable and that its content is to be decided in the specific context of each case (
Knight v. Board of Education of Indian Head School Division No. 19
, [1990] 1 S.C.R. 653; 106 N.R. 17; 83 Sask.R. 81, at paragraph 46). Furthermore, having considered the five factors set out in
Baker v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [1999] 2 S.C.R. 817; 243 N.R. 22, at paragraphs 23 to 27, the Respondent proposes that there is a minimal procedural fairness requirement in this context and that any such duty does not require advance notice to the Applicant.
