The Respondent argues that when it comes to the nature of the decision, the decision at bar is more akin to a purely ministerial decision and as such, should be afforded minimal protection. It is also a policy driven decision involving considerable discretion and consideration of multiple factors and the duty of fairness should be relaxed accordingly. Finally, it proposes that to burden DIAND with an onerous duty of procedural fairness in this context would limit the Minister's capacity to ensure that funds are spent for their intended purpose.
