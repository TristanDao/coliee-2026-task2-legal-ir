As set out by the Court of Appeal in
Ozdemir v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
(2001), 282 N.R. 394; 2001 FCA 331, decision-makers are not bound to explain why they did not accept every item of evidence before them. Nor will a reviewing court infer from the failure of reasons to specifically address a particular item of evidence that the decision-maker must have overlooked it. In
Ozdemir
, above, the Court of Appeal noted that it is inappropriate to require administrative officers to give as detailed reasons for their decision as may be expected of an administrative tribunal that renders a decision after an adjudicative hearing.
