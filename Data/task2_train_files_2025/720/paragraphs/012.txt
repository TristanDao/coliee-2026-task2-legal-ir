Irreparable harm must be more than a series of possibilities. The jurisprudence is clear that the court must be satisfied that it will occur if the relief sought is not granted. (See,
Syntex Inc. v. Novopharm Ltd.
(1991), 126 N.R. 114; 36 C.P.R.(3d) 129 (F.C.A.) per Heald, J.A., at p. 135.) I am not satisfied at this stage that such case is established. Thus, I find that there is not irreparable harm on the basis of the evidence and argument before me.
