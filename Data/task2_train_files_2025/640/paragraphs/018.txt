Considering the
Williams
test, discussed above, and the effect of the High Court of Delhi's
Dolkar Decision
, Justice O'Reilly held in
Wanchuk
that the documentary evidence before the RPD showed that acquiring Indian citizenship was not within the applicant's control, but rather would require that the Central Tibetan Administration exercise its discretion not to withhold its approval and that Indian authorities recognize the
Dolkar Decision
as binding authority. In fact, it was only "a mere possibility that [the applicant] could obtain Indian citizenship". As Mr. Wanchuk might have to litigate the issue, just as the applicant in the
Dolkar Decision
did, Justice O'Reilly found the Board's conclusion to be unreasonable.
