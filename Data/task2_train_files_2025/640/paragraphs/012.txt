The applicant further submits that the Board erred by drawing a distinction between possession of citizenship on the one hand, and acquisition of an Indian passport and other benefits of citizenship on the other. Citizenship cannot exist without recognition thereof and resulting national protection by the authorities. As the purpose of the "control" test is to retain the surrogate nature of refugee protection, in that a claimant cannot seek international protection if she has within her control the ability to acquire national protection by citizenship in another country (
Williams
at para 22), a claimant who is refused national protection cannot be denied refugee protection on the basis of a putative "citizenship".
