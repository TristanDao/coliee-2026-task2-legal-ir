In
Bouianova v Minister of Employment and Immigration
(1993), 67 FTR 74 (TD), Justice Rothstein, writing for the Trial Division of the Federal Court of Canada, found that if an applicant is entitled to citizenship in a particular country and can acquire that citizenship by completing mere formalities, with no room for the state in question to refuse status, then that country will be considered a country of reference for the purposes of assessing his claim.
