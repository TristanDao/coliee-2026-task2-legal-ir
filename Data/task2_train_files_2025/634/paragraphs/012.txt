It is agreed by the parties that applications for judicial review are intended to be expeditious proceedings. Courts will grant interlocutory motions to strike out applications only in exceptional cases, where the application is bereft of any possibility of success (
Bull (David) Laboratories (Canada) Inc. v. Pharmacia Inc. et al.
, [1995] 1 F.C. 588; 176 N.R. 48 (F.C.A.)). It is obvious that a matter that is beyond the jurisdiction of the Court is "bereft of any possibility of success" and should be struck. The application for judicial review filed by Mr. Pieters is, in my view, a case which falls within the exception.
