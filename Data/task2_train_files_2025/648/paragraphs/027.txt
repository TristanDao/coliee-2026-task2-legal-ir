I agree that, generally, the internal grievance procedure ought to be exhausted before an inmate seeks judicial review. Strong policy reasons favor this approach. That said, I also agree that where there are urgent, substantial matters and an evident inadequacy in the grievance procedure, the Court may exercise its discretion to hear an application. See, for example,
Gates et al. v. Canada (Attorney General)
, [2007] F.C.J. No. 1359; 316 F.T.R. 82 (F.C.) at paragraph 18.
