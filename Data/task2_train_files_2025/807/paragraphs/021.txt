Counsel for the Crown argues that the Ermineskin defendants have not cited any conclusive authority to establish - as a matter of settled law - that a fiduciary obligation could not be owed in the circumstances. In the absence of such authority, Crown counsel argues that this aspect of the Ermineskin defendants' motion to strike can be dispensed with on the basis of the comments of Dickson, J., for the Supreme Court of Canada in
Guerin v. Canada
, [1984] 2 S.C.R. 335; 55 N.R. 161, at p. 384 [S.C.R.]:
"The categories of fiduciary, like those of negligence, should not be considered closed: see e.g.,
Laskin v. Bache & Co. Inc.
(1971), 23 D.L.R.(3d) 385 (Ont. C.A.), at p. 392;
Glodex Mines Ltd. v. Revill
(1974), 7 O.R. 216 (Ont. C.A.), at p. 224."
