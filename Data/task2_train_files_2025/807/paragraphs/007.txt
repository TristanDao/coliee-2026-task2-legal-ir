The Ermineskin defendants do not dis­pute that this court has jurisdiction over decisions of the Band Council pursuant to ss. 18 and 18.1 of the
Federal Court Act
, the Band Council being a "federal board, com­mission or other tribunal". However, the Ermineskin defendants argue that the court does not have jurisdiction to hear a claim by an individual (including a band member) against the Band Council based on a breach of fiduciary duty or trust obligation (see
Powderface et al. v. Baptiste et al.
, [1996] 3 F.C. D-52; 118 F.T.R. 258 (T.D.), at paragraph 22), or for damages for a breach of contract or tort (see
Beauvais v. Canada and Mohawk Council of Kanawake
, [1982] 1 F.C. 171 (T.D.) and
Trotchie v. Canada
, [1981] 2 C.N.L.R. 147 (F.C.T.D.)). Other decisions that were referred to as demonstrating the court's limited jurisdiction are:
Lower Similkameen Indian Band v. Al­lison et al.
, [1997] 1 F.C. 475; 122 F.T.R. 1 (T.D.), at paragraph 19;
Varnam v. Canada (Minister of Health and Welfare), Director of Bureau of Danger­ous Drugs and College of Physicians and Surgeons (B.C.)
, [1988] 2 F.C. 454; 84 N.R. 163 (F.C.A.);
Lubicon Lake Indian Band v. Canada
, [1981] 2 F.C. 317 (T.D.); and
Alda Enterprises Ltd. v. R.
, [1978] 2 F.C. 106 (T.D.).
