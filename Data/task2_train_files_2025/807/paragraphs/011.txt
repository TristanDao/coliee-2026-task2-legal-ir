The tests for Federal Court jurisdiction were set out in
Miida Electronics Inc. v. Mitsui O.S.K. Lines Ltd. and ITO-In­ternational Ter­minal Operators Ltd.
, [1986] 1 S.C.R. 752; 68 N.R. 241, at p. 766 [S.C.R.]. There must be: (1) a statutory grant of jurisdiction by Parliament; (2) an existing body of federal law essential to the dis­position of the case; (3) the law must be a law of Canada. The
Indian Act
, R.S.C. 1985, c. I-5 satisfies the second and third requirements in this case.
