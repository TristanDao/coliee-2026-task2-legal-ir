On the other hand, the affidavit evidence may not be as strong as it appears at first glance. The statement contained in the Harvey affidavit that the Minister stated "... that under the law he was obligated to act on the recommendation of the Superintendent", is capable of at least two meanings. I think it is fair to say that the Minister was indeed obligated to act on the recommendation of the Superintendent on all of the facts of this matter. He was obligated to act either by accepting the recommendation, giving a direction that varied from the recommendation, or rejecting the recommendation. He could not simply ignore it. To say he was obligated to act is not the same as to say that he was obligated to
accept
the recommendation. In the event, he only accepted the recommendation in part.
