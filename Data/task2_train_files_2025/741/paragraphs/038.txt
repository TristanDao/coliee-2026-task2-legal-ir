Wade and Forsyth continue at p. 356 in the following terms:
"Departments of the central government have the benefit of a special rule whereby officials may act in their minister's names without any formal delegation of authority. When powers are conferred upon ministers who have charge of large departments, it is obvious that they will often not be exercised by the minister in person. Parliament is well aware of this, and ministerial powers are therefore taken to be exercisable by officials of the minister's department acting in his name in the customary way."
