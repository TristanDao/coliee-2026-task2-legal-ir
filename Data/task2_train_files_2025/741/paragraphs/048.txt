Counsel for Sovereign urged that the Minister had no authority to delegate his responsibility under s. 680(2) to the Minister of State and that if he did have such authority, he was obliged to delegate the whole of his responsibility and not merely part thereof. In particular, counsel urged that it was improper for the Minister to delegate the responsibility to preside at a meeting intended to provide Sovereign a reasonable opportunity to be heard, while retaining to himself the decision-making responsibility. For ease of reference, I quote again s. 704 of the
Act
:
"704. The Minister may delegate any of the Minister's powers, duties and functions under this Act to any Minister of State appointed pursuant to the Ministries and Ministers of State Act to assist the Minister."
It was not in dispute before me that the Minister of State who met with Sovereign's delegation on 30 November 1992 and heard their oral presentation, and subsequently reported to the Minister with recommendations, was a Minister to whom a delegation could be made under s. 704. But did the Minister, by entrusting to the Minister of State the function of hearing Sovereign's oral representations, make a delegation within the meaning of s. 704 of the
Act
?
