In considering what factors are relevant in a case such as the present it is important to distinguish these facts from those typically presented on judicial review, namely where a parent is to be deported, a child of that parent has a right to stay in Canada, and it is necessary to have regard to the consequences of the child for this radical disruption of the status quo. In the present case we have an applicant who has no right to come to or stay in Canada, who has never been in this country, and who has lived all of her life in China and all without any prolonged presence of her parents there and, as far as the evidence indicates, without any serious problems.
