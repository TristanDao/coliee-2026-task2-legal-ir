I am satisfied that the proper standard of review here is that of reasonableness simpliciter. This appears to be well-established by the jurisprudence since the decision in Baker v. Canada (M.C.I.), [1999] 2 S.C.R. 817, even though some doubts may remain as to how one measures reasonableness. It seems apparent that in reviewing such a decision for reasonableness one cannot reweigh the factors considered by the decision-maker, as long as the relevant factors were considered by that person; see Suresh v. Canada (M.C.I.) (2002), 208 D.L.R.(4th) 1 (S.C.C.); Legault v. Canada (MCI), [2002] 4 F.C. 358 (C.A.). This means that if the Visa Officer here had regard to the correct factors it is not for the Court to say she gave inadequate weight to the interests of the child and therefore quash her decision.
