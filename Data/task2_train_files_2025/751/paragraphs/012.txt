With respect to the first ground, the Prothonotary found that the counterclaim was not an attack on the lawfulness of a decision of the Minister because AstraZeneca is not seeking to overturn or invalidate the decision of the Minister to issue an NOC to Apotex. The Prothonotary found that the counterclaim was expressly based on the alleged negligence and thus the Court of Appeal's ruling in
Grenier v. Canada,
2005 FCA 348, 262 D.L.R. (4th) 337, that a Minister's unlawful conduct can only be impugned by way of judicial review, is not applicable.
