The Crown submitted that the counterclaim should be struck on any one, or all, of the following four grounds:
1. that any action or decision of the Minister may be found unlawful only by way of judicial review;
2. that there is no right of action against the Crown as the Minister is specifically exempted from liability for damages under section 8(6) of the
NOC Regulations
;
3. that the Minister did not have a duty of care to AstraZeneca or Apotex; and
4. that even if there were a duty of care by which the Minister might be liable, no breach is made out because the Court found in
Apotex Inc. v. the Minister of Health
, supra, that Apotex was required to address the '762 patent.
