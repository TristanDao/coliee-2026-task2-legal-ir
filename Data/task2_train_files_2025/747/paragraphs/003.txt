Furthermore, the parties are not apart on the applicable test under Rule 221(1)(a), the ground of non-existence of a reasonable cause of action. That test is whether it is plain and obvious the case is beyond doubt, without any chance of success. In respect of the other grounds provided for in Rule 221 (immaterial and redundant, scandalous, frivolous or vexatious or otherwise an abuse of the process of the court), the test is whether the ground is so clearly immaterial, frivolous, embarrassing or abusive that it is obviously forlorn and futile.
