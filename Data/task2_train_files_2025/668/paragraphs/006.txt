As explained by Mr. Justice Dureault, the Board has considerable powers. Pursuant to ss. 80, 81, 82, and 88, a patentee is required to disclose information to the Board about its patented medicines: the identity of the medicines, prices, costs or marketing and making the medicine, and so forth [see
Patent Act Amendment Act
, S.C.. 1992, c. 2, s. 7]. However, as pointed out by counsel for the respondent, the Board does not have investigative powers to determine, for example, if the patentee is actually using a particular patent to produce a medicine.
