Counsel for the Respondent has referred the Court to the case of Sumaida v. Canada (MCI) 2004 F.C. 1533 where an H & C application based on "best interests of the child" was rejected on the basis that the applicant was a member of the Iraqi secret police the "Mukhabarat" an organization said to be dedicated to torture and like activities. I have reviewed the memoranda filed in that case and the comments of Campbell J. at paragraph 2 of the decision. In that case there was evidence that the Applicant apparently personally participated in brutal activities such as activity as a "mole" turning people over to be tortured and facilitated an arms sale. Further the Applicant could be returned to either Iraq or Tunisia. It can be seen that, on balance, in that case an application could fail.
